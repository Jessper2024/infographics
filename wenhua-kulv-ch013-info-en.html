Wenhua Kulv · Ch.13 "The Silhouette of the Mountain Resort"

An armchair that cradled a weary dynasty — the Qing dynasty's fate encoded within the Mountain Resort

Core Insight: The Chengde Mountain Resort is the best key to understanding the Qing dynasty — from Kangxi's vigorous opening, replacing the Great Wall with "virtuous governance to pacify the people," to Cixi's desolate order closing all construction, to Wang Guowei drowning himself in the Summer Palace with his queue intact, a dynasty's rise and fall is encoded in this armchair's armrests.

QuestionWhy did Qing emperors place their "armchair of repose" beyond the Great Wall? What cultural code hides behind this resort?

AnswerKangxi used the resort to realize a two-pronged political wisdom — the military deterrence of the Mulan Hunting Grounds and the cultural integration of the Eight Outer Temples. But Qianlong's megalomania and Xianfeng's flight turned this resort from an emblem of vigor into one of melancholy.

1
Emotional Barrier

Why do Chinese harbor complex feelings toward the Qing?

Qing history is the only elementary school curriculum soaked in tears — beginning with weeping for the "Yangzhou Massacre" and "Jiading Three Atrocities," ending with tears for the "Burning of the Old Summer Palace" and the "Hundred Days' Reform." Hatred planted in childhood is hard to dissolve.

Yet Yu Qiuyu grew wary of such sentiment: many emotional social judgments, passed down with apparent legitimacy, harbor profound injustice — surname orthodoxy, then ethnic orthodoxy. The Han are undoubtedly great, but justice, light, and hope cannot be monopolized by one ethnicity. Hence his decision to reexamine the Qing through the Mountain Resort.

2
Kangxi's Vigor

How did Kangxi replace the Great Wall with a resort?

Kangxi refused to repair the Great Wall: "The way to guard the realm lies only in cultivating virtue and pacifying the people. When the people's hearts are content, the nation's foundation is secure — this is what 'many wills form a city' means." He sought to build an invisible wall with both hard and soft power.

Hard power: the Mulan Hunting Grounds beyond the Great Wall, where 10,000+ men conducted massive annual hunts — military exercises to maintain ferocity and deter the northern frontier. Soft power: the Eight Outer Temples around the resort, providing religious spaces for minority leaders and venues for cultural exchange. Transforming complex political objectives into tranquil gardens — this was Kangxi's genius.

🏹 Hard: Mulan Hunts

Kangxi attended 48 hunts. Once shot 318 hares in a single day — "Physical vigor and spiritual vigor are inseparable."

📚 Soft: Cultural Fusion

Studied Zhu Xi's philosophy deeply. Edited major reference works. Also studied Euclidean geometry, astronomy, physics, medicine — far surpassing Ming emperors.

3
Cultural Reconciliation

How did Han intellectuals come to accept Manchu rule?

From Liu Zongzhou starving himself to death in protest, to Huang Zongxi sending his son into the imperial history bureau — this was neither betrayal nor compromise, but cultural-ecological recognition: if Kangxi identified so sincerely with Han culture, why couldn't Han literati reciprocate?

Kangxi held the "Erudite Scholars Examination" to personally recruit talent. When Fu Shan was forcibly carried to Beijing and wept upon seeing the "Great Qing Gate," Kangxi not only forgave him but appointed him. When Huang Zongxi repeatedly refused summons, Kangxi had officials copy his books for the palace library — Huang eventually sent his son into the Ming History project.

The ultimate irony: Han intellectuals sought to "oppose the Qing and restore the Ming," while the Qing emperor himself led Han historians in soberly studying the Ming dynasty — the resulting Ming History became one of the better entries among the Twenty-Four Histories.

4
Dynastic Decline

How did the resort witness accelerating collapse?

From Qianlong haughtily lecturing British envoys on "Celestial Empire protocol," to Xianfeng fleeing here with silver to ratify humiliating treaties — the Mountain Resort fell from spiritual pinnacle to refugee shelter.

Jiaqing read his father's poems and sighed at the burden. Daoguang avoided the resort entirely. Xianfeng fled here and used it as refuge while ratifying treaties...

5
Melancholy Finale

What final punctuation did Wang Guowei's suicide provide?

In 1927, the great scholar Wang Guowei drowned himself in the Summer Palace lake, his Qing queue still intact. His last words: "At fifty, I owe only death; through this world-changing calamity, honor brooks no further disgrace." He died for culture, not politics — what perished around him was a classical age in the cultural sense.

Chen Yinke explained: when a culture is in decline, those formed by that culture suffer deeply; the greater their embodiment of it, the deeper their pain. Wang Guowei's bookshelves — the great Qing reference works, dictionaries, literary classics — every page bound to the dynasty. He couldn't reach Chengde, couldn't push open the resort's bolted gates.

"A dynasty of centuries of storm always begins with the heroic bearing of the strong, yet its final punctuation is often delivered by the refined, melancholy souls of scholars."
💡
Key Takeaway

The Mountain Resort is an armchair that cradled a weary dynasty. From Kangxi's spiritual vigor that sought to replace the Great Wall with virtuous governance, to Cixi's order halting all construction at Rehe — cultural resilience outlasts political turnover, and intellectuals always embrace culture's final dignity with their very lives.

Source: Yu Qiuyu, Wenhua Kulv (A Bitter Journey Through Culture) · Changjiang Literature & Art Press, 2014
