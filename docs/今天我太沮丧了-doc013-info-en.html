01Langlangshan Film — Solid But Safe, Lost Its Edge

7.6/10: strong execution with a novel story, but the script eventually retreated into conventional tropes.

The original short A Little Demon's Summer went viral because it told the story from the perspective of unnamed, unvoiced "side characters" in Journey to the West. The protagonist, a pig demon, had his own life, dreams, and attachments — relatable to ordinary workers. The film version gave the protagonists a "cheat code" power-up during the climax for a rousing finish — not bad, but lost the original's uniqueness.

03Reader Scammed ¥40K — Fake "Discount Stock" Tender Offer

The author's friend / long-time reader was funneled from a blog-recommended chat app "Yinlihui" to a fake stock app "yxzj," losing ¥40K to a non-existent discounted tender offer.

The author listed obvious red flags: ① No tender offer exists below market price; ② Tender offers don't distribute shares to retail; ③ No legitimate broker uses all-English-letter app names; ④ Brokerage deposits never go to trade accounts; ⑤ Legitimate chat apps are never downloaded outside App Store. The recommending blog was permanently banned by Tencent — registration was a general store sole proprietor, a typical scam front company.

"I've always thought my readers were cognitively sophisticated urban elites. Today really slapped me in the face."

04Fake Celebrity Stock Tips — Another Scam

The author also mentions someone impersonating him online, leading stock trading with commission-based fees, resulting in losses of over ¥100K.

Author's response: the blog averages 400-500K daily reads — he would never individually contact readers to add friends. He's been managing large-scale assets for 7-8 years and would never lead retail investors with tens of thousands. Three red lines: never actively add readers, never lead stock trading, never take commission. Anyone asking to add friends, lead trades, or share profits is an impersonator.
