01Black Tuesday – Trillions Erased in One Day

KOSPI plunged 10%, the biggest single-day drop since March, going from "world's best" to "world's worst" in hours. The index had just broken through 9,000 for the first time, hitting a string of all-time highs after investors shrugged off Iran-war concerns. Then came the brutal reversal.

Blue-chip chipmakers collapsed: SK Hynix -12.47%, Samsung Electronics -12.31%. Japan's Nikkei 225 followed with a -3.55% drop. Kioxia cratered 15.1%, SoftBank fell 10.09%.

02Triple Trigger – Overheated, Correction, Risk-Off

Three factors combined to detonate the crash: overheating signals, US tech correction transmission, and Micron earnings hedging.

Hana Securities flagged that SK Hynix's valuation had surpassed Samsung's — historically abnormal, given Samsung's Q2 profit is expected to exceed Hynix's. SpaceX's sharp drop in US trading shook global optimism. Market focus turned to Micron's earnings this week — "that's the real test."

03Leveraged ETFs – Regulator Says "I Regret It"

Korea's Financial Supervisory Service chief publicly said he regrets approving SK Hynix/Samsung single-stock leveraged ETFs. He stated: "These products generated enormous turnover that made brokerages rich. Investors have no real returns — only asset managers profited."

Leveraged ETFs amplified volatility and worsened the decline. High retail leverage + leveraged ETFs formed a positive feedback loop: leverage up on the way in → forced liquidation on the way out → crash amplified.

⚠️ Regulatory alert: FSS Chief Lee Chan-jin said "allowing these high-risk products to list was a mistake" and is drafting investor protection measures. This isn't a market debate — it's a public regulatory admission of error.

04Hong Kong Contagion – Hynix Fund Becomes HK's Largest ETP

The CSOP 2x Long Hynix fund surpassed HK$130 billion, ending the Tracker Fund's 27-year reign as Hong Kong's largest ETP. SK Hynix's rally fueled a frenzy in HK-listed leveraged products. When leveraged products balloon to this size, it means the trade is extremely crowded. If Hynix keeps falling, forced unwinding will cascade from Hong Kong back to Korea, creating a cross-market stampede.

05Trader Survival Rules – Micron Earnings Decide Everything

This week's Micron earnings report will determine whether the AI hardware trade lives or dies.

  • Micron earnings is the real test — strong data = Samsung/Hynix can rally; weak = AI hardware trade is over
  • Foreign investors dumping ₩2 trillion in one day is a clear signal: smart money is leaving, retail is catching knives
  • Leveraged ETFs have been publicly disavowed by regulators — trading them means betting against the regulator
  • HK's Hynix leveraged fund is dangerously oversized — passive unwinding risk is extreme
"Strong earnings data will show up directly in Samsung and SK Hynix's share prices — it will indicate whether this AI trade's hardware leg still has room to run." — Dilin Wu, Pepperstone strategist
