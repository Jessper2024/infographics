The Sikhs – What Role Do They Play in India?

Source: Global Magazine · December 22, 2024 | A unique ethnic group built on three pillars — ethnicity, religion, and politics — clashing with Hinduism for centuries, from kingdom-building to near-annihilation to revival

Sikhism was founded in 1499 by Guru Nanak in Punjab, built on monotheism + radical equality — directly challenging Hinduism's polytheism and caste system. The Sikhs are renowned warriors who built a kingdom spanning 520,000 km² (1799) before the British destroyed it. After Partition, the Sikh struggle for autonomy went through Operation Blue Star, Indira Gandhi's assassination, anti-Sikh massacres, and eventually produced India's first Sikh PM in 2004. Today, Modi's Hindu nationalism is reigniting Sikh grievances.

01Birth of Sikhism – Punjab's Rebel DNA

Sikhism was born in a soil of discrimination and invasion — Punjab's geography made it a perpetual battleground, and the religion inherited a rebel gene.

Punjab sits at the gateway from Central Asia into India (Khyber Pass). For millennia it was invaded by Aryans, Persians, Greeks, Arabs, and Turks. Mixed races, multiple languages, and religious fusion led Hinduism to label Punjab "impure" — assigned to low castes and subjected to severe discrimination.

In 1499, Guru Nanak fused Hindu Bhakti thought with Islamic Sufi elements to create Sikhism. Over 10 Gurus and 200 years, the faith grew rapidly. The 6th Guru turned it into an armed organization of citizen-soldiers. In 1799, Ranjit Singh built the Sikh Kingdom — 520,000 km², 3.5 million people. Britain destroyed it in 1849.

02Root Conflict with Hinduism – Monotheism vs Polytheism, Equality vs Caste

The Sikh-Hindu conflict is rooted in a fundamental clash of faith: one God vs 330 million gods, equality vs caste.

Hinduism claims 330 million gods — from the cosmic trinity to the sun, cows, and monkey gods. Sikhism is strictly monotheistic: "There is one God, and His name is Truth." What Hindus worship as divine, Sikhs may see as ordinary or meaningless.

To destroy caste, Guru Nanak created Sangat (all worship together regardless of status) and Pangat (anyone can eat free at the communal kitchen). The 10th Guru decreed: all men take the surname "Singh" (lion), all women "Kaur" (princess).

03Warrior Tradition – "Singh" Means Lion

From the 6th Guru onward, martial spirit was written into Sikh doctrine. The kirpan (dagger) symbolizes fighting oppression and defending freedom.

The "Five Ks" are Sikh identity markers: uncut hair (natural completeness), comb (discipline), steel bracelet (eternity), kirpan/dagger (fight oppression, destroy evil), short breeches (separation from Hindu dress). The dagger and breeches are literally declarations of war against Hindu norms.

During British India, the British heavily recruited Sikhs for the Punjab Regiment and Sikh Regiment — their ferocity and combat effectiveness far exceeded other troops. A former Indian president said: "The Punjab Regiment's history is the Indian Army's history."

⚠️ The warrior tradition is a double-edged sword: it gave Sikhs prestige in the military, but also laid the groundwork for violent conflict and separatist movements.

04Political Struggle – From Statehood Dream to Operation Blue Star

After 1947 Partition, Sikh demands escalated from language-state recognition to the "Khalistan" independence movement, culminating in bloodshed.

In 1949, Sikhs first demanded a Punjabi-language state — rejected. In 1956, India reorganized states by language but classified Punjabi as a Hindi dialect. Sikhs felt humiliated and vowed to fight. After the 1965 Indo-Pak war, India finally granted Punjab statehood.

In 1980, extremists declared "Khalistan" (Land of the Pure) — printing flags, stamps, passports. Violence spread. Indira Gandhi launched Operation Blue Star — 100,000 troops stormed the Golden Temple, killing at least 500 Sikh militants. Indira was then assassinated by 2 Sikh bodyguards, triggering anti-Sikh massacres — 2,500+ killed in 4 days.

05Today's Powder Keg – Modi's Hindu Nationalism Reignites Grievances

Manmohan Singh's era nearly ended the Sikh separatist movement, but Modi's Hindu expansion policies are reigniting the fire.

In 2024, Canadian Sikhs held mass rallies demanding closure of all Indian consulates. India and Canada expelled each other's diplomats for the second time. Sikh diaspora (especially Canada, UK, US) has become the new separatist force. Canada is now the overseas center of the Khalistan movement.

  • Global Sikh population: ~30 million — 24M in India, 6M overseas (550K+ in Canada)
  • Punjab remains Sikh-majority; the Akali Dal remains a political force
  • Modi's Hindu nationalism fundamentally conflicts with Sikhism's "equality for all"
  • Overseas Sikhs are the new political variable — Canada is the new Khalistan center
"After Modi came to power, his policies expanding Hindu influence have reignited long-standing Sikh grievances rooted in centuries of conflict with Hinduism." — Global Magazine
