Wealth Transfer Is Spiraling Out of Control
The Middle Class Is Being Harvested

The true logic of class mobility: it's never about hard work — it's about standing on the right side of wealth transfer

Most people believe years of grueling study builds human capital. Nobel laureate Michael Spence says otherwise: over 90% of what you learn from elementary school through university is never used again. The true function of elite exam systems is not education — it is sending an extremely expensive "signal" to capital markets. And that signal determines which direction the wealth transfer current will carry you.
01
The Signal Deception
Exams Don't Teach Knowledge — They Send Signals
Spence's market signaling theory: an interviewer cannot tell if you are truly talented or just a good performer. A diploma is the only high-cost, hard-to-fake differentiator. Those geometry proofs you stayed up solving will never serve you again. Their sole function is to broadcast to capital: "I can endure hardship, I follow rules, I execute." Elite exams are not educational selection — they are social stratification, outsourced.
02
Structural Checkpoints
Every Mature Industry Is Fortified by Gatekeepers
A wealthy family buys every commercial property in a city's prime retail districts. From that point on, every transaction is taxed by their rent. A newcomer can never afford to buy in. Every checkpoint is a class barrier. You think wages can break through? The Maginot Line was never meant to be breached head-on.

Wealth Transfer
Wealth Is Never Created — It Is Transferred From Old Landlords to New Platforms
E-commerce did not "create" consumption — it diverted offline transactions online. Landlord rental income halved. That money became platform traffic revenue, which fueled stock market valuations, which became equity wealth for platform employees. Every e-commerce billionaire's fortune was transferred directly from shrinking landlord rents.
