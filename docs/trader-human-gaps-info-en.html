Seven Uncrossable Gaps in Human Nature for Traders

Jack Zhang in conversation with Trader's Voice · 2024

Every trader knows what they should do — set stop-losses, control position size, never hold losing trades. But with real money on the line, seven human nature gaps stand between knowing and doing.

They share a single root cause: uncertainty. You don't know whether tomorrow will bring a rally or a crash. Fear makes you sell too early; greed makes you go all in; hope makes you cancel your stop-loss. Drawing on the I Ching's principle of reversal (fǎn zhě dào zhī dòng) and decades of trading experience, Jack Zhang argues: to make money, stop studying how to win — study how to lose, then build rules to eliminate every source of loss.

01
Core Concept

The Seven Peaks: Seven Faces of Uncertainty

The seven peaks no retail trader can climb — FOMO, premature selling, crash whipsaw, over-leveraging, holding losers, account attrition, and emotional trading — every losing trader suffers from at least one. They all spring from the same source: you don't know what tomorrow brings, so fear and hope take turns hijacking every decision.

03
Cognitive Reframe

Stop-Loss as Liberation: Profit & Loss From the Same Source

Most traders treat losses as the enemy. But Jack argues the opposite: a stop-loss is a good thing — it frees you from that trade so you can find the next opportunity. There is absolutely no holding losers in this system.

"
Once you embrace the truth that profit and loss come from the same source, what is there to mourn or fear? Just follow your rules. When you're wrong, let it be wrong — being wrong on a small loss saves you far more. When you're right, let the trend run. If you refuse this stop-loss and hold, that one blow-up wipes you out and you lose every future opportunity.
— Jack Zhang
✅ The Stopper's Path

Stop out → exit → review → find next trade → by the law of large numbers, the third attempt wins. Loss is finite and bounded.

❌ The Holder's Fate

Hold for three months → blow up account → lose all capital → forfeit every future opportunity. An R:R of 1:10 inverted means one loss devours ten wins.

04

The I Ching teaches that all things move toward their opposite (fǎn zhě dào zhī dòng). To make money, stop studying how to make money — study how to lose it. Map out every way you lose, build a rule to neutralize each one, and profit emerges naturally.

05
Practice

Self-Awareness: Observe Yourself From Outside

Wang Yangming taught "stillness in stillness, stillness in motion." Train in live conditions, not in simulation. Jack's concrete method — self-awareness (jué chá): stop staring at the chart and learn to watch yourself from outside yourself.

"
Review today's trades. At which exact moment, when that big red candle appeared, did your heart tighten and you cancelled the stop-loss — and then flipped short on top of it? Now look at those actions: were they right? If you never review, never examine yourself, you stay trapped forever. Awareness is the prerequisite for living in the present.
— Jack Zhang
✅ The Self-Aware

After market close, review in third person: "When the red candle hit, what was he thinking? Why did he cancel the stop?" Observe every action as if observing a stranger.

❌ The Unaware

Close the platform after a loss. Distract yourself. Tell yourself "I'll be better next time." Never look back at what you actually did in that moment. Forever stuck.

Bruce Lee's insight: scattered thoughts produce scattered energy. Water dripping on stone — energy must focus like a drill bit on a single point to pierce through. Focus your energy on self-growth, not on chasing money.

06
Trading System

Three Core Rules: Position → Runner → Risk-Free Trade

A qualified trading system needs three pillars. Together they form a complete chain: defense → endurance → zero-risk freedom.

⚖️
Fixed Risk Per Trade
  • Fixed risk amount per position
  • The #1 priority — prevents one blow-up from ending you
  • No position sizing = nothing else matters
Defense: ensure survival
🌊
Keep a Trend Runner
  • Trend trades must retain a core position
  • Break the "take profit too fast" pattern
  • Gold from 3,000 to 5,000 — hold the whole ride
Endurance: let profits run
🛡️
Risk-Free Trade (RFT)
  • At 2:1 R:R, close half to recover cost
  • Capital recovered = zero risk on remainder
  • Remaining position runs on pure profit
Freedom: trade without fear
💡
Key Takeaway

Crossing the human nature gaps isn't about talent — it's about rules and awareness. Do the opposite of all seven problems: fix your position size, honor your stops, review every trade, and shift the goal from "making money" to "loving the craft of trading itself." You don't "learn" to trade — you become a trader.
