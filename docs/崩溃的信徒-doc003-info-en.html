01Belief to Breakdown

A Xueqiu user who went all-in on one Chinese tech stock experienced the full arc from peak conviction to total collapse. Last year his unrealized profit hit nearly 3M RMB. He believed in "stars and seas" for the company and never sold. When the tide turned this year, he kept adding — even sold a house. Last month he ran out of money entirely. He held onto his retirement fund but that was it.

In June, the stock plunged further. He snapped — started cursing the market, himself, the company, and the CEO he once worshipped.

⚠️ Classic trap: Conviction during gains → averaging down during losses → selling house to inject cash → total blow-up. This isn't bad luck. It's a complete absence of risk management.

02The China ADR Trap – Squeezed on Both Sides

China stocks listed overseas are structurally trapped: shut out of domestic liquidity and shunned by international capital.

HSTECH is down 21% YTD, Hang Seng Internet down 30%, Kweb down 27%. And it's only June — the annualized loss matches the worst bear markets in history.

The author sold all his China ADRs in 2022 and switched to US stocks. "It felt like switching from Hell to Easy mode." He's given up on Hong Kong: "The worst part? Down 40% one year, up 20% the next — and you pay tax on the 20% rebound. Can't beat it, I concede."

05Survival Rules – What Not to Touch

Mao Bi Dao offers concrete takeaways: stay away from China ADRs, and don't bottom-fish unless you can hold 2-4 years.

  • Xiaomi at 22, Tencent at 400, Meituan below 70? None are safe short-term. Only if you can stomach 2-4 years for the next cycle
  • Opportunity cost + time cost + emotional pain = poor risk-reward ratio
