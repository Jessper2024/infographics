Binance · Time-Based K-Lines

Zoom into the daily candle — solve the "when to enter" problem

A single daily candle represents 24 hours of price action. But if you want to know what actually happened inside that day — when the dump occurred, when the pump began — you need to slice the daily candle into smaller timeframes. The core value of time-based K-lines: revealing the details hidden inside the daily bar so you can enter and exit at more precise levels.
01
What It Is
Time-Based K-Lines = the Daily Candle, Sliced Into Smaller OHLC Bars
Each time-based candle is a miniature daily candle — containing open, high, low, and close — with a lifespan measured in minutes or hours. Binance's common timeframes:

02
Why It Matters
Same Price Move — Two Completely Different Stories
On the daily chart, a big green candle closing +5%. Looks like a smooth ride. Zoom into the 15-minute, and you find two panic dumps and three recoveries inside that same candle. The daily gives you the outcome. Lower timeframes give you the process — and trading happens in the process.

03
How to Use
Daily Decides Direction — Lower TFs Pinpoint the Entry
The daily/weekly answers "long or short." The lower timeframe answers "exactly where to get in." Together, they let you place a tighter stop and achieve a better risk/reward ratio. Lower timeframes don't replace the daily — they execute it with surgical precision.

04
Know the Limits
Lower TFs Are Not a Magic Wand — Know When to Step Back
Lower timeframes carry extreme noise and price wicks. They can lure you into overtrading or fake breakouts. They must be used alongside the daily/weekly — never for standalone decisions.

05
Real Scenarios
Three Common Use Cases That Make It Click
Knowing the theory isn't enough. The real skill is knowing when to zoom in — and to what magnification.
1

Finding the Entry — the Daily Candle Is Too Big

Daily looks bullish but the candle is too long to chase → drop to 15m or 1h, enter on a pullback to support, stop below the structure low.

2

Reading an Unfinished Daily Candle Early

Daily is painting a big green bar → switch to 1h and see it's actually fading from the highs → you know this daily may not hold, skip the FOMO entry.

3

Crypto's 24/7 Edge — No Gap, Clean Structure

Stock futures open with price gaps that break lower-TF continuity. Crypto never closes, so lower-TF structure is seamless and more reliable than equities.
