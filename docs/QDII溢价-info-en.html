QDII Premium
On-Exchange vs OTC

Why you pay more than NAV for QDII funds — 2026 Edition

Premiums only exist on-exchange. OTC subscriptions execute at NAV (zero premium), but QDII funds hit forex quotas fast. When the OTC door shuts, everyone rushes on-exchange for second-hand shares — pushing prices above NAV. As of late May 2026, nearly 50% of US-equity QDII funds have suspended or restricted subscriptions.
01
Core Concept
What Is the Premium? Two Pricing Mechanisms
OTC subscriptions execute at NAV — zero premium. On-exchange ETFs are priced by supply and demand. When OTC channels close, all buying pressure funnels on-exchange, pushing prices above NAV.

04
Custody Transfer
Can OTC Shares Move On-Exchange? Only LOFs
OTC subscribe → custody transfer to broker → sell at premium on-exchange. But not all funds qualify.
✅

LOF Funds: Transfer OK

OTC subscribe → T+2 transfer → shares land in brokerage → sell at premium. Beware premium retracing during the 2-day window.

❌

ETF Feeder / Plain QDII: Cannot Transfer

Most QDII funds bought on Alipay have no on-exchange ticker. You can only redeem at NAV — the premium is never yours.

05
Risk Warning
Premiums Only Move One Way: Down
A premium is not a bullish signal — it's a congestion signal. When quotas reopen or sentiment shifts, premiums can collapse overnight.
