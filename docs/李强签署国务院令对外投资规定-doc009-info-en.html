Li Qiang Signs State Council Order · Regulations on Outbound Investment

Source: China Finance · Xinhua · June 1, 2026 | State Council Order No. 837 · Passed April 17, 2026 · Effective July 1, 2026

Premier Li Qiang signed State Council Order No. 837, promulgating the Regulations on Outbound Investment, comprising 34 articles and effective July 1, 2026. The Regulations cover five key areas: scope of application, general requirements, comprehensive services, management measures, and protection mechanisms for all outbound investment activities by domestic investors.

01Scope — Who and What Is Covered

The Regulations apply to all activities where domestic investors directly or indirectly acquire interests in overseas enterprises or assets. Investments in HK, Macau, and Taiwan are managed by reference.

Investor definition: enterprises, other organizations, and individual residents within China.

Outbound investment definition: activities where investors obtain ownership, control, management rights, or other related interests in overseas enterprises or assets through capital contribution, equity participation, financing, guarantees, or other means.

Extended coverage: investments in overseas financial markets using own funds, raised funds, or entrusted funds; reinvestment of assets and interests obtained from outbound investments.

02General Requirements — Balancing Openness and Security

Outbound investment follows the basic state policy of opening-up while implementing a holistic national security approach.

Strategic direction: actively align with high-standard international economic and trade rules, promote high-quality Belt and Road cooperation, advance multilateral and bilateral investment cooperation mechanisms, participate in international investment rule-making, promote supply chain cooperation, and oppose unilateralism and protectionism.

Investor autonomy: investors enjoy the right to make independent decisions, bear their own risks, and be responsible for their own profits and losses. They must comply with laws, international practices, respect local customs, uphold commercial ethics, and fulfill social responsibilities.

03Comprehensive Services — Full-Chain Support for Overseas Investment

The state establishes a comprehensive overseas service system integrating foreign affairs, legal, fiscal, financial, trade, logistics, immigration, customs, and trade promotion resources.

Public services: provincial and higher-level governments provide public goods including legal frameworks, policy guidance, investment handbooks, IP protection, risk prevention, and rights protection.

Professional services: support for legal, accounting/auditing, credit rating, mediation/arbitration, and IP service providers to expand overseas networks.

Financial services: banks provide financing within market-oriented and risk-controllable principles; policy insurance institutions encouraged to offer overseas investment insurance.

Industry services: trade associations and chambers of commerce provide information consultation, market expansion, trade exchange, rights protection, and dispute resolution services.

04Management — Classified, Tiered, Whole-Process Regulation

Outbound investment is subject to classified and tiered whole-process regulation, including registration/filing, information reporting, cross-border capital registration, and security review.

Policy tools: relevant State Council departments define encouraged, restricted, and prohibited investment categories based on economic development needs, investment environment changes, and risk levels.

Technology export controls: investors may not export prohibited goods, technologies, services, or data — including through cross-border personnel deployment, training, or technical guidance.

Security review: mandatory security review for outbound investments and related asset transfers that affect or may affect national security.

Countermeasures: when any country imposes discriminatory restrictions on China, countermeasures may be taken under the Anti-Foreign Sanctions Law, including sanctions lists, transaction bans, and entry restrictions.

05Penalties and Protection Mechanisms

The Regulations specify economic penalties and activity restrictions for violations, plus consular protection, dispute resolution, and investment barrier investigation systems.

Protection: consular protection (during war, unrest, natural disasters); investment barrier investigations (Ministry of Commerce can investigate and impose reciprocal measures); dispute resolution (encouraged through negotiation, mediation, arbitration, litigation).

Key Points:
① 34 articles, effective July 1, 2026 — covers all outbound investment including HK/Macau/Taiwan;
② Framework: opening-up + national security + market principles, supporting Belt and Road and supply chain cooperation;
③ Services: foreign affairs, legal, financial, insurance support — banks and policy insurers provide financing and investment insurance;
④ Management tools: classified/tiered regulation + filing + security review + tech export control + anti-sanctions measures;
⑤ Penalties: fines from 1‰ to 10‰ of investment amount; serious violations trigger 1–3 year ban on outbound investment.
