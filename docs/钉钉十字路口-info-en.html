DingTalk's Crossroads
Founder vs Employee

A 70,000-word resignation exposes the collision between two worldviews

In early 2026, a P5 product manager at DingTalk was rushed to the ER twice due to overwork, then wrote a 70,000-word resignation letter that ignited a public firestorm. On the surface: a labor dispute. Underneath: the return of founder Wu Zhao with a scorched-earth management style, colliding head-on with a new generation's values. Behind it all: Alibaba's strategic gamble on DingTalk as its AI gateway.
01
The Spark
A 70,000-Word Scream from the ER
A junior PM was sent to the emergency room twice from overwork. From his hospital bed, he documented every detail of DingTalk's high-pressure management, performative busywork, and his own physical collapse. The letter went viral, forcing DingTalk into a public response.
02
Founder's DNA
Wu Zhao: Exiled, Then Recalled
Once a marginal figure at Alibaba after his social app "Laiwang" was crushed by WeChat, Wu Zhao rallied the survivors to build DingTalk 1.0 from nothing. Later forced out, he founded HHO. Now, in the AI era, Alibaba has called him back. He treats his product as his child — not an asset.
03
Shock Therapy
Attendance, Python Exams, 7AM Standups
Upon his return, Wu Zhao imposed strict attendance tracking, required all employees to pass a Python coding test, and mandated daily morning standups. Outsiders called it chaos. In truth, it was deliberate screening — those who couldn't handle it would leave. Those who stayed were the soldiers he wanted.
04
The Collision
All-In Founder vs Work-Life Employees
This is not a simple case of exploitation. Wu Zhao's logic: DingTalk faces an existential battle in the AI era and needs comrades willing to bet everything. The new generation's logic: a job is just a job, and health is non-negotiable. Neither side is wrong — they are simply incompatible.
05
AI Gambit
Why Alibaba Is Betting on Wu Zhao, Again
DingTalk commands over 100 million active users — Alibaba's most critical consumer-facing AI entry point. Alibaba offered Wu Zhao 10-month year-end bonuses and unlimited resources. This is not a vote of confidence in his management. It is a bet that he can repeat the miracle: building victory from the rubble, this time in the AI era.
Wu Zhao is not a good manager. He is a founder.
The real question: can he win the bet one more time, in the age of AI?
