The Vanishing Gaokao Fever

When a degree no longer changes your destiny, the collective illusion dissolves · June 2026

For years, the Gaokao — China's college entrance exam — was an internet carnival every June. Not this year. Weibo's trending page went silent. No mothers in qipao dresses flooding social feeds. It's not that people stopped caring about education. It's that the return on a degree has fallen so low that no one can muster excitement anymore. Parents and students alike are voting with their feet: this is no longer a ritual worth buying into.

02
Degree Devaluation
80% of Non-Elite Grads End Up in Sales — the Diploma Is Now a Participation Trophy
Eight out of ten graduates from non-elite universities end up in sales roles, starting at ¥2,000–3,000 per month. Admission rates have climbed relentlessly — and so has the degree's evaporation of value. A bachelor's degree is no longer a ticket to the middle class. It's a souvenir that says "I went to college." Elite employers now filter by school name before they even read your resume.

03
Arms Race
The Acceptance Letter Hasn't Arrived — but a Four-Year Grind Roadmap Already Has
Consulting agencies start calling the moment admission results drop, mapping out a freshman's path to a coveted grad school slot four years away: which GPA to maintain, which competitions to win, which papers to publish, which professors to network. College hasn't even started, and a complete "hustle blueprint" is already on the table. The Gaokao was never the finish line — it was just the starting pistol for an even more brutal race.

The Network Gap
Some Get a Red Carpet, Others Get a Desert — Two Lives, Two Starting Lines
In Beijing's elite high school circles, "strategic teenage dating" — deliberately pairing with a classmate whose family has resources — is treated as a serious life optimization strategy, not a joke. Meanwhile, small-town grinders spend four years solving problem sets, only to find that someone else's parent made one phone call and landed the job they dreamed of.
