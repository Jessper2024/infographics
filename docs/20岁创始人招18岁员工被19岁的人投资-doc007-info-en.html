20-Year-Old Founders, 18-Year-Old Employees, 19-Year-Old Investors

Source: LatePost · June 22, 2026 | AI is mass-producing million-dollar-salary youth. "AI Native" makes experience obsolete and age a liability. "This era's rewards for the extraordinary have never been greater — and its punishment for the ordinary has never been harsher."

A 17-year-old intern earns ¥5,500/day. A 22-year-old fresh graduate gets ¥6M/year. AI unicorn founders' average age dropped from 40 (2020) to 29 (2024). Big tech is shattering age, rank, and experience barriers — ByteDance Seed created a "Student Work Department," Kuaishou chartered a cruise ship on the Han River, Alibaba hosted dinners on the 38th floor of the Grand Hyatt. "AI Native" is the new rule: youth beats experience. But the flip side is brutal: the punishment for being ordinary has never been harsher — the salary gap between elite and ordinary graduates has reached 100x, and 35-year-old programmers' experience has become "negative equity."

01Salary Explosion – Mass-Producing Millionaire Youth

A 22-year-old AI researcher with zero management experience, zero business decisions, zero performance cycles — gets the same pay as a ByteDance 3-2 or Alibaba P9 who worked 12 years to get there.

One 5,500-yuan/day intern = 10 Beijing delivery drivers. One 3M-yuan/year AI researcher working one year = a 2025 graduate working 39 years. Previously, reaching 3M at a tech giant required a master's degree, 8-12 years, at least 3 promotions each landing in the top 30%. Now a 22-year-old gets it directly.

02AI Native – Youth Is Worth More Than Experience

After studying DeepSeek, ByteDance concluded: in AI, what truly drives progress is key researchers. Past management experience and business achievements don't matter much.

DeepSeek has less than 1/10 of big tech's headcount, half the per-person work hours, zero outside investment — yet entered the global first tier before China's biggest internet companies. 70%+ of its 172 researchers are under 30. ByteDance initially let senior "big brothers" lead new AI business — went through 2-3 of them with mediocre results. Then younger Zhou Chang joined and rapidly advanced multimodal capabilities.

"AI Native" definition: your thinking is fully aligned with LLM input/output; you ask AI first, and you know what the next question should be. Recruiter screening trick: if a candidate asks "how's the company's revenue?" — instantly flagged as not AI Native.

⚠️ The unwritten rule recruiters discovered: 33 is probably the ceiling. Experienced people might adapt, but why bet on them when there are younger ones? "Genius" managers only want to hire their own kind.

03The Talent War – Cruise Ships, High Schools, Student Work Departments

Big tech is scouting high schoolers. ByteDance Seed's "Student Work Department" has a database that virtually exhausts every outstanding student in China.

At CVPR: NVIDIA, ByteDance Seed, Intel host dinners. Next day: Tencent Qingyun, Alibaba Star, MiniMax. At ICML: Alibaba, Kuaishou, Tencent on the same night. Kuaishou chartered a cruise ship on the Han River with custom fireworks. Alibaba at the Grand Hyatt 38th floor (where Buffett spoke). Tencent sent at least 12 department heads.

04Youth Networks – Peers Helping Peers, "Fighting the Old Guard"

In San Francisco's Bay Area, a 20-year-old founder hires an 18-year-old employee, selected by a 19-year-old investor — they'd never met. Just a cold email: "I'm interested in your paper. Let's talk."

Young researcher proves ability → joins big tech or starts company → gains power → trusts peers → younger people get opportunities → young investors find them → invest in winners → rise faster. A positive feedback loop.

Yunqi Capital's Y Transformer fund: exclusively for post-1998 founders, ¥100M budget, 20-25 deals, $600K each, 2-3 week decision cycle. Sequoia's youngest partner Cao Xi: "It's the era of post-90s founders" — half a year later, founders he meets are born 2000-2002. "Sometimes I wish I weren't born in the 80s."

"Finally it's our turn to ride the era's tailwinds." — A post-2003 AI entrepreneur who completed 2 years of grad school in 6 months, raised tens of millions in his first round

05The Brutal Flip Side – Punishment for Being Ordinary

"This era's rewards for the extraordinary have never been greater — and its punishment for the ordinary has never been harsher." People only notice the first half.

Elite university AI graduates: ¥5M/year. Ordinary university graduates: can't even get ¥50K. The gap is 100x — and widening. 35-year-old programmers used to think "keep improving and I'll be fine." Then AI arrived — frontend developers immediately became "redundant," other programmers are just on a countdown. Past experience became "negative equity."

A 30+ programmer: "You can't explain to them that transformation isn't that easy." A post-2000 researcher: "Isn't it obvious? Advanced productive forces will always replace backward ones." — "Four words: too narrow a vision."
