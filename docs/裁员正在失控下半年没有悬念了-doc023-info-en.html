From ancient IOUs to modern banking, the essence of wealth has never changed: it is your claim on society's products and services. When your savings grow, someone (corporation/government) is increasing debt. This is the foundational framework for understanding layoff logic.

Middle-class home buying is the largest engine of net labor demand—a million-yuan property transaction equals purchasing 100 person-months of labor. When housing faith crumbles and the middle class starts paying down debt, this engine dies, labor supply overflows, and unemployment becomes the market's clearing mechanism.

When residents cut consumption (increasing net labor supply) while wages stay flat, everyone is supplying more labor but net demand is shrinking. The market's balancing act is layoffs—zeroing out some people's labor supply while forcing them to consume savings, converting them from net suppliers to net demanders. This is the rebalancing mechanism.

Spence's signaling theory: recruiters can't assess true ability, so they infer from a few signals. Degrees expire after 5-10 years; afterward, age and employment gaps become negative signals. The most useful network contacts aren't daily colleagues (overlapping circles) but cross-department weak ties—they hold opportunities you don't know about.

📌 Key Takeaway

Layoffs are not a recession—they are market clearing in a supply-heavy, demand-weak economy. Understand the底层 logic: wealth=debt identity, middle-class mortgages are the largest net demand engine, and once faith cracks unemployment becomes inevitable. Survival strategy: stay debt-free, hold cash, and enjoy cheap goods in an era of labor oversupply. If you're already leveraged and midlife-laid-off, re-employment depends not on skills but on signals—your network's endorsement is the only credible market signal, and that depends on workplace expectation management: make those who matter feel your investment in the relationship.
