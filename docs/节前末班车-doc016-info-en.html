Last Train Before the Holiday

By Mao Bidao · 2024/09/29 · WeChat Official Account

The final dispatch before the National Day holiday, written on the eve of the historic 9/30 surge. Friday's Shanghai Exchange outage severely distorted prices—the true market level was hiding ~2% of gains, already near 3150 rather than the reported 3087. Over the weekend, bullish signals stacked up: new account applications surged 5-6x, the ICBC bank-to-broker transfer index hit 704% of the 2017 daily average, and bond funds sold off as money fled to equities. Technical indicators were already screaming overbought, but in extreme moves they temporarily fail. The author's verdict: "Rather than fear a pullback, guard against missing out." 03
Divergence Amid the Frenzy: Who's Buying, Who's Selling
Every trade has a buyer and a seller—Friday's ¥1.5T volume concealed enormous disagreement.

Plenty of people sold into strength and fled at break-even; only the sheer volume of new money pushed through the selling pressure to keep rising. The entire industry, beaten down for three and a half years, was finally getting to flex—everyone wanted to make this wave count.

🐱 The author's cold eye: As for how many bull-callers truly believe it and how many just want prices higher so they can offload—that's hard to say.

On technical indicators: Thursday was already severely overbought; by all logic Friday should not have risen. But it did—spectacularly. In extreme moves, technical indicators temporarily fail—maybe only 3-4 trading days, but those 3-4 days can produce some truly absurd outcomes.

04
Short-Term Call: Rather Fear Missing Out Than a Pullback
Taking all information together: a crash within 1-2 trading days is unlikely—the risk of missing the move exceeds the risk of a pullback.

This was the author's clear verdict on the eve of the 9/30 explosion:

🏠 For ordinary people: a home purchase decision deserves far more caution than a stock trade—stocks you can exit with two mouse clicks; buying and selling property will strip a layer off your skin.
Core Takeaway

Written on the eve of the historic 9/30 surge, this dispatch is a rare piece of ex-ante judgment, not hindsight. The Shanghai Exchange outage concealed ~2% of real gains, the bank-to-broker index hit 7× the 2017 daily average, and new accounts surged 5-6×—all pointing to the same thing: money was flooding into equities with manic intensity.

Yet Mao Bidao kept his cool. He flagged the critical detail: ¥1.5T in volume means ¥1.5T in selling alongside ¥1.5T in buying—plenty of people were dumping into strength and fleeing at break-even. "As for how many bull-callers truly believe it and how many just want higher prices to offload—that's hard to say."

He also captured the structural spillover underway: bond funds hemorrhaging capital to equities, and housing purchase restrictions about to exit history. The stock market's manic bull was not an isolated event—it was reshaping the entire Chinese asset landscape.
