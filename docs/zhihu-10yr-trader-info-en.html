Why Can't Most Traders Achieve Consistent Profits After 10+ Years?

Classic Zhihu answer by "Bu Er Ge" · 2018 · 34-page deep analysis

Trading is not a knowledge contest — it's a cognitive evolution. From beginner to consistent profitability, every trader must pass through five unstoppable stages. 80% remain forever trapped at the elementary stage of "learn indicators → frequent stop-outs → shrinking account," because they never grasp that consistent profits come not from technical indicators, but from probabilistic thinking, disciplined execution, and belief.

02
Cognitive Trap

The Elementary Trap: Three Fatal Flaws of Technical Indicators

80% of traders are stuck here, voraciously learning KDJ, MACD, moving averages, Elliott Wave, Gann Theory… yet their accounts keep shrinking. Because every technical indicator carries three inherent defects:

🔀
Contradiction
  • MACD crossover but KDJ death cross
  • Moving average crossover too — which to follow?
  • Each added indicator doubles judgment combos
3 indicators = 8 possible combinations
🔄
Blunting
  • Make small profits in ranges using KDJ
  • When trend begins, KDJ repeatedly signals short
  • Short for a month → massive loss
Range profits lost in trending markets
⏰
Lagging
  • KDJ crossover appears after the move
  • Enter long → stuck at the top
  • Go contrarian during a bull market → blow up
Every indicator is a rear-view mirror
03
Probability Principles

Three Rates: How Casinos Win Consistently

No one can accurately predict the market — every market behavior is a probability outcome. Understand these three concepts and you shift from "guessing" to "planning":

Win Rate
Profit ratio per trade
Casino dice edge: 51.35%
→ guaranteed profit
Risk:Reward
Profit / Loss ratio
30% win rate + 1:3 R:R
→ net profitable system
Ruin Rate
Position / risk management
Max 2% loss per trade = 50 shots
Give probability time to work
📐
Soros' formula: "It's not whether you're right or wrong that's important, but how much money you make when you're right and how much you lose when you're wrong." — Risk:reward is the fundamental question for every trader.
04
Cognitive Reframe

Outcome ≠ Execution ≠ P&L: Three Layers of Discipline

Most traders fixate on a single dimension: profit & loss. But consistent profitability requires distinguishing three completely separate concepts — this is how probabilistic thinking becomes operational:

🎯
Outcome
  • Did price follow your high-probability plan?
  • Small profit but not per plan = counted as a failure
  • Focus on trend-reading ability
Level 1: Probability mastery
✅
Execution
  • Did you follow the plan completely?
  • Even flawed plans must be executed, fixed later
  • Never alter the plan mid-trade
Level 2: Plan discipline
💰
P&L
  • Least important — merely a byproduct
  • The more you focus on P&L, the less you earn
  • Master outcome + execution → P&L follows
Level 3: Results take care of themselves
"
Obsessing over your P&L swings your psychology back and forth until you lose execution ability. Focus solely on outcome (trend-reading) and execution (plan adherence), and profit becomes inevitable — it's merely a byproduct of your plan.
— Ray Dalio, Principles: First, Second, and Third-Order Effects
05
Behavior Change

From Habit Change to the Middle Way

You know all the principles — so why can't you follow them? Because willpower is unreliable. You must treat yourself like a child and find external accountability mechanisms. A new behavioral habit takes roughly 21 days to form.

✅ Virtuous Cycle

Confidence → Discipline → Profit → Pride → Stronger confidence. Repeat discipline mechanically until it becomes habit. Keep a trade journal, have a spouse review your account, close your platform after hitting your trade limit.

❌ Vicious Cycle

Profit → Break rules → Loss → Frustration → Emotional spiral → Blow-up. After 3-4 consecutive stop-outs, psychology breaks, you start chasing and revenge-trading, abandoning your high-probability system. Over-aggressive → big loss → over-cautious → miss opportunity → over-aggressive again…

Overtrading
→ Trade count limit
Top/Bottom Picking
→ Trend pullbacks only
Over-leverage
→ Fixed position %
Emotional Trading
→ Reset to zero first
💡
Key Takeaway

Trading is an evolution from knowing to doing to becoming: master the three rates (win rate, risk:reward, ruin rate), let discipline rule all probability, distinguish the three layers of outcome / execution / P&L, reform destructive behavior patterns — and ultimately you'll discover that consistent profitability isn't a technique, it's a way of being: the highest good is like water (shàng shàn ruò shuǐ), the Middle Way (zhōng yōng zhī dào). Left-inclined adventurism burns you; right-inclined defeatism starves you. Control your emotions, walk the central path.
