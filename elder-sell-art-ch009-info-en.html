Chapter 9 "Making Money in Bear Markets"

After a 4.5-year bull run (2003–2007) grew tired, the 2007–2009 bear market destroyed trillions in value — MGM fell 95%, SHLD fell 80%
Individual traders who read the technical signals could have profited by shorting, but most missed the opportunity because they didn't know how to sell

The Core Question
When the bull market ends and value destruction begins, how can individual traders use technical analysis to not just survive but profit by shorting in a bear market?

Alexander Elder uses the 2007–2009 Great Bear Market as his textbook, systematically demonstrating the Triple Screen trading system in action: weekly charts set the strategic direction (identifying divergences and death crosses), daily charts determine tactical timing (shorting in the value zone, covering near the lower channel band). From the NH-NL indicator's "officers fleeing" signal, through complete short-selling case studies of MGM, ISRG, SHLD, BAC, and FRE, to the cautionary tale of Buffett buying GE too early — this chapter delivers a complete bear market trading methodology. Core conclusion: swing trading beats position holding through the entire trend; perfection is the enemy of excellence; never stop learning.

Core Signal

Divergence — The Short Seller's Strongest Signal

Bearish divergence is the most reliable sell signal in technical analysis. When prices make new highs but the MACD histogram, MACD line, or Force Index fails to follow, upward momentum is exhausted. The "Triple Crown" — all three indicators diverging simultaneously — is a near-perfect short signal. The combination of an upward false breakout plus divergence is especially lethal: price briefly breaks above the prior high, only to fall back immediately, signaling the market's refusal to accept higher prices.

✅ The MGM Lesson

At ~$100, MGM showed a perfect Triple Crown: MACD histogram, MACD line, and Force Index all diverged simultaneously. Yet Elder, influenced by a fellow passenger's "great fundamentals" pitch, only bought, never shorted. The stock ultimately collapsed 95% to single digits.
Lesson: even the most clear-eyed analyst can be mesmerized by appearances. Chart signals override others' opinions.

❌ Common Misconception

Novices love trading breakouts in the direction of the move, hoping they'll keep going. But professionals know: most breakouts fail. When price breaks above resistance but key indicators show divergence, the false breakout + divergence combo is the strongest short setup. Don't follow the crowd — follow the signal.

Tactical Tools

Systematic Shorting in Declining Channels

Once a stock enters a declining channel, two systematic approaches produce consistent short-selling profits. The MACD Reversal System: short when the MACD histogram crosses below the zero line, cover near the lower channel band. The In-Channel Shorting System: short when price rallies into the value zone between the two EMAs, place a stop just above the recent high, and take profits near the lower band. SHLD's daily chart provided four clear short signals during 2007–2008.

✅ Correct Approach

Trade with fixed position size. Never add to a position. Capturing 30% of the channel width earns an A-grade trade. Swing trading — buying low and selling high, or shorting high and covering low — is far more realistic than riding the entire trend.
Think of yourself as a merchant and your positions as inventory: rapid turnover is the hallmark of a healthy business.

❌ Fatal Mistakes

Adding to losing positions — the inevitable loss at the trend's end will devastate your account.
Chasing perfection — trying to catch the absolute top or bottom.
Discarding systems — when a system stops working, shelf it; don't discard it. Market conditions will change.
Perfection is the enemy of excellence. Greed is the enemy of success.

Lessons & Reflection

Bear Market Bottoms & Eternal Lessons

Bear markets have no floor, but bullish divergence is the strongest signal of a bear-to-bull transition. When prices make new lows but the MACD histogram only dips slightly, selling pressure is exhausted — a bounce is imminent. However, the "Hound of the Baskervilles" signal warns us: bullish divergence can also fail. FRE (Freddie Mac) saw two consecutive bullish divergences collapse, eventually plunging to $0.46. Back-to-back Hound signals are virtually unheard of — a reminder to never say "impossible."

"
The week Buffett bought GE, my weekly composite system was red. Had this great man asked for my advice, I would have suggested he wait. Three weeks later, the GE weekly chart turned from red to blue, flashing a strong bullish divergence signal... I began buying in March 2009 when the secondary test completed the bullish divergence.
— Alexander Elder, on Buffett's 2008 GE purchase

✅ Survival Rules

• Never hold long when the weekly EMA has a death cross
• Triple Screen: analyze weekly first, then daily
• Always set a stop-loss — never trade naked
• Think probabilistically: accept multiple small losses
• Choose a time frame that fits your temperament and stick with it

❌ Path to Ruin

• Ignore weekly signals — stare only at the daily chart
• Trade without stops or casually move them wider
• Keep adding to long positions as the stock falls
• Try to catch the absolute top or bottom
• Abandon your trading system after one or two stop-outs
