01
Strategic Retreat

Distance Brings Clarity

Back home after market defeat, Livermore discovered that observing from a distance actually improved clarity — calm reflection on past follies was the key to his comeback.

The moment he arrived home, he knew his life had only one pursuit: raising capital to return to Wall Street. Now he had time to calmly reflect on past mistakes. The immediate task was raising new capital. He was more eager than ever — next time, he could do better.

This cycle of ‘strategic retreat → deep reflection → renewed assault’ defined Livermore’s entire trading career. Every defeat was the eve of a cognitive upgrade.

02
Blacklisted

Bucket Shop Blacklist Exposed

Old bucket shops remembered him and refused service; new ones were unreliable with position limits. Livermore used friends as proxy traders with secret signals — but this too was soon exposed by the clerk: ‘Livingston is behind you.’

He had a friend place orders while he pretended to wander in. They used pre-arranged signals. But soon the shop complained about the orders. One day, when the friend tried to sell 100 St. Paul, the clerk said bluntly: ‘When Livingston isn’t hanging around, you never do anything. The moment he walks in, suddenly you’re a different person.’

This cut off his livelihood. But after expenses, he had already netted several hundred dollars — even in extreme adversity, he could still turn a profit.

03
New Channel

Sham Brokerages: Promising Superior Execution

While complaining to veteran traders about poor brokerage execution, a stranger introduced Livermore to ‘telephone brokerage firms’ — sham brokerages with branches in 78 cities, claiming superior execution through special relationships.

Roberts told him these firms had deep connections with the biggest NYSE houses and, through personal influence and monthly volumes of hundreds of thousands of shares, could get far better service than individual clients. They treated a 10-share order the same as a 1,000-share order.

Livermore immediately recognized this as solicitation for sham brokerages — but at this point, he needed exactly such a trading channel to build capital. Their existence proved the market always had grey areas providing opportunities for ‘talented but undercapitalized’ traders.

04
Method Upgrade

Methodological Upgrade Before the Third Assault

Through this reflective period, Livermore realized the essential difference between scalping for small moves and anticipating major moves — between gambling and speculation. This marked a fundamental shift in his trading attitude.

The first change in his method involved the time factor. He couldn’t wait for certainty to scalp 1–2 points like in bucket shops. To profit at a legitimate brokerage, he had to start much earlier — anticipating coming moves, forecasting price changes. It sounds banal, but it held extraordinary significance for him.

He began cultivating interest in trade reports, railroad earnings, financial statistics, and commercial data. Anything that could help him trade more intelligently — he welcomed it all, never tiring.

05
Relentless Spirit

Only One Pursuit in This Life

Livermore’s obsession — ‘my only pursuit in this life: raise capital and return to Wall Street’ — was the fundamental engine that allowed him to rebuild from zero time and again.

He wasn’t overly hopeful but kept trying to break back into bucket shops. When refused, he found alternative routes. He’d honestly confessed his New York defeat — the shops had no reason to think he wouldn’t be a good customer, yet they still refused. But he never gave up. Someday, his trading path would be right.

‘If the goal you pursue is the right one, everything will come to you, rewarding your correctness.’ — Livermore’s words to himself, and a maxim for all traders.

💡
Key Takeaway

Livermore’s second retreat exemplifies strategic withdrawal: not surrender, but changing battlefields to rebuild strength. He replaced complaint with reflection, old paths with new channels. The strongest traders aren’t those who never fail, but those who always find new ways to rise after every fall.
