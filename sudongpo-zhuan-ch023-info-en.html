苏东坡传 · 第23章「Governor of Hangzhou」

In 1089, Su Dongpo arrived in Hangzhou as governor at age 52. The ancient city of half a million was in ruins: official buildings collapsing on residents, canals choked with silt, no public hospital, citizens drinking salt-tainted water. How could a poet-governor transform Hangzhou into a true 'paradise on earth' in just eighteen months?

▎CORE QUESTION

Su Dongpo worked relentlessly, not opening a book for eighteen months. He tackled four major projects — water supply, public hospital, canal dredging, and West Lake restoration — transforming a decaying city into a true paradise. He judged cases with poetic flair, painted fans to settle debts, proving governance could be both efficient and humane.
01
Public Service

China's First Public Hospital

Su Dongpo allocated 2,000 strings of cash and donated 50 taels of gold to establish China's first public hospital, Anle Fang, in the heart of Hangzhou.

Hangzhou's 500,000 residents at the Qiantang estuary were highly vulnerable to epidemics. Anle Fang treated over 1,000 patients in three years. Previously in Mizhou, he had posted prescriptions on city walls. The hospital later moved lakeside as Anji Fang and continued serving.

02
Urban Engineering

Canal Dredging & Water Supply

Su Dongpo engineered an ingenious diversion system: silt-laden seawater through suburban Maoshan Canal, urban Yanqiao Canal kept clear; West Lake spring water piped through a citywide ceramic network.

Consulting a 70-year-old monk, he replaced bamboo pipes with ceramic ones protected by stone slabs — 300 yards of pipeline. Canal dredging began three months into his tenure and finished by April the following year, achieving an unprecedented depth of eight chi.

03
Poetic Governance

The Judge Who Painted Fans

Su Dongpo used his judge's brush to paint calligraphy and landscapes on twenty silk fans, enabling a debt-ridden youth to sell them — a poet's wisdom warmer than any decree.

A young man was sued after his father's death and rainy spring. Su pondered, painted twenty fans on the spot — they sold at 1,000 coins each within minutes. He also forgave an exam candidate fraudulently using the Su name, even writing him a recommendation — human compassion beyond legal bounds.

04
Engineering Legacy

Su Causeway: Eternal Landmark

Su Dongpo's restoration of West Lake earned Hangzhou its enduring reputation as 'paradise on earth' — a masterpiece where human genius harmonized with nature.

West Lake was choked with weeds and silt. Su petitioned the court, dredged the lakebed, and used excavated mud to build a causeway ('Su Causeway') planted with peach and willow trees. He leased water areas to farmers for water caltrops, preventing weed spread while generating maintenance revenue.

KEY TAKEAWAY

Eighteen months: one public hospital, one water system, one dredged canal, one eternal causeway. Su Dongpo proved a poet can also be a doer — identifying problems with an artist's sensitivity, solving them with a doer's decisiveness. The highest form of governance is not power, but making the city better because you were there.
