Cultural Bitter Journey · Ch.18 "Arctic Chill"

Seven European images—from the Medici to the EU flag—what comparative coordinates do they offer for Chinese civilization?

CORE PROPOSITION

Europe is not the answer—it is a mirror. Seven comparative images reveal fundamental differences in how civilizations mobilize power, wealth, and culture. Seven images—the Medici's cultural convergence, German scholars' independence, Lyon's inventions, Britain's gradualism, European leisure, Iceland's Law Rock, and the EU flag—all point to one core insight: civilization needs the constructive convergence of power, wealth, and culture, not confiscation and radicalism.

01
Convergence

The Medici Letters: Power, Wealth and Culture United

The Medici family used wealth and power to gather humanist masters, completing a collective elevation for humanity—something China's historical transitions never truly achieved.

No Renaissance, no modern world. No Florence, no Renaissance. No Medici, no Florence or Renaissance. They sought true creators with the utmost reverence—discovering, cultivating, protecting. Chinese reformers thought more about confiscation—even when righteous, it lowered the grade of historical transition.

“
This city's double following—citizens following the Medici, the Medici following art masters—completed a collective elevation concerning all humanity.
—— Yu Qiuyu

Dante's death mask was kept in the Medici household. Traces of Michelangelo and Da Vinci were everywhere. China's transitions often neglected building a new spiritual-cultural foundation.

02
Independence

German Scholars' Graves: Why Are They Purer?

German scholars are far purer than Chinese literati—unentangled with power structures, they could reach greatness.

Hegel upheld academic freedom as Berlin University rector. Kant lived quietly contemplating the cosmos, yet proposed: 'Dare to use reason in all public spheres'—precisely the fatal weakness of Chinese intellectuals. Goethe held high office without affecting Faust.

✅ European Intellectuals

Goethe: high office, great art · Hegel: academic freedom · Kant: village solitude, cosmic thought · Havel: presidency and writing

⚠️ Chinese Intellectuals

Entangled with power · Engagement deconstructs thought · Withdrawal becomes performance · Meager legacy for the public sphere

The contemporary model is Václav Havel, who spent over a decade as Czech president while maintaining independent intellectual reflection.

03
Innovation

Lyon's Invention Chart: Beyond the Four Great Inventions

In the 19th century alone, Lyon produced over a dozen inventions that propelled humanity's modernization—Chinese people must stop resting on the laurels of the distant Four Great Inventions.

By the late 19th century, the French themselves were reflecting: 'Compared to American and German inventions, France has fallen far behind.' Meanwhile, China barely participated in modern civilization's creation. Even today, nearly all major components of modern life were not invented by Chinese people.

⚠️
Needham's Reminder: Don't seek one-way comfort from Science and Civilisation in China. Even as Needham wrote, Britain kept creating—penicillin, radar, computers, television... That the British organized ancient Chinese inventions more thoroughly than the Chinese themselves—that is magnanimity. If we become smug, that is small-mindedness.

Even more painful: the next generation doesn't feel this pain, still intoxicated by 'national essences,' as cultural conservatism intensifies.

04
Path

Windsor Castle and Eton: The Wisdom of Gradualism

Britain's path of moderate gradualism—with less bloodshed, less stridency—has been proven by history to be a remarkable path.

Chinese civilization originally believed in the Golden Mean, but under modern existential crisis was deeply influenced by French radicalism. From the French Revolution to the Paris Commune—impassioned rhetoric and bloody struggle infected Chinese reformers. Radicalism's damage harms the present and future.

“
They've done everything society's development required, crossed every hurdle civilization's progress demanded, acquired every concept a modern society needs—what more can you say?
—— Yu Qiuyu on the British path

Britain: 'Just proceeding amiably, feeling as you go, following experience, skirting obstacles—the path of least consumption, the path of fastest development.'

05
Ecology

European Leisure: Busy-ness May Be Confusion

Leisure may well be clarity, and busy-ness confusion—the Chinese pattern of endless toil has hardened into psychological inertia.

Europeans believe weekday labor is largely for vacation—only during vacation can people briefly escape alienation and recover humanity. Chinese immigrants accumulate wealth through relentless labor, yet have almost no leisure. Nordic countries' slower growth stems partly from thorough welfare policies.

Europe
Weekday work
largely for vacation
China
Occasional rest
only to work better
Sweden
Social security

Sweden's economic ethic: property must manifest as duty, freedom must manifest as responsibility. Chinese people rapidly accumulating wealth—have they considered their future mode of existence?

06
Rule of Law

Iceland's Law Rock and the EU Flag: Beyond Revenge and Nation-States

Nordic heroes spent centuries setting down swords and blood-oaths to listen to the law and church bells—this is the spiritual pass humanity must cross.

China's martial arts (wuxia) spirit—clan revenge, self-defined justice—is inherently lawless. The Norse Sagas record heroes like Njal who recognized the new honor based on reason and were willing to die for it. In Brussels, the EU flag signifies a grand conscience transcending nation-states.

“
Many evil deeds hide behind the banners of 'nation' and 'state.' We should tear aside these banners and expose the vast dark currents of anti-humanity, anti-society, and anti-life to broad daylight.
—— Yu Qiuyu

Kant believed humanity would overcome antagonism toward harmony, building a world-scale 'universal legislative civil society'—the theoretical foundation of European unification. Common civilizational principles must be the ultimate direction.

💡
KEY TAKEAWAY

From Florence to Iceland, Europe offers seven mirrors: the Medici's power-wealth-culture convergence, German scholarly independence, Lyon's innovation anxiety, Britain's gradualist wisdom, Europe's leisure ethic, Iceland's legal domestication, and the EU's transcendence of nation-states—each illuminates a blind spot in Chinese civilization. Learning from Europe is not worship but self-understanding through contrast: can China complete the civilizational transition from 'reform through confiscation' to 'creation through convergence'?
