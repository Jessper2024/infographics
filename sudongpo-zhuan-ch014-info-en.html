Core Insight: Su Dongpo's genius only truly began at forty—in Xuzhou he transformed from poet to man of action, anchoring his immortal identity as "champion of the common people" through the flood-rescue miracle and the Yellow Tower.

Core QuestionNeither Hangzhou nor Mizhou allowed him to fully deploy his talents—how would Su Dongpo find his true stage in life?

Core AnswerXuzhou gave him the opportunity. When the Yellow River burst its banks and besieged the city for 45 days, he lived atop the city wall personally directing reinforcements, then built the Yellow Tower as a symbol of earth's power over water. He also cared for prisoners, reformed army discipline, and became the nation's foremost scholar after Ouyang Xiu's death.

01
Crisis

Battling the Great Flood

Only three months after taking office, the Yellow River burst its banks fifty li north of Xuzhou—the water rose to 28.9 feet, and Su Dongpo risked his life to save the city, not returning home for weeks.

The flood was blocked by southern mountains and kept rising. Wealthy families fled the city; Su Dongpo stopped them at the gates: "With me here, you'd better stay." He forced them back to prevent panic and rioting. Wading through mud, he went straight to the army camp to ask the garrison commander for help—despite these being imperial troops under the emperor's direct command. The commander immediately agreed: "If the governor personally supervises, we too must do our part." Walls were raised and reinforced; defensive works stretched 984 zhang long, 10 zhang high, and 2 zhang wide.

The city was besieged for 45 days; on October 15 the Yellow River returned to its old course. The city saved, citizens were wild with joy. Su Dongpo petitioned the court for funds to build stone walls and eventually received over 30,000 strings of cash to construct a timber dam.

02
Architecture

The Yellow Tower

Su Dongpo built a ten-zhang tower atop the outer wall and named it "Yellow Tower"—the name embodies profound Chinese cosmological wisdom.

According to the Five Elements theory: yellow represents earth, black represents water. Earth has absorptive power and is said to conquer water—the Yellow Tower thus became a permanent symbol of flood resistance. On the Double Ninth Festival of 1078, a grand opening ceremony was held. The entire city came to see it. Thick fog that morning made everyone feel, as they peered from the windows hearing oars below, as if aboard a ship at sea. When the sky cleared, distant fishing villages and temples beneath rocky peaks came into view.

Su Dongpo wrote an essay to commemorate the occasion and had it carved in stone. Later, when he was exiled, all his steles were ordered destroyed—but the prefect buried it in a moat instead. A decade later, after the ban was forgotten, a subsequent prefect dug it up, made thousands of rubbings, and made a fortune.

The Yellow Tower belonged to the whole city—a visible symbol of future flood resistance. Su Dongpo loved building: his "Transcendence Terrace" in Mizhou and the Yellow Tower in Xuzhou became synonymous with his collected poems from each period.

03
Reform

Prisoners & Soldiers

Few governors personally cared about prisoners—Su Dongpo visited them and for the first time assigned a prison doctor, declaring that "prisoners are also people."

The law prohibited officials from flogging prisoners to death, but had no provisions against prisoners dying from illness or neglect. Dongpo identified this loophole and corrected it. He also discovered that many soldiers deserted and became bandits because they received no travel allowance for distant missions, sinking into debt. He revised the regulation—at a cost of only a few hundred strings of cash per year, the problem was solved. He banned gambling and drinking in the barracks, reporting to the emperor that local troops were now "the best trained in all commanderies."

🏥 Prison Healthcare

Dongpo assigned doctors to prisoners for the first time. He criticized a law that "banned flogging to death but not dying from illness." Prisoners were people—this conviction guided his entire life.

⚔️ Army Discipline

Provided travel stipends for soldiers on missions, eliminating desertion. Banned gambling and drinking; Xuzhou troops became the best-trained in the entire country.

04
Mentor

Literary Master

After Ouyang Xiu's death, Su Dongpo's poetic fame elevated him to become the nation's undisputed foremost scholar—literati all addressed him as "Master."

Qin Guan wrote to Dongpo: "I alone do not wish for a marquisate of ten thousand households—I only wish to meet Su of Xuzhou." He called Dongpo "a qilin in the heavens" and declared, "Never letting worldly things obstruct natural genius—how many such people exist south of the Dipper?" Huang Tingjian sent two modest poems introducing himself, comparing Dongpo to a lone pine on a cliff and himself to humble grass in the valley. Dongpo replied: "I was just seeking your friendship, and feared I might not obtain it." Zhang Lei and Chao Buzhi had already met him earlier—thus the "Four Scholars of the Su School" were complete.

"Your letter comes with such formal courtesy, as if meeting someone you fear. Why? I was just seeking your friendship, and feared I might not obtain it." — Su Dongpo's reply to Huang Tingjian

The great poet-monk Canliao also became Dongpo's lifelong close friend during this period. He had lived near Hangzhou for three years without approaching Dongpo, merely watching from afar in silent admiration—he was too proud to seek fame. At the grand Yellow Tower gathering that autumn, Dongpo's warmth and wit won him universal scholarly devotion.

05
Life

Life Begins at Forty

Lin Yutang declares: even for a genius like Su Dongpo, life only truly began at forty—during the Yellow Tower period, Su Dongpo "suddenly found himself."

As vice-governor of Hangzhou he could accomplish little; as governor of Mizhou the region was too remote and impoverished. After a brief forced retirement and a politically dark period, there emerged "a perfect, well-rounded, mature, active, and loyal Su Dongpo—witty and worldly-wise, a friend and champion of the people, and a great soul." That Mid-Autumn he had a grandson, felt lonely at night; six days later he received a poem from Ziyou—"Who washed the eyes of heaven? It must have cost a thousand gallons of the Milky Way"—the brothers' poetic exchanges sustained each other.

It was precisely because he was so beloved and highly esteemed that his later arrest and trial shocked the entire nation. The man of action of the Yellow Tower period had already proven that Su Dongpo was not merely a poet—he was a great doer.

💡
KEY TAKEAWAY

The Yellow Tower is more than a building—it is the monument to Su Dongpo's life transformation. Before forty, he was a genius poet; after forty, he became a "champion of the people." True maturity is the power to turn poetic talent into purposeful action.
