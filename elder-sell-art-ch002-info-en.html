Trading Psychology and Risk Management

The New Trading for a Living II: The Art of Selling · Chapter 2 · Alexander Elder

📋 Chapter Overview

Psychology is the real trading tool — money management is your safety net

Traders own computers, software, and data, but technical tools alone cannot guarantee success. Emotional swings destroy decisions — undisciplined money management blows up accounts. This chapter builds a safe trading system on two pillars: five psychological disciplines + the 2% & 6% dual rules.

Core Insight

Your Mindset Is Your Core Trading Tool

Your emotions, expectations, and fears directly determine your trading outcomes. Excessive emotional swings will ruin you — decision-making must be clear and free of bias. Elder insists that mastering your personal emotions is the key to trading success.

✅ Disciplined Trader

Executes a system-based plan with clear, unbiased decisions. Emotions are signals, not commands. Every trade builds replicable experience.

❌ Emotional Trader

Too afraid to enter, too greedy to exit. Copies others under pressure, abandons discipline, and gets trapped in a cycle of self-punishment.

03
Risk Warning

Five Warning Signs of Doomed Traders

The market generates endless temptations — certain traders have almost no chance of winning. Elder identifies five behavioral patterns. If you recognize any in yourself, do not trade until you fix it:

⚠️
Addictive Behaviors: Alcoholics and drug addicts cannot succeed in trading. They may win a few trades by luck, but over the long term they have almost no hope. Do not trade until you overcome these problems.
⚠️
Trading Under Emotional Distress: Even an excellent trader has only a "narrow" edge. A severe toothache or a conflict with your spouse that distracts you — anything that reduces this edge breaks the balance. Stop trading.
⚠️
Relying on Others' Opinions: Thoughtlessly copying others' trades or uncritically adopting their views, rather than formulating and firmly executing your own trading plan.
⚠️
The Self-Punishment Loop: Constantly breaking rules and beating yourself up. Hotheads who never take their own views seriously spiral through failure — these people cannot become good traders.
⚠️
Emotional Heavy Positioning: When positions are too large, you become rigid and nervous, making emotion-based decisions — the results cannot be satisfactory.

If trading is a tightrope walk, risk control is the safety net below — it saves your life when you slip. The 2% rule is like a shark: a single deadly bite, but one you can guard against. Risk no more than 2% of your total account on any single trade.

05
Circuit Breaker

The 6% Rule · Monthly Drawdown Circuit Breaker

Piranhas are more dangerous than sharks — they swarm and strip your account bite by bite. Most people add to losing positions in a last-ditch attempt to turn things around. The right move: stop trading and rest. The 6% rule mandates stopping all trading for the rest of the month once total monthly losses reach 6%.

The Piranha Rule: If an ox stumbles into a piranha-infested river, only a pile of bones remains. After losing ⅓ of your capital, you need a 50% gain just to break even. Before every trade, ask yourself: "Does the 6% rule allow me to trade? How much have I lost this month? How much more can I afford to lose?"
💡
Key Takeaway

Trading success = Disciplined Psychology × Money Management. Mindset is your real trading tool. The 2% rule guards against shark-like single blows; the 6% rule protects against piranha-style slow bleed. Used together, your trading becomes remarkably safe — celebrate when you profit, and remain calm when you lose.
