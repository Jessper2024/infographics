01
Root of Distress
'Doing A Thinking B': The Root of Anxiety and Incompetence
Running while thinking about work, eating while worrying about relationships, sleeping while mind races like a waterfall — 'doing A thinking B' is the classic sign of mind-body separation. Attention splits into 'action-focused' and 'feeling-focused' parts. Initially they're unified, but as actions become automatic, feeling fades — distraction replaces focus. Action without feeling is a soulless shell, continuously undermining emotional state and skill growth. Mind-body unity creates a high-quality life of happiness and focus.

02
Mindfulness Hack
One Trick to Boost Attention: Return Feeling to Action
Taste food when eating, feel the ground when walking — transform 'body doing A, mind thinking B' into 'body and mind united doing A.' Physical sensation is the best medium to enter the present moment; feeling things fade is excellent focus training. Before enlightenment: chopping wood thinking of water, carrying water thinking of cooking. After: chopping wood is chopping wood, carrying water is carrying water, cooking is cooking. Mind-body unity isn't just about focusing on the present — it's about enjoying the present. Changing this habit changes your fundamental behavioral pattern.

03
Flow Code
Deep Immersion = Flow: The Safe Handle of Evolution's Sword
Deep immersion (flow) is evolution's double-edged sword — immerse in learning and you rise; immerse in pleasure and you fall. The ability to enter deep immersion is the biggest difference between experts and ordinary people. The weak are easily distracted, needing ideal environments to learn; the strong actively block interference, immersing even in noisy places. The former are in the dominated class; the latter in the dominating class. The golden key to standing out is deliberately honing deep immersion — not just through passion, but through a technical methodology.

04
Genius Formula
Genius = Right Method + Massive Practice
Psychologist Anders Ericsson found: the essence of genius is 'the right method' plus 'massive practice.' The right method has four elements: ① Clear goals — 'play three times without error' not 'practice for 30 min'; ② Extreme focus — short 100% > long 70%; ③ Effective feedback — coaching, peer exchange, or self-reflection; working in isolation guarantees inefficiency; ④ Stretch zone practice — too hard frustrates, too easy stagnates. All four are indispensable.

05
Intersection Zone
Focus = The Intersection of Emotion and Intelligence
Focus is both an emotional issue (anxiety causes distraction) and an intellectual one (wrong methods are ineffective). Tackle focus from both ends. Emotion: return feeling to action, unite mind and body. Intelligence: master the four elements of the right method, establish active immersion patterns. The secret of two-brain collaboration: first extreme focus (conscious mode), then complete release (subconscious diffuse mode) — inspiration will surface. Archimedes discovered buoyancy in the bath — the result of relaxing after intense focus.
