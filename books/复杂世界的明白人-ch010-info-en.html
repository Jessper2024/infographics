Understanding a Complex World · Ch.10 "Big Cities Are a Crucible"

Life Needs Leverage · Big Cities Offer Multiple Value Systems A big city is a "force field" — here your potential has more chance to ignite. You may become someone you never imagined possible. But relentless pressure and anxiety are the price you must pay. If you are young, from an ordinary background, and want to venture out, a tier-one city is an absolutely sound choice. If you already have decent conditions and want a simple life, staying in a second or third-tier city is just fine.

This chapter presents a systematic analysis of geographic life choices. The argument unfolds in three layers: First, personal value needs a "fast track" to take off — opportunity and acceleration channels provide wealth leverage, and big cities concentrate the most opportunities. Second, big cities force you to keep moving — crisis awareness and perceived gaps drive self-transformation, raising your pain tolerance. Third, the costs are equally enormous — chronic anxiety, class-comparison stress, and declining fertility are big-city byproducts. The final verdict: big cities are "self-inflicted growth," best suited for the young and ordinary to undergo trial by fire.

01
Leverage
Life Needs Leverage — Your Luck, National Luck, and Big-City Opportunity
Getting rich through individual ability alone is extremely hard. You need opportunity, and big cities overflow with it. Hard work and hustle only set the floor — without them, you lose even the bare minimum. To push further, you need someone to pull you up, an entry into an emerging industry, or contact with something about to explode. In 2010, only people with no better options built mobile apps. Now they couldn't fail if they tried. Big cities provide an "acceleration channel" — a lukewarm person joins a wildly expanding place and soon leaps from grunt to project lead, a path normally taking four to five years.

02
Value Discovery
A Single Work of Art Meets Completely Different Fates in Different Settings — Big Cities Offer Diverse Value Systems
The more people and scenarios a city gathers, the more chances to discover your unusual skills. A Monet painting authenticated as genuine fetched a fortune. Proven fake, it became instantly worthless. The same object meets a different fate in a different setting. Your output can sell for completely different prices in a different environment. In a small place, the people you meet have limited reach — even if they want to pull you up, they can't. In a big city, you meet more people — what if one recognizes your talent, or you stumble upon work that matches your unique gift?
🏘️

Small-Town Limitations

The people you meet have limited reach. They can't pull you up even if they want to. Your odd skills may stay buried for life.

🏙️

Big-City Diversity

Switching jobs and environments is easy. Your value gets assessed from multiple angles. A top-selling realtor was nothing back home — only in Beijing did they discover their natural gift for selling.

03
Self-Reform
Progress Means Being Brutal to Yourself — Big Cities Come with Built-In Crisis and Gap Awareness
Without pressure, nobody can resolve to hurt themselves. Big cities push you forward as a whole — slow down even slightly and you get punished. In a small place, you might clock out on time and feel sorry for yourself when overtime hits 10 p.m. In Beijing, Shanghai, Guangzhou, or Shenzhen, the subway is packed by 7 a.m., and at 10 p.m. the streets still teem with commuters. After Tesla arrived, BYD made visible progress — threat is what drives improvement. Personal "pain" resembles an expected threshold: recruits who go through boot camp dramatically raise their pain threshold without actually becoming superhuman.
"Progress itself is about being brutal to yourself. Without pressure, no one can resolve to hurt themselves."
04
Balance Sheet
Big Cities Are a Double-Edged Sword — Money Saved Is Fuel, Money Spent Is Burning
Tier-one city salaries far exceed elsewhere. It all depends on how you spend. A Beijing delivery driver earns seven to eight thousand a month, pays six hundred in rent, spends twenty to thirty yuan a day on food, and sends five to six thousand home to their children every month. If you blow every extra yuan you earn, your youth becomes fuel for that city when you leave. If you save it and take it to a tier-two or tier-three city, then the tier-one city has effectively supplied fuel for you. Most people never unlock special skills, never catch good luck, and after burning time retreat to a second-tier city — this fear haunts you from the moment you set foot in the big city.

05
Ultimate Choice
Big Cities Breathe Anxiety — High Income Brings No Joy, Transforming Views on Fertility and Lifestyle
Tier-one cities swarm with top talent everywhere. Even high earners feel zero superiority. The author saw a woman's comment — in Shanghai, she was so exhausted and breathless every day that having a child felt like creating sin. She and her close friends agreed not to have kids. She returned to Chengdu for a 9-to-5 job, walked to work, had weekends off, and her mother-in-law lived nearby — naturally she felt ready to have one child, found it easier than expected, and then wanted a second. Big-city anxiety directly shapes how people judge their life choices. But for the young from ordinary backgrounds, going to a tier-one city for "self-torture" equips them to face life's heaviest blows with calm.
🏃

Suited for Tier-One

Young, from an ordinary background, want to venture out. Go to a big city for trial by fire. Raise your capacity to endure.

🌳

Better for Tier-Two / Three

Already have decent conditions, want a simple life. No need to suffer in a big city — quality of life is higher in smaller cities.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
Big cities are a "crucible" — here you have more possibilities. Your potential has more chance to ignite. You are more likely to become someone you cannot even imagine. But the costs are equally real: relentless pressure, class-based anxiety, and wear on body and mind. Life is simply a choice: if you are young from an ordinary background, go to a tier-one city to be forged in "self-torture"; if your conditions are decent and you want a simple life, stay in a smaller city. The most important thing — save the extra money you earn in the big city rather than frittering it away. This way, even if you leave eventually, the tier-one city has provided the fuel you needed to take off.
