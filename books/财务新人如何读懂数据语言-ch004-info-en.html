Cognitive Mechanism
The Brain's Energy-Saving Mode: Forebrain Reason vs Hindbrain Intuition
The human brain processes vast amounts of information daily. To conserve energy, it offloads previously processed information (especially what has formed certain cognitions) to the hindbrain's intuition system for batch processing. Only fresh information goes to the forebrain's rational CPU. In the birthday problem, we fixate birthday diversity at 365 and naturally assume 25 people must be a small probability ŌĆö that's conceptualized thinking. Application 1
Transform Conceptual Thinking into Data Thinking for Decision-Making
When making decisions, wherever "good" or "bad" can be expressed with data, use data. Case: localizing imported materials ŌĆö conceptually it's of course a good thing. But when we track the high defect rates from cheap materials and quantify various quality costs like rework and order cancellations, we discover: the "good concept" of using local materials requires other conditions to be in place. Deciding without quantification is like judging birthday probability by intuition ŌĆö seems reasonable, but is actually dangerous.
