Insider's Guide to a Complex World · Ch.31 "What It Means When US and Japanese Companies Want to Leave China"

Is the exodus real or fabricated? The three chains locking foreign capital to China — and how we should respond

In April 2020, headlines blared "US and Japanese companies pulling out of China" — but the White House website had no such policy. It was merely advisor Kudlow's personal suggestion, repackaged by domestic media as national strategy. Cut through the fog: the truth is three "chains" — low cost, complete supply chains, and a massive market — bind foreign capital firmly to China. Tesla's 65% cost reduction and 64% sales surge after entering China is the best proof. China has wedged itself into the heart of the global industrial chain. As long as it doesn't shoot itself in the foot, no one can move it.

01
FACT CHECK
The "exit" news is clickbait — same playbook every year
The US and Japan never actually demanded withdrawal — it was merely a suggestion. The author scoured the White House news website and found no exit policy whatsoever. Tracing the source revealed it was Kudlow's personal suggestion, blown up by domestic media as US national policy. Even more absurd: for nearly a decade, there's been a wave of "exodus" headlines every year, with breathless "SHOCKING" alerts. But look at the historical data — those companies not only didn't leave, they increased investment every year. Once you've seen this routine a few times, you become as unflappable as the author.

02
FUNDAMENTALS
Capital is a money-making machine — why foreign companies come to China
Foreign companies come to China to make money, not to do charity. Failing to grasp this is childish thinking. The West began shifting production capacity to Asia in the 1960s — from the "Four Asian Tigers" to mainland China — driven solely by cost. China offers not just low cost but an educated "industrial population." You can hire a college graduate for ¥5,000–6,000 a month (~$1,000), while in the US you can't even hire a functional illiterate at that rate. But this is only the surface layer — cost was the key two or three decades ago. It no longer is.

03
CLUSTER
Complete supply chains are the ultimate chain — leave and you get replaced
The core of modern innovative economies is countless "small and specialized" modular companies. A drone that "delivers takeout and cleans windows" needs three modules — and only China can pull them all together in one place: shout it out in Shenzhen's Huaqiangbei and four teams immediately bid on it. When 80% of an industry's components are in one location, the remaining 20% will quickly be replaced if they don't relocate. Companies depend on upstream and downstream partners — get too far from the cluster and dynamic adjustment becomes a pipe dream.

04
MARKET
Demand is the ultimate weapon — China is both factory and market
For a business, the biggest headache isn't whether you can produce — it's whether anyone will buy. China has an ocean of a market: it can produce and it can consume. Tesla is the perfect proof: after entering China, sales exploded. Despite declining North American sales, China sales surged 64% in Q3 2020. The Chinese market rapidly became Tesla's second-largest globally. Musk himself said "without China, there would be no Tesla today." Exit China and you suffer a double blow to both efficiency and cost.

05
STRATEGY
Dig deep · Store grain · Build low walls — the adult mindset for society
Focus on your own affairs. Don't pick fights. But if others pick fights with you, hit back decisively. Both extreme positions (rabid xenophobia vs. rabid pro-Americanism) are adolescent thinking. China has wedged itself into the Western system like a heart — impossible to remove — as long as it doesn't self-sabotage. The playbook: "dig deep" (advance technology depth), "store grain" (strengthen reserves), "build low walls" (expand cooperation). As an ordinary citizen: exercise and stay healthy, work hard and don't add to the chaos, keep your mouth shut and stop the doom-mongering — new giants rise in every crisis. Maybe your opportunity is coming.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
Exit rumors surface every year, but foreign companies are locked in by three chains: cost advantage → industrial clusters → market pull. China is wedged into the heart of the global supply chain — as long as it doesn't self-sabotage, no one can move it. The key is to focus on your own work: dig deep (advance technology), store grain (strengthen reserves), build low walls (expand cooperation). In every crisis, super-giants grow bigger and new titans rise. Put in the effort — maybe your opportunity is coming.
