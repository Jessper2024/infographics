Replay: Mental Rehearsal of the Past · Ch.1 "What Is Replay"

Review, Reflect, Explore, Improve — deduction is what fundamentally distinguishes replay from mere summary. Before action, a sandbox; after action, a replay.

The core of replay is not simply "summarizing lessons learned" — it is rerunning past events in your mind: reviewing the process, reflecting on causes, exploring patterns, and improving capabilities. The key to replay is deduction — it is deduction that elevates replay from a flat summary into a three-dimensional cognitive reconstruction. This chapter unpacks replay's definition, core elements, and its fundamental distinction from summarizing, tracing its origins from Go to stock markets to everyday life, revealing the underlying logic of this mental method.

Core Definition
The Four Elements: Review, Reflect, Explore, Improve
Review the process, reflect on causes, explore patterns, and improve capabilities — the first three are replay's actions; improvement is replay's result. Finishing a task is only half the work. The other half is replay. Replay is not an optional bonus — it is the necessary step that transforms experience into capability.

02
Fundamental Distinction
Replay ≠ Summary: Deduction Is the Soul
A summary is flat; replay is three-dimensional. A summary is static and jumpy; replay is dynamic and continuous. Summary merely describes, analyzes, and inducts what has already happened. Replay goes further with "deduction" — virtually exploring unrealized actions, examining their possibilities and feasibilities, and finding better paths forward.
📋

Summary

Flat behavior · Organizes events · Describes, analyzes, inducts only what happened · Focuses on milestones · Static, jumpy · Post-hoc presentation

🔄

Replay

3D behavior · Re-examines from start to finish · Includes summarizing plus virtual exploration · Examines premise logic and thought process · Dynamic, continuous · Finds new paths through deduction

⚠️ Many people think replay is just summarizing lessons learned — this is a serious misconception.
Without "deduction," replay degrades into summary. Deduction lets us examine: Why did something succeed or fail? Could successes be even more successful? Could failure points find different paths? This is replay's irreplaceable value.
03
Historical Origin
Origins of Replay: From the Go Board to a Thinking Method
The term "replay" (复盘) originates from Go — after finishing a game, players rerun every move on the board, rethinking each one. Through replay, Go players discover questionable moves and decisive moves, explore better alternatives, distill new patterns, and even form game records (optimal responses for specific situations). Replay is considered the single most important method for improving Go skill.

04
Methodology Contrast
Before-Action Sandbox, After-Action Replay
The sandbox is deduction before action; replay's conclusions are deduction after action — no one confuses a sandbox with a plan, and likewise, replay is not summary. A sandbox's conclusions are imagined possibilities; replay, with execution experience as reference, yields conclusions that are verified, determinate methods.
🏗️

Before-Action Sandbox

Pre-action deduction · No real-world reference · Conclusions are imagined possibilities · Helps formulate plans · Explores "what might happen"

🔮

After-Action Replay

Post-action deduction · Execution experience as reference · Conclusions are determinate methods · Eliminates wrong paths · Determines "what should be done"

"When one masters replay, they develop profound insight and intuition about their work — they can instantly grasp the key from complex phenomena and find the solution without going through deduction step by step."

Everyday Practice
We've Always Been Replaying: A Method Everywhere
Replay doesn't require physically redoing everything — it's more a mental reconstruction, whether a realistic rerun or a brainstorming on paper. Everyone uses replay: workplace reflection, life reconsideration, friends' "let me think it through with you" — these are all replay. We've always been replaying; we just didn't call it that.

💡 Key Takeaways
Replay is rerunning past events in your mind. Its essence is the four-step cycle of Review, Reflect, Explore, Improve; its soul is deduction — the very act that distinguishes replay from summary: summary is flat, replay is three-dimensional. Replay originated from Go's "re-running the game," permeates stock analysis and daily life, and is a thinking method we all use. Before action, a sandbox; after action, a replay — together they form a complete cognitive improvement loop.
