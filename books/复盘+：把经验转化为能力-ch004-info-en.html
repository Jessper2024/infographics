SITUATION

US Military's After Action Review (AAR) — The Origin of Team Review

AAR is a standard operating procedure for US military units at all levels — essentially a professional discussion of an event where participants discover what happened, why, and how to improve. AAR comes in two forms: informal AAR happens quickly after an activity in just ten minutes; formal AAR involves meticulous planning, professional facilitation, and can last hours or even days. Both share the goal of making learning a daily practice.

📋

Formal AAR

Planned 6-8 weeks ahead with designated Observers & Controllers (OCs), support materials, and structured facilitation — suited for company-level and above

⚡

Informal AAR

Low resource, quick feedback on-site using improvised tools like pinecones and stones to model the situation — suited for platoon-level and below

The Four-Step Core Logic of Team Review — From Reflection to Transformation

Whether in the military or business, team review follows the underlying logic of "Review & Evaluate → Analyze & Reflect → Extract & Distill → Transform & Apply" — all four steps are indispensable. Corporate review involves "one large, one small" process: the large process is the design, preparation, implementation, and follow-up of "review facilitation"; the small process is the "Way of Review" that each session should follow — four interconnected stages.

QUESTION

The "Two Schools" of Review — Process Reconstruction vs Result-Oriented

In practice, review evaluation splits into "Process Reconstruction" and "Result-Oriented" approaches — they converge on the same goal, and the choice depends on circumstances. Process Reconstruction (US Military, BP) replays events chronologically — easy to follow but prone to "running logs"; Result-Oriented (Lenovo, Google) evaluates directly against goals — efficient but requires team consensus. Both serve the ultimate purpose of learning and improvement.

🔄

Process Reconstruction

Replays events on a timeline — ideal for new teams needing mutual understanding; clear but can become verbose

🎯

Result-Oriented

Evaluates performance against goals — ideal for mature teams in series reviews; focused but requires high consensus

ANSWER

The Review Facilitator — The Key to Successful Team Review

Professional, well-executed facilitation is the critical factor in team review success — the facilitator's core duty is to distinguish "process" from "content," managing team interaction rather than providing answers. Facilitators must cultivate a trusting, open dialogue environment, maintain objectivity and neutrality, believe in the team's collective power, stimulate thinking through questioning, and transform conflict into constructive creation. Huawei, Yili, and CR Power have proven the value of professional facilitation.

"Three Stages Nine Steps™" — The Complete Team Review Framework

Dr. Qiu Zhaoliang's "Three Stages Nine Steps™," developed through extensive practice, covers Meticulous Preparation, Effective Facilitation, and Thorough Follow-Through — it is the standard operating guide for team review. Stage 1 (Prepare): Plan the approach, organize the meeting, prepare materials in advance. Stage 2 (Facilitate): Open the session, conduct structured discussion, close properly. Stage 3 (Follow Through): Share results, drive implementation, evaluate and improve.
