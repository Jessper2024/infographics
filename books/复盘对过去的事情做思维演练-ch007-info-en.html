Fupan · Chapter 7 「Steps of Fupan」

From Lenovo's 4-step method to the expanded 8-step framework — a complete operational guide for transforming fupan from "gut feeling" into a disciplined process

Fupan must follow a structured sequence of steps — the steps serve as both process assurance and a guide enabling every participant to contribute effectively. Lenovo distilled fupan into 4 steps (Review Goals → Evaluate Results → Analyze Causes → Summarize Patterns). For stronger practical applicability, this chapter expands them into 8 steps: ① Review Goals ② Compare Results ③ Narrate Process ④ Self-Analyze ⑤ Group Questioning ⑥ Summarize Patterns ⑦ Verify with Cases ⑧ Archive Fupan. Each step assigns clear roles and responsibilities, transforming fupan into a tool for collaborative participation and collective thinking.

02
INPUT PHASE
Review Goals & Compare Results: Clarify Direction · Identify Gaps
Goals are the yardstick of success — display them visibly so every participant can always see them, keeping the direction correct. When reviewing goals, ask: Is this what we truly wanted? Did we mistake the means for the goal? (e.g. "buy a plane ticket" vs. "arrive at the client on time"). The purpose of result comparison isn't measuring how big the gap is — it's identifying where the gap appears and asking "why."
🎯

Key Points for Goal Review

Emphasize quantitative attributes
Identify the "true goal" not "means"
Display goals on whiteboard/screen
Keep them visible to everyone

📊

Five Result Comparison Types

= Match: result equals goal
> Exceed: result surpasses goal
< Fall short: result below goal
Newly added: unplanned outcomes
Disappeared: planned but zero result

Insurance agent case: Planned to sell pension + critical illness insurance. Result: critical illness exceeded (doubled coverage), pension "disappeared," and children's education insurance was newly added. The comparison table instantly reveals both the "entry" and "exit" of the entire engagement.
03
CORE ANALYSIS
Narrate Process & Self-Analyze: Reconstruct Facts · Examine Yourself
Process narration builds a shared knowledge foundation for all participants — "writing itself is thinking." The recommended method is "reading from the script" — documenting the process in writing. Self-analysis requires distinguishing three tiers of controllability and asking different questions for each tier, achieving "inward-seeking" — examining your own thinking and actions.
🎛️
Controllable
Fully within your control
Both time and quality
Did you do your very best?
At least meet the planned target?
⚙️
Semi-Controllable
Partially within your control
Time or quality partly external

Uncontrollable

At most: communicate & coordinate
Did you communicate in advance?
Did you follow up on progress?

Core of Self-Analysis

Find "what you didn't give your best"

Liu Chuanzhi: "Summarize lessons through fupan — especially failures must be taken seriously, think it through without sparing yourself any mercy. If you and your colleagues are truly open, able to dissect your own shortcomings ruthlessly, repeatedly conducting fupan is the path to greater capability."
04
COLLECTIVE WISDOM
Group Questioning & Summarize Patterns: Break Limitations · Distill True Insight
Group questioning breaks the bounds of individual insight — different people naturally notice different information; combining their intelligence yields more comprehensive and reliable understanding. Pattern summarization is the most important part of fupan, but beware: correlation does not equal causation. "After this" does not mean "because of this." Patterns summarized too hastily are likely false.
💬

Group Questioning Essentials

List questions and explore systematically
Identify key success factors
Find root causes of failure
Explore conditions and boundaries of each possibility

⚠️

Three Pitfalls of Pattern Summarization

Grasshopper fallacy: cut legs → won't jump → "ears are on legs"
Car-wash jinx: "it always rains after I wash my car"
Skinner's pigeons: postures unrelated to food
Root cause: mistaking correlation for causation

⚠ Critical Reminder: Don't Jump to Conclusions Too Quickly
Liu Chuanzhi, reflecting on competing with Dell: they thought it was fair competition, but in reality the competitor was "swimming in a full military uniform" while they were only "in swim trunks." The way to avoid false patterns: ask the question behind the question, find the logic behind the logic.
05
VERIFY & ARCHIVE
Verify with Cases & Archive Fupan: Validate Universality · Codify Knowledge Assets
Patterns must be universal — they must validate not only in one case but in others as well. Case verification tests patterns for applicability; archiving transforms insights into codified knowledge, fixed in text for dissemination and reference. Lenovo has accumulated 240+ fupan documents over 10 years. Archiving lets those who didn't participate in the fupan still grasp the patterns and insights, avoiding detours.
✅

Case Verification Principles

Same industry, same type of case
Verify best-selling products with unsuccessful-product conclusions
Verify successes with failure conclusions
Can explain prior cases → inclusiveness test

📁

Archive File Format

Fupan date · Event name

Knowledge management → preserve fupan wisdom

Haidilao, You Can't Learn It vs. Steve Jobs biography: Apply conclusions from fupan of unsuccessful business books → verify against two bestsellers → derive the pattern: book publishing needs great authors with exclusive content. Readers should skip "compiled" books — time has a cost, and the opportunity cost of reading a low-value book is significant.
💡 Core Insight
The 8 steps of fupan form a complete cognitive cycle. Review Goals to set direction → Compare Results to find gaps → Narrate Process to build consensus → Self-Analyze to seek internal causes → Group Questioning to gather wisdom → Summarize Patterns to distill insight → Verify with Cases to test universality → Archive Fupan to solidify assets. Every step is an indispensable cognitive link. Liu Chuanzhi said: "Ruthlessly dissect yourself, repeatedly conduct fupan — that is how capability grows." Most importantly — be factual, spare no feelings, and make fupan an organizational habit and culture.
