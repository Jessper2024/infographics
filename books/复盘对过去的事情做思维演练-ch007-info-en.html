Fupan · Preface — Master the Art of Review

SCQA · Core Insight We are often trapped by our own experiences, habits, education, and culture — locked in mental blind spots without realizing it. Without an outsider's perspective, are we doomed to sing "yesterday's songs" forever? Fupan offers a path to learn from ourselves and our past: Review → Reflect → Explore → Elevate, creating a continuous upward spiral.

Shaohua took over a university cafeteria stall selling meal combos. Despite a captive customer base, business was dismal. His friend Yajie, a consultant, spent two days observing and uncovered three fatal blind spots: sticking to the original menu design, failing to scale the hit dish (salt-fried pork), and recycling leftovers that locked the menu. Shaohua's problem is everyone's problem — locked in by past decisions without realizing it. Liu Chuanzhi introduced and championed fupan at Lenovo: a term borrowed from Go strategy, now a business methodology of re-examining past actions through four stages, creating a self-reinforcing improvement loop. Fupan is ranked first among Lenovo's three core methodologies.

Blind Spots
Three Fatal Blinds: Why Shaohua's Business Failed

02
Definition
What Is Fupan? From Go Strategy to Business Methodology
Fupan is the most important method for Go players to grow their skill: after a game, replay every move on the board — keep what worked, explore better moves for what didn't. Liu Chuanzhi brought this into business: mentally re-examine past actions to find what went right and what went wrong.

Four Stages
The Fupan Spiral: Learning by Doing, Doing by Learning
Fupan consists of four progressive stages: Review goals & process → Reflect on causes → Explore patterns → Elevate ability. The first three stages are the fupan process; the last stage is the fupan result. Apply what you gain to the next task, then fupan again — forming a continuous Review → Improve → Review → Improve upward spiral.

Fupan in Action
If Shaohua Had Fupan, He Wouldn't Need an Outsider
Yajie's diagnosis for Shaohua was essentially a complete fupan demo: Review (sales fell short) → Reflect (menu locked, no scaling, leftovers loop) → Explore (market feedback daily, drop the unpopular, keep the popular) → Elevate (in about one month, identify the optimal three-meat-three-veg combo). If Shaohua had mastered fupan, he could self-examine through these four steps — discovering problems, finding solutions, and solving them without external help.

05
Enterprise Fupan
Lenovo's Fupan: From Battling Dell to Acquiring IBM PC
Fupan helped Lenovo safely navigate every major challenge: confronting international giants, acquiring IBM's PC division, and global expansion. In 2001, Liu Chuanzhi formally introduced fupan, establishing project teams, organizing reviews, and producing fupan reports. Over ten years, Lenovo accumulated 240+ fupan documents. Fupan played a crucial role in Lenovo becoming China's #1, acquiring IBM PC, and becoming the world's largest PC maker.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Fupan is the ordinary person's weapon against being locked in by the past and by themselves. It requires no genius perspective — just the discipline to push through four stages: Review → Reflect → Explore → Elevate. As Professor Zhao Xiao said: Fupan is a highly effective method of "learning while doing, doing while learning." Dear reader, master fupan.
