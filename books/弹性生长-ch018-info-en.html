🔍 Deep Dive
Parts 1 & 2 Recap: What We've Already Understood
Part 1 dissected macroeconomic fundamentals—the end of the golden 40 years, real estate entering a divergence era, the internet shifting from scale wars to cash-flow battles. Part 2 dove into population and cities—bottom-up population flow, tier-1 megaregions growing larger, stagnation as the norm, genius far outweighing numbers. Building on all this, Part 3 must answer: now that we see clearly, what should we personally do? Which efforts are worth investing in, and which are just meaningless rat-race churn?
The Core Mindset Shift: From Anxiety to Judgment
Too many people live in anxiety—worrying about housing prices, unemployment, demographics. But anxiety solves nothing. The author's core methodology: instead of worrying, build judgment. Judge where prices will rise, which industries still have opportunities, which skills won't be automated. Judgment comes from understanding trends—and trends aren't mysterious. They're embedded in the patterns we've already decoded: the end of the golden 40 years, the micro-growth era, second urbanization.
What Kind of \"Hustle\" Actually Matters
The rat race hurts not because of effort itself, but because effort is directed wrong—squeezing with everyone else onto the same narrow path. Meaningful hustle means: choosing a rising track, using non-linear growth logic to persist to the inflection point, building irreplaceable professional barriers. For example: instead of competing with 10,000 people for one civil service slot, go deep in AI, new energy, or chip technology frontiers. It's not about not working hard—it's about not working hard in the wrong place.
Key Questions the Following Chapters Will Answer
Why are ordinary people more likely to succeed in entrepreneurship than rich second-generation heirs? Why does wealth inheritance so often fail? What kind of education is truly valuable? How do you build irreplaceability in your career? These seemingly separate questions share one underlying logic: abundant resources don't equal success; what matters is correct trend judgment and the capability built from working your way up from the bottom. The upcoming chapters will dissect each.
The Ultimate Mindset of Elastic Growth
The book's title is the answer—elastic growth. Not linear growth (10% annually), not explosive growth (overnight wealth), but staying flexible in uncertainty: not fixating on a single path, adjusting direction based on environmental shifts, acknowledging being ordinary while continuously improving, staying at the table. The golden age is over, but those with resilience will always find their niche. This book aims to help readers build that resilience.
💡 Key Takeaway

The book enters Part 3, shifting from "understanding the problems" to "seeing the direction." The key transition: stop asking "why is this happening" and start asking "what should I do now." Trend judgment matters ten thousand times more than blind effort—persist on the right track until the non-linear inflection point; on the wrong track, the harder you hustle, the worse it gets. The essence of elastic growth: stay at the table, continuously adapt, acknowledge being ordinary but keep improving. Don't wait until you're fully prepared—by the time you are, the opportunity has already passed.
