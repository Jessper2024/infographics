弹性生长 · Ch.2
「Ordinary People Succeed by Accumulating Small Victories」

Escape the Comparison Trap and Go Further with 80% Effort

📖 Chapter Overview

As a ‘small-town exam warrior,’ the author found that even after exceeding expectations by settling in Beijing, stress didn’t disappear. The roots of pain: an ingrained ‘scarcity mindset’ that sacrifices the present, and endless comparison that keeps you perpetually dissatisfied. The solution: lower targets, feed confidence with small wins, give 80% effort to work and 20% to living.

📊 Key Metrics
Feeding Small Victories
Complete small goals to build confidence; avoid learned helplessness
Avoid the Comparison Trap
Comparison is adults’ primary pain source; you’re always in the middle
80% Effort Rule
Reserve 20% for joy and happiness to sustain the long run
Trickle Accumulation
Reading, walking, travel—low immediate pleasure, massive long-term foundation
🔍 Deep Dive
The Shepherd’s Scar and Sacrificing the Present
A generation was raised with ‘study hard or herd sheep,’ etching a scarcity scar into their mental firmware. They sacrificed all quality of life. By the time they could finally live, they were too old to care. This isn’t uniquely Chinese—postwar Europeans share it too.
7,000 Yuan Bonus vs. Thirty Thousand
The author was thrilled with a 7,000-yuan bonus until his buddy showed three fingers—30,000. Years later he surpassed his old boss but still feels resentment. That buddy eventually quit: anyone else could surpass him, but not the author—his ‘anchor point.’
Old Beijingers’ Wisdom
Beijingers have seen it all—emperors fell, warlords rose and fled, Manchu princes groveled. So old Beijingers stay cheerful on 3,000 yuan a month. They’ve witnessed towers rise, banquets thrown, and public executions. That equanimity is worth learning.
The Elite Coach’s Goal-Splitting Method
An elite coach summed up his job: break huge goals into slightly challenging mini-goals, then constantly correct small issues. Confidence and willpower are trained—they need to be ‘fed’ through achieving small targets. Too-high goals → learned helplessness → can’t even handle small ones.
Embracing Your 80% Life
Don’t give 100%—best is 80%, leaving 20% for life’s joys. Sacrificing your entire life for a goal usually means losing both. When economies stagnate, life goes on. Few go bankrupt from inflation; most go bankrupt trying to beat it.
💡 Key Takeaway

The secret for ordinary people isn’t setting high goals—it’s accumulating small victories that feed confidence. Accept you’re ordinary, use 80% effort for the long haul, and don’t let comparison steal your joy. Big things and small things aren’t that different—all big things must be broken into small steps.
