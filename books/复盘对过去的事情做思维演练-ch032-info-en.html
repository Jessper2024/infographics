Fupan · Ch.32 The Narrator (敘述人)

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer Fupan rests on the Narrator's account — but if the Narrator hides or fabricates out of fear, fupan is built on false information. How should the Narrator view fupan, recount events truthfully, and answer questioning with an open mind? Through two attitudes (detachment + objectivity), three standards (truth + completeness + detail), and two requirements (receptivity + honesty).

The Narrator is the foundation of fupan. The entire process and content rest on the Narrator's account and answers. The Narrator is the person — besides the key leader — who gains the most from fupan. The Narrator's attitude directly determines the atmosphere — if they harbor reservations, no amount of Facilitator effort may achieve the fupan's goals.

Core Attitude
Detachment: View the Fupan from Outside Yourself
Detachment means distinguishing the self observing in the fupan from the self who acted. See the participating self as if it were another person — using another person's eyes and perspective to examine the self who acted. This allows calm engagement, even ruthless self-dissection. Self-reflection is the present self dialoguing with the past self — the thinking self conversing with the acting self.

🧘 Xunzi

I examine myself three times daily

🏛️ Socrates

"Team fupan has more people helping you examine — be grateful."
02
Core Attitude
Objectivity: Observe as Is, Add No Inference
Objectivity is the natural result of detachment. Standing from a "guest" perspective, like an outsider observing reality as it is — adding no inference, avoiding emotional interference. See the mountain as the mountain, see the water as the water — no concealment, no embellishment. With detachment and objectivity, situation recreation and iterative reasoning have a solid mindset foundation.

Narration Criteria
Three Narration Standards: Truth, Completeness, Detail
Truth is the first requirement in narrating events. False information leads to opposite results — a wrong starting point determines a wrong ending. What obstructs truth is "fear" — fear that one's actions and thinking appear naive to others, fear of being judged incompetent. Beyond truth: comprehensiveness (cross-sectional, all aspects) and completeness (longitudinal, start to finish). Detail makes events fuller, narration more relatable and credible.
✅ Truth
→
Requirement on information nature. Truthfulness and fact-seeking always paramount. Must not hide or fabricate out of fear. Detail makes truth even truer.
✅ Complete + Full
→
Information classification method. Complete = cross-sectional, no aspect missed. Full = longitudinal, start to finish. Ensures no information gaps.
✅ Detail
→
Information presentation technique. Makes information more perceivable, more memorable. But must track logic between details — don't drown in the sea of details.
04
Answering Attitude
Receptive Answers: Empty Yourself, Welcome Questions
Do not view questions as interrogation — view them as shared exploration. Never guess at others' motives — once you guess motives, you interpret words through a conspiracy lens and naturally enter self-defense mode, closing your mind and blocking information flow. The "commitment and consistency" psychological principle: anything involving our own choices and decisions becomes immune to criticism — the most likely response is immediate attack, defensiveness, and justification.
"Lu Xun, On Human Nature: Even unnamed sores, when growing on a Chinese person, become 'red like peach blossoms; festering, sweet as cream.'"

Celebrate challenging questions — the more challenging, the more they advance everyone's thinking and progress.
05
Moral Requirement
Honesty Without Pretense: Roles Are Interchangeable
Be honest. Don't pretend. If you don't understand, figure it out — don't feign understanding. The roles of Questioner and Narrator are not fixed — the Narrator can also raise questions about industry information or assumptions, and the Questioner can respond with their knowledge. This yields more comprehensive and accurate understanding for everyone. What harms you, your team, and your growth is "feigning understanding while confused."

🔑 Core Takeaway
The Narrator is the foundation of fupan — without truthful, complete, and detailed narration, fupan is building on sand. Attitude determines everything: detach to view yourself, objectively observe as is. Narrate to three standards (truth, completeness, detail). Answer questions with an emptied mind, honestly without pretense. Overcome the greatest enemy — "fear" — because the true value of team fupan lies precisely in having more people to help you examine and grow.
