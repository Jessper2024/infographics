In 1367, after eliminating rivals Chen Youliang and Zhang Shicheng, Zhu Yuanzhang finally turned to the tyrannical Yuan Dynasty. Buried beneath lay not only the grudge of his family destroyed by Yuan misrule, but the grand mission to expel the Mongols and restore Han China. Adopting Xu Da's strategy—Shandong first, then Henan, then Dadu—Zhu launched one of history's rare south-to-north unification wars. On January 4, 1368, Zhu declared himself emperor in Yingtian, naming the dynasty Ming, era Hongwu. Xu Da and Chang Yuchun took Shandong in three months, broke Henan, and entered Dadu on August 2—Yanyun, lost for 400 years, was recovered. But Mongol general Wang Baobao still commanded 100,000 cavalry in Shanxi. The next great battle loomed.

Strategic Offensive
Dadu Taken: Yanyun Recovered After 400 Years
On January 4, 1368, Zhu Yuanzhang declared himself emperor in Yingtian—a great dynasty was born, lasting 276 years. On August 2, Xu Da's army entered Dadu; the Yuan emperor had fled on July 28. The Yanyun region, lost for 400 years since Shi Jingtang's betrayal, was finally recovered. Zhu mockingly called the fleeing emperor "Yuan Shundi"—"Emperor Who Followed Heaven's Will."
👑

Jan 4, 1368: Emperor

Zhu ascended in Yingtian, named the dynasty "Ming," era "Hongwu." From cowherd Zhu Chongba to Emperor Zhu Yuanzhang—no one could have predicted this ending.

🗺️

Yanyun Recovered

Since Shi Jingtang ceded the 16 Yanyun prefectures, the Central Plains had no northern shield for 400 years. Zhu's northern expedition finally restored this strategic depth.

03
Key Figures
The Mongol Opponent: The Northern Yuan
Mongol cavalry had been the world's most feared military force, with 300m-range composite bows that made direct combat impossible. But by the late Yuan, they had degraded: some could not even ride horses. The elite Asu Army robbed civilians, then fled when rebel forces appeared. However, Wang Baobao—adopted son and nephew of the great general Chaghan—emerged as Ming's most dangerous enemy after reorganizing Yuan forces.

04
Battles
Northern Expedition: Three Phases
The campaign advanced at astonishing speed: October 1367 to August 1368—just ten months. Phase one: Shandong fell. Phase two: Henan—Chang Yuchou forded the Luoshui River at full speed, crushing the 50,000-strong Yuan army. Phase three: Dadu—the Yuan emperor had already fled. The Yanyun region, lost for 400 years, was recovered in a single campaign.

05
Aftermath
Yuan Destroyed, Northern Yuan Remains
The Yuan Dynasty as a national regime ended—but Mongol military power was far from destroyed. The Yuan emperor fled to Shangdu, forming the "Northern Yuan" with forces still holding Shanxi, Gansu, Liaodong, and Yunnan. Wang Baobao in Shanxi with 100,000 cavalry remained Ming's greatest threat. Destroying a ruling house is easier than eradicating its military capacity—the war was far from over.

🔑 核心结论
Zhu's revenge advanced with precision and speed: adopting Xu Da's Shandong-first strategy, the Northern Expedition launched in October 1367. Dadu fell on August 2, 1368—just ten months. The Yuan Dynasty as a national regime ended, but the Northern Yuan remained with Wang Baobao's 100,000 cavalry in Shanxi and 200,000 under Nahachu in Liaodong. The war against Mongol military power would continue for two more decades.
