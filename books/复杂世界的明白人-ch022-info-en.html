Clear Thinker in a Complex World · Ch.22 "How We Caught Up Despite Once-Severe Brain Drain"

Rising Environments Forge Strong People · Mass Selection Never Runs Dry Over a decade ago, top talent was streaming toward the West in droves. The author felt a deep chill—"this country is finished." But circumstances improved far more than expected. Talent is not a static stock—it flows from rising environments. China's German-model education conducts mass selection from a giant talent pool. After 2010, a return wave emerged as domestic conditions matured. Today R&D spending is on track to match America. Every difficulty may prove to be a reward.

This chapter uses talent as a lens to examine China's structural reversal from "brain drain anxiety" to "accelerating talent return." The core logic chain: rising environments automatically forge strong people → German-model education mass-selects vast talent → market combat evolves talent into top-tier practitioners → domestic conditions mature after 2010 → R&D investment closes in on America's → talent has a path home. This is not simple patriotic narrative—it is systemic analysis grounded in environment-talent co-evolution, educational model comparison, and the economics of emigration.

01
Core Principle
Talent Is Fluid: Rising Environments Shape Strong People Who Then Push the Environment Higher
Environment and individuals co-evolve. A rising environment shapes strong people—those who grow in brutal competition transform "experience into strategy and mental toughness." Ordinary people explode with growth; capable people iterate even faster. Conversely, top talent entering stable environments for screw-tightening jobs quickly "become screws" themselves—their brains calcify, they turn conservative, they stop taking risks. Overseas elites become corporate cogs; domestic top graduates face the same risk. But the key insight: even if capable people leave, as long as the organization keeps advancing, that progress itself forges strong people. Masters emerge endlessly from the ranks.

02
Educational Foundation
German-Model "Mass Selection" Education: 1.4 Billion Base, an Inexhaustible Talent Pool
China chose the German model under enormous fiscal pressure, and laid the foundation for everything. Global education splits into two types. German model: equal education rights → standardized training and selection → uniform standards like the military. British model: unleash natural gifts → geniuses stand out → ordinary people do their own thing. The German model suits late-developing nations. It is like a marathon: the fast and durable break away; the slower runners stay in the race, which still beats not running. East Asian nations largely adopted this model. The biggest benefit: a massive population base selects massive pools of outstanding talent. Chinese youth literacy and scientific literacy rank high globally. This vast talent pool enters market combat and evolves into the next generation of top-tier talent.
🇩🇪

German Model (China's Choice)

Equal access · uniform standards · mass selection. High cost but ideal for late-industrializing nations. 1.4B base screens vast talent.

🇬🇧

British Model (Elite Path)

Unleash gifts · personalized development. Suits leading nations cultivating genius, but cannot mass-produce technical workers industry needs.

03
Combat Evolution
Market Combat Is the Ultimate Filter: Business and Politics Now Match the West—Tech Catch-Up Is Just a Matter of Time
Entrepreneurs and politicians matter more than scientists. Technology that cannot transform into market-ready products is often worthless. China's business and political sophistication does not lag behind the West. Tech lags only because the prior mission was catching up, not innovating—first eat, then prosper, then everything else. After 2008, conditions improved and future planning began. China laid vast internet infrastructure, naturally spawning the world's most combat-capable internet companies. The telecom explosion produced Huawei. The world's largest phone maker, Samsung, barely sells in China—proof that domestic tech content rises higher each year.

04
Comparative Mirror
The Economics of Emigration: Overseas Earning Channels Are Narrow—Small Populations Limit Business
Among those who sold homes and emigrated between the 1990s and 2010, very few thrived. Most drive cabs or run corner stores. The slightly better-off manage construction crews or run immigration agencies. Overseas Chinese populations are large—they need many people serving each other—but small total populations mean "some businesses simply cannot exist," further capping wealth potential. Chinese women overwhelmingly work as retail clerks; men cluster in restaurants and supermarkets. The harshest description: "These people didn't look at prices back home—abroad they penny-pinch. Few earning channels, money drains eventually." PhD migrants do okay but hit a glass ceiling—mid-level common, upper-level rare.
✈️

Early Emigrants (Before 2010)

Most drive cabs, run shops, manage crews. Lived large back home, penny-pinch abroad. Few earning paths, assets drain.

🎓

Highly Educated (PhDs)

Do fine anywhere but face a clear glass ceiling. Mid-level common, upper-level rare. Career path visible from day one.

05
Trend Reversal
The Post-2010 Return Wave: Domestic R&D Conditions Mature—"Chokepoints" Ignite Self-Reliance
After 2010, a "returner" cohort split off from overseas students—they went abroad precisely to learn and come back. In 2018, returning students grew 8% (+38,500). Chinese commercial companies began absorbing research talent. In 2004, when the author finished his biology PhD, not a single domestic company could take him. Now conditions are mature. The deeper benefit of expanding R&D: it increases the number of technical workers earning ¥100K–300K—the very group that drives domestic demand. Recent "chokepoint" pressure is not a long-term negative. It dispels illusions and sparks determination toward self-reliance across academia and industry.Tech breakthroughs aren't just about technology—they are about life and death. Cardiac stents dropping from over ¥10,000 to a few hundred yuan saves more lives.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The reversal of brain drain came not from slogans but from environment-talent co-evolution. ① Rising environments automatically forge strong people—progress itself produces talent. ② German-model mass-selection education provides an inexhaustible talent pool. ③ Market combat is the ultimate filter—business and politics now match the West. ④ After 2010, domestic conditions matured, R&D investment closed in on America's, and talent found its path home. Seventy years of hard-won progress proves: looking back, every difficulty was a reward.
