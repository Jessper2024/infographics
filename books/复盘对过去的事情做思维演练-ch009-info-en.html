Fupan · Chapter 9 — Lenovo's Fupan Methodology

SCQA · Core Insight Why did Liu Chuanzhi talk about fupan in every interview? Because fupan helped Lenovo defeat Dell, acquire IBM's PC division, and become the world's #1 PC maker. Fupan is not Liu's personal preference — it is an institutionalized weapon, formally established in 2001, producing 240+ documents over ten years, and validated as Lenovo's most important methodology. And Lenovo isn't the only company using it.

At the 2011 Legend Star Entrepreneurship Forum, Liu Chuanzhi once again emphasized: learning ability matters — you must know how to fupan. This wasn't his first time, nor his second or third. Since 2009, practically every media interview featured fupan. Fupan can be seen behind every major Lenovo milestone: in 2003, facing Dell's aggressive expansion, fupan revealed the real issue wasn't direct sales vs. distribution but superior process and efficiency, leading to a decisive turnaround in 2004. In 2009, the $200M buyback of the mobile phone business also came from fupan-driven insights into Apple and the industry. Fupan evolved from habit into the top pillar among Lenovo's three methodologies, and has been independently validated by entrepreneurs like Feng Lun, Li Dongsheng, Zhou Hongyi, and Sun Taoran.

But through fupan, Lenovo realized the real issue: the competition wasn't about direct sales vs. distribution — it was about whose process was more excellent, who was more efficient, who captured more customer groups. After adjusting strategy accordingly, Lenovo turned the tables in 2004. Dell never threatened again.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Fupan is not one leader's personal preference — it is a universal methodology systemized by Lenovo over ten years, validated by 240+ documents, and repeatedly proven effective by industry leaders across different sectors.
