Fupan · Chapter 9「Liu Chuanzhi's Cycle: The PDF Method of Doing Things」

SCQA Analysis: Most people only focus on "getting it done," but truly "doing it well" requires preview before execution + review after execution — doing the same thing three times.

BLUF: The complete method of doing things = Sandbox Preview (P) → Execute (D) → Review (F). The PDF cycle lets you do the same thing three times — Preview builds a contingency plan, Do provides actual experience, FuPan allows retrospective reexamination — breaking it apart, chewing thoroughly, fully digesting experience and mastering patterns, enabling continuous spiral ascent in capability. This chapter contrasts PDF with PDCA (Deming Cycle), clarifying the core value lies in "doing three times" rather than "doing in steps."

Core Framework
The PDF Triple Cycle: P(Preview) → D(Do) → F(FuPan)
PDF — "Preview—Do—FuPan" — is the methodology Liu Chuanzhi introduced into the process of getting things done. It lets you process the same thing three times: sandbox preview to establish a contingency plan, execution to gain real experience, and FuPan to reexamine retrospectively. By tearing apart, breaking down, and chewing thoroughly, you extract as much experience, truth, and pattern as possible from a single event, making the entire situation visible to the doer in full detail.

Pre-Action Preview
P · Sandbox Preview: Run It Once in Your Mind
Sandbox previewing originated from military red-blue team simulations, exploring possibilities on the sandbox to clarify strengths and weaknesses, discover strategic and tactical problems, and find paths to victory. When major events approached, Zeng Guofan would retreat alone to the attic, light incense, and quietly contemplate various possible scenarios and their development paths — comparing, discerning, probing underlying logic, and ultimately determining his response strategy. The essence of sandbox previewing: reducing unfamiliarity, increasing familiarity, maintaining a holistic perspective, and knowing the situation inside out.

In-Action Execution
D · Execution: Mastery Lies Beyond the Act Itself
Execution is often the focal point — the popularity of concepts like "execution power" and "details determine success or failure" reflects dissatisfaction with execution itself. But the key to doing something well does not lie in execution alone. Mastery lies beyond the act itself — execution quality depends on the two surrounding phases: the capability preparation built by prior sandbox previewing, and the pattern recognition extracted through subsequent FuPan. Without the support of these two rings, execution becomes a candle in the wind.

04
Post-Action Review
F · FuPan: Return to the Scene, Discover New Possibilities
FuPan moves in the opposite direction from sandbox previewing. Having already previewed and executed, you now look back at the entire process — asking questions to find problems, identify causes, and arrive at answers. After the Gulf War, the U.S. military returned to the "Battle of 73 Easting" site. Soldiers, relying on memory, diaries, and video footage, reconstructed a 3D simulation. Future cadets can enter the virtual battlefield and modify assumptions: What if the enemy had infrared night vision? What if missile range was doubled? FuPan not only redoes it — it creates new ideas and hypotheses.
"Soldiers walked back and forth across the sun-baked ground, arguing heatedly about who hit whom. Lasers and radar mapped the digital terrain... Next, future cadets can set hypothetical conditions in the simulation system and battle the Iraqi Republican Guard." — Kevin Kelly, Out of Control
05
Method Comparison
PDF vs PDCA: Three Times vs. Step-by-Step
PDCA (Deming Cycle) comprises different stages of the same process — Plan→Do→Check→Action — four stages of one process; unresolved issues enter the next PDCA cycle. PDF, in contrast, does the same thing three times — Preview→Do→FuPan — processing the same event three separate times. PDF enables spiral ascent in capability with each iteration, until execution can eventually align perfectly with the sandbox preview plan, achieving "planned precision strikes."
