How to Trade in Stocks · Ch.10 "Livermore's Trading Chart Manuscripts and Explanations"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer The market always moves in trends, with every long-short transition leaving traces in price movements — this chapter presents 16 complete chart manuscripts of U.S. Steel from March 1938 to February 1940. Most traders get lost in intraday noise, waiting for external "good news" before acting. How can systematic recording reduce complex price movements to actionable trading signals? The answer: using a six-column table as the framework, let the market tell you when to act through price behavior itself.

This chapter is the live-fire demonstration of the Livermore Market Analysis Method — using 23 months of real U.S. Steel price action, it presents the complete process from a downward trend to an upward trend, through consolidation phases, to a full bull-bear cycle. The 16 manuscripts prove three eternal truths: first, market action itself is always the first and only signal — steel stocks warned 4 months before the news broke; second, price patterns repeat, making it more important to identify pivotal psychological moments than to chase volatility; third, composite prices effectively filter out false signals. Maintain records personally, and let the market speak to you through price behavior.
01
Recording System
The Six-Column Recording Method — Livermore's Trend Dashboard
The six-column classification breaks down each leading stock's price movement into six states: Secondary Rally, Natural Rally, Upward Trend, Downward Trend, Natural Reaction, Secondary Reaction — forming a complete trend monitoring system. Upward and downward trends use ink because they represent clear direction; the remaining four columns use pencil because they are merely adjustment phases.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The essence of Livermore's six-column recording method: reduce price movements to Upward Trend (black ink), Downward Trend (red ink), and four adjustment states (pencil), with 6 points (single) or 12 points (composite) as the reversal threshold, and 3-point breakout as the confirmation signal. The 16 manuscripts prove three eternal truths: first, market action itself is always the first and only signal; second, price patterns repeat — identifying pivotal psychological moments matters more than chasing volatility; third, composite prices effectively filter out false signals. Maintain records personally, and let the market speak to you through price behavior — this is the most precious legacy Livermore left for traders.
