01
ESSENCE
What Is Fupan? Three Keywords
Fupan means learning from personal experience — one of the most important forms of adult learning. Originally a Go term, Fupan refers to replaying a finished game to analyze gains and losses. In business, it means learning from past work to summarize experience, build capability, and improve performance.
🔍 Personal Experience
Your own events are the primary source of information

⏳ Past

📖 Learning

"Fupan means sitting down after a task and reviewing the whole process — what we planned, what went wrong, why it didn't work. Next time, those lessons are absorbed." — Liu Chuanzhi, Lenovo Founder
02
DISTINCTION
Fupan ≠ Work Summary
Fupan differs fundamentally from work summaries: it is learning-oriented, process-driven, and team-based. Work summaries highlight achievements and tie to performance reviews. Fupan follows a structured logic, encourages collective reflection, and does not assign blame.
📋

Work Summary

Highlights achievements; no fixed template; linked to performance reviews; usually individual perspective; deep reflection not required

🔄

Fupan

Learning-oriented; structured flow (Goal → Gap → Cause → Lesson → Action); collective team reflection; no blame assigned

03
CLARIFICATION
Five Common Misconceptions
Fupan is not case study, not simple recall, not a problem-solving tool, not outdated, and does not stifle innovation. Many misunderstand Fupan, preventing them from using this powerful method correctly.
❌
Myth 1Review others' work
→ That's case study, not Fupan
❌
Myth 2Post-project review
→ Simple recall ≠ systematic Fupan
❌
Myth 3Problem-solving method
→ Fupan transcends problem-fixing
❌
Myth 4Too fast for Fupan
→ Fast iteration needs Fupan even more
⚠️ Myth 5: Fupan kills innovation?
Proper Fupan digs into success factors and root causes with an open mind — it actually sparks innovation. Google, Tencent, Alibaba, and others actively use Fupan to drive rapid iteration.

💡 KEY TAKEAWAY
Fupan is not simple review — it is a structured learning method built on personal experience. It differs fundamentally from work summaries and requires clearing up common misconceptions to be used correctly.
