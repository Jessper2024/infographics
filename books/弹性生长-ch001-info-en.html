Building a Healthy Worldview and Life Expectations

📖 Chapter Overview

This chapter explores a fundamental question: how should we view the imperfections of our nation and society? The author points out that many people’s patriotism is built on “comparison”—deriving hollow pride by belittling other countries, a mindset that reality will eventually shatter. A healthy worldview means acknowledging society’s flaws while believing problems can be solved—because that is exactly how progress has always been made, through pragmatic problem-solving.

🔍 Deep Dive
Unhealthy Patriotism: Comparative Pride
Many base patriotism on comparisons—‘America divided, Europe declining, Japan-Korea involution.’ Once reality hits—tough job market, low pay, injustice—their worldview collapses, oscillating between patriotism and cynicism.
The Haidian Mom Epiphany
A mom thought her kid knowing 200 words was brilliant—until meeting a Haidian mom whose kid knew 3,000. She later realized: love your child because they’re yours, not a genius. Same for your country.
The Power of Constructive Confidence
When a violent incident sparked nationwide outrage, a colleague was actually glad—‘Our hometown will now be the safest place for barbecue.’ No pressure, no improvement. Mature citizens want problems exposed so they can be solved.
Seek Truth from Facts: Mao’s Core Insight
When bad things happen, don’t spin them as good or find worse examples abroad. Acknowledge they’re wrong, then fix them. That’s the essence of ‘Seek Truth from Facts.’ All social progress comes from continuous correction.
From Pendulum Swing to Maturity
Youth is the peak of pendulum swings—entering the real world shatters old beliefs. After adjusting, you see: society isn’t perfect but has transformed; many aren’t rich but are striving; each solved problem strengthens confidence.
💡 Key Takeaway

Love a country not because it’s perfect, but because it’s constantly improving. Acknowledge imperfections and solve problems—that’s the true source of confidence. When your faith rests on ‘we will solve everything,’ your worldview grows clearer every day.
