Su Shi Biography · Ch10
"Four Terms as Prefect, Benefiting the People"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer "A Belly Full of Untimeliness" — Driven from court, Su Shi governed four prefectures, creating the Su Causeway, Dongpo Pork, Anle Clinic, and lasting public welfare. Why couldn't Su Shi remain at court after returning to the capital? How did he transform political adversity into concrete achievements in local governance?

In one line: Su Shi's "belly full of untimeliness" made court life impossible — he clashed with Sima Guang over the exemption labor law, mocked Cheng Yi, and faced attacks from two rival cliques. Requesting external assignment, he governed Dengzhou, Hangzhou, Yingzhou, and Yangzhou in succession. Far from factional strife, he delivered monumental achievements: Su Causeway, Dongpo Pork, China's first public hospital, cancellation of exploitative festivals, and tax relief — proving that real governance happens among the people.

01
Court Clashes
"Sima Ox" and Factional War: Attacked from All Sides
Upon returning to court, Su Shi became a firm defender of the exemption labor law from practical experience — only to meet Sima Guang's obstinate refusal. He argued face to face, then stormed home shouting "Sima Ox!" His mockery of Cheng Yi triggered the Luo-Shu factional war, drawing attacks from both the Luo and Shuo cliques. Chao Yun captured it perfectly: "The Scholar has a belly full of untimeliness."
🔴

Exemption Law Clash with Sima Guang

Su Shi argued from practical governance to retain the law. Sima Guang insisted on total abolition. Su Zhe assessed: "He knew the harm but not the benefit of the exemption law."

🟠

Luo-Shu Factional War

After Sima Guang died, Cheng Yi insisted on archaic ritual. Su Shi mocked him, sparking the factional conflict. Luo disciples impeached Su Shi for "slandering the former court."

02
Imperial Trust
Golden Lotus Candles: Empress Dowager's Trust, Emperor's Coldness
Empress Dowager Gao summoned Su Shi privately and revealed his advancement was Emperor Shenzong's dying wish — Shenzong would pause his chopsticks reading Su Shi's memorials, exclaiming "A genius! A genius!" She sent him home with golden lotus candles from the imperial desk. But Su Shi knew the young Emperor Zhezong resented his grandmother's dominance and repeatedly requested external posting. When the Empress Dowager died in September 1094, a storm was inevitable.

03
Hangzhou Legacy
Su Causeway and Anle Clinic: A City Reborn
In Hangzhou, Su Shi achieved three monumental projects: work-relief dredging of West Lake, building the 880-zhàng Su Causeway with six bridges, and founding China's first public hospital "Anle Clinic" — not a single person starved during the famine. During Dragon Boat Festival, residents brought pigs and wine in gratitude. He cooked the pork using his Huangzhou method — "slow fire, little water, patience yields perfection" — distributing it to laborers. Thus Dongpo Pork was born.

04
Wisdom & Mercy
Painted Fans and Abolished Festivals: Governance with Heart
In Yingzhou and Yangzhou, Su Shi demonstrated governance at two scales: macro — blocking a massive wasteful canal project using 5,811 surveying poles of empirical data; micro — resolving a debt lawsuit by painting twenty fans. He abolished an exploitative peony festival consuming 100,000+ peonies yearly. Going incognito among villagers, he compared the 200,000-strong tax-collection apparatus to "tigers and wolves scattered among the people."
🔵

The Painted Fan Case: Art's Compassion

A fan maker couldn't repay debts due to persistent rain. Su Shi painted twenty fans with bamboo, rocks, and poems — they sold out instantly. Debt resolved.

🔵

Abolishing the Peony Festival

Cai Jing's "Ten Thousand Flower Gathering" consumed vast resources and enabled exploitation. Su Shi canceled it outright: "Spoils the scenery, but avoids creating bad karma."

05
Final Post
By the Snow-Wave Stone: Last Command and Eternal Homesickness
Dingzhou was Su Shi's final active post. Facing "border administration so decayed one could hardly bear to look," he disciplined troops, punished corrupt officers, and revived the "Archery Society" militia plan for 30,000 local fighters. But deeper worries loomed — his wife had died, Empress Dowager Gao was gone. Bidding farewell to his brother in the rain, he sensed "white-haired, no knowing when I'll return."

🔑 Key Takeaway
Su Shi's four prefectures reveal a profound truth: real political achievement often happens not at the center of power, but among ordinary people. Besieged by factional warfare at court, he created enduring legacies in local governance — Su Causeway, Anle Clinic, Dongpo Pork. Even with mere days in office (Dengzhou: two memorials in five days), he acted for the people; when wielding real authority (Hangzhou: commanding six prefectures), he applied scientific rigor to flood control and famine relief. The golden lotus candles' glow had barely faded when farewell beneath Dingzhou's parasol tree became forever — Su Shi proved through deeds that an official's worth is measured by what they do for the people, not by rank.
