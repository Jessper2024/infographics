Chapter 7 Epilogue

Five Years of Pain and Hatred · A Generation's Predicament · The Birth of Alpha Star · Guo Juyang in Baishizhou · The Book's Final Resonance

Core Overview Chapter 7 serves as the emotional climax and conclusion of the entire book. The author reviews five years of inner pain and hatred, using autobiographical narrative to reveal the collective predicament of a generation of young Chinese men — imprisoned in schools for 20 years, then pushed into 30-year mortgages as "forced labor." The creation of Alpha Star's automated trading robot (project codename Sirius, first line of code on February 21, 2023) serves as the emotional vehicle, interspersed with memories of Guo Juyang in a Baishizhou rental apartment, and reflections on the fates of Ling Feifei and Xu Jie. The chapter closes with: "Without these young men stepping up to date married women, these married women's lives would be too tragic."

1 A Generation's Collective Predicament

The author uses intense personal emotion to describe the situation of a generation of young Chinese men: locked in schools for 20 years or more, then forced to marry, buy a house, and take on a 30-year mortgage. He likens this to being released from "prison" only to enter "forced labor" — Bao'an propping up prices near Nanshan Science Park, Tangzhen near Zhang Jiang, Xinzhuang near Caohejing, Changping near Haidian, and Yuhang propping up all of Hangzhou.

The author describes seeing Chinese-made industrial products in stores worldwide, hearing white colleagues and ABCs at top AI companies in the Bay Area cry about "can't keep up with Tsinghua graduates," and having to wait until 8 PM to meet friends for dinner in Nanshan Science Park or Zhang Jiang — "Wanting to go out for barbecue and beer with an old friend at 8 PM, and having to apologize and request approval from some damn boss."

"Why is our generation — the most excellent, hardest-working, most capable generation of Chinese men — locked in schools like prisoners for 20 years or more, then married off, forced by some woman to buy a house in 2021, forced to apply for a 30-year mortgage?"

2 Alpha Star: From Vengeance to Creation

Alpha Star's automated trading robot was written entirely by the author, one line of code at a time. The project codename is Sirius (the Dog Star), with the first line of code dated February 21, 2023. The name comes from both Alpha Centauri and Alpha Canis Majoris (the battle star Sirius). The author describes countless nights waking from nightmares in the middle of the night, then opening the computer to write code.

The author poses the question: young Chinese men created Tencent, Alibaba, DJL, CATL, and even the core workforce of Google DeepMind, OpenAI, and Anthropic — why are all the fruits possessed by the "Li Songjians" and the "Guo Juyangs"? This questioning forms the emotional climax of the entire book.

3 Baishizhou: Another Side of Guo Juyang

During Shenzhen's 2022 real estate frenzy, in a rental apartment in Baishizhou Xincun Village, the author and Guo Juyang lay together on the sofa afterward. He told her "I'm so envious of your Shenzhen hukou," and Guo Juyang replied in Chaoyang dialect: "Xin Ye, stop mocking me. How can a Shenzhen hukou compare to your American identity?"

In that moment, the author felt that the person before him was no longer the vulgar woman who traded her body for a mansion, but more like the 14-year-old girl who confessed to him in Chaoyang dialect 14 years ago. Compared to the landlady downstairs and the tea-shop owner across the street who proudly boasted about their Shenzhen properties, Guo Juyang "at least had that one moment where, beyond money, her eyes still aspired to ideals and love — otherwise why would she walk from the Portofino mansion to a Baishizhou rental?"

4 Portraits: Ling Feifei and Xu Jie's Fateful Questions

The author poses literary questions about the fates of Ling Feifei and Xu Jie. In Ling Feifei's 500-million-yuan Tan Gong mansion in Shanghai, did she see the youth she sacrificed for money in the security guard's eight-pack abs? When Xu Jie met her childhood sweetheart in Hunan, did she regret leaving home for Shanghai?

5 The Book's Final Close

The author concludes with the book's final emotional declaration. He knows many will want to criticize him just from seeing the title, but poses a counter-question: "Without these young men stepping up to date married women, these married women's lives would be too tragic! If dating married women were illegal, then this already so corrupt society would be too hopeless!"

This epilogue elevates the book from an operational manual to an emotional indictment of social reality, carrying a generation's fate within personal narrative. From the high school memories in Chapter 1 to the Baishizhou scene in Chapter 7, Guo Juyang serves as the narrative thread running through the entire book, completing an arc from girlhood to married woman to "at least having one moment of aspiring to ideals and love."

Key Takeaways

Chapter 7 is the emotional climax of the book, sublimating personal pain into a generation's collective predicament. The creation of Alpha Star's automated trading robot (codename Sirius, 2023.2.21) becomes the vehicle for transformation from hatred to creation. The scene in the Baishizhou rental where Guo Juyang responds in Chaoyang dialect is the book's most literarily powerful moment. The epilogue closes with "without the young men's action, married women's lives are too tragic," completing the elevation from operational guide to socio-emotional narrative.
