SCQA · Core Insight What drives personal capability growth? Training accounts for only 10%, peer exchange 20% — the decisive 70% is on-the-job practice. Drawing on the review practices of masters like Liu Chuanzhi, Zeng Guofan, and Confucian sage Zengzi, this chapter illuminates the value of personal review. The 70:20:10 rule proves fupan is the core path for adult learning. 01
Why Fupan
Generals Are Forged in Battle — But Fupan Is the Prerequisite
Fupan is the most effective path for personal learning: Ren Zhengfei said "generals aren't taught, they're forged in battle," yet without the habit and ability to review, even fighting many battles won't produce a general. Confucius classified people into four types — those born with knowledge, those who learn, those who learn through hardship, and those who never learn. His core path of "learning to know" is essentially fupan-style reflection.
"My self-assessment: above-average IQ, relatively high EQ… What then is my advantage? Diligence in fupan." — Liu Chuanzhi, Founder of Lenovo
  • Zengzi: "I reflect on myself three times daily" — structured self-reflection
  • Zeng Guofan: habit of quiet reflection after major events
  • Wang Jianlin: embedded fupan into commercial plaza operations
  • Ren Zhengfei: required all employees to "summarize after every battle"

Review Targets
Four Types of Events Worth Reviewing: Focus on Priorities
Not everything needs a review, but four types of events deserve dedicated time: new things (first-time attempts), important matters (high resources, high impact), valuable activities (skill-building opportunities), and underperforming outcomes (deviations and defects) — the latter are often the most valuable learning opportunities.

Operating Methods
Two Personal Fupan Methods: Simple Self-Review and Coach-Guided
Choose based on event complexity: simple events can be processed in 10–15 minutes of self-review; major projects may require half a day or more of systematic review. Alternatively, find a coach trained in fupan facilitation, who uses structured questioning to help you break through mental blind spots.
✍️

Method 1: Simple Self-Review

Estimate time → find undisturbed space → follow fupan steps to reflect and analyze → use a template or the "Yi-Fupan" mini-app. Simple events: 10–15 min. Monthly work: ~1 hour. Annual work: half a day.

👥

Method 2: Find a Coach

The coach stays neutral, focuses on process, and uses structured questions to stimulate deeper thinking. The course "Fupan: Turning Experience into Capability" provides a coaching guide — subordinates, colleagues, or friends can serve as coaches.

05
Key Practices
Five Disciplines: Making Personal Fupan Truly Effective
Fupan isn't complicated, but doing it right requires five disciplines: confront yourself honestly without rationalizing → first rigidly follow the process, then optimize → record key points and review periodically → maintain an open mind and avoid experience traps ("Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish") → make fupan a continuous habit. To avoid experience traps, deeply question whether existing rules and assumptions remain valid, or apply first-principles thinking from fundamentals.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Personal fupan is the shortest path from experience to capability. No complex tools needed, no team coordination required — just be honest with yourself. Follow the four-step review process, timely review new, important, valuable, and underperforming events, and persist until it becomes a habit. Liu Chuanzhi said his advantage is simply "diligence in fupan" — this should be everyone's secret to capability growth.
