Five Cities of Du Fu · Ch.14 "Crossing Qinghai"

Xiahe · Linxia · Lintao · Xining · Qinghai Lake · Golmud

Leaving Zoigê for southern Gansu, Lai detours to Xiahe—seduced by the beauty of the name alone. At Labrang Monastery, he feels Tibetan Buddhism’s profound gravity. In Linxia, an Islamic stronghold, he wakes at 5 AM to the Qur’an’s morning prayer. He makes a special trip to Lintao, where the Tang Dynasty stationed its first permanent frontier army. Passing through Lanzhou for the third time without staying, he heads to Xining. Then he boards China’s highest railway to Golmud, skirting the north shore of Qinghai Lake—‘pale blue, tinged with melancholy.’ Crossing the Ten-Thousand-Zhang Salt Bridge, he arrives at Golmud, a city of outsized buildings like a sci-fi set. Returning by road along the lake’s south shore, he recalls Xue Rengui’s defeat at Dafeichuan: ‘Beneath the green grass of this Qinghai Head lie countless unburied bones.’

01Xiahe & Labrang: Detouring for a Beautiful Name
Seduced by the name ‘Xiahe’—‘a very beautiful place name, with an aquatic image and a sense of distant grace.’ The town sits at the foot of a barren mountain that ‘occupies half the sky like a prehistoric dinosaur.’ At Labrang Monastery in early morning stillness, old lamas in red robes walk about, believers prostrate themselves in worship, Tibetans turn prayer wheels and burn cypress leaves—‘I felt, for the first time, the profound gravity of Tibetan Buddhism.’
02Linxia & Lintao: Qur’an Prayer & Tang Frontier Army
Linxia is a major Islamic center in the northwest, its mosque dome towering. At 5 AM, Lai wakes to the Arabic Qur’an morning prayer—‘a lingering, slightly mournful tone, deeply moving.’ He detours to Lintao, where the Tang Dynasty’s first permanent frontier army farmed military colonies. Today Lintao has only two streets, ‘barren yellow, with the desolation of primordial earth.’ The Tao River, thin and dry, has buried countless garrison soldiers’ bones.
03Qinghai Lake: Melancholy Blue from a Train Window
Boarding China’s highest railway to Golmud. The train skirts Qinghai Lake’s north shore—the water ‘pale blue, tinged with melancholy’—recalling Du Fu’s verse: ‘Do you not see, at Qinghai’s head, since ancient times, white bones lie unburied.’ The train runs less than a hundred meters from the lake: ‘I gazed blankly at this lake until it gradually vanished into the twilight.’ A vow: someday, return to this lakeside, keep company with history’s lonely ghosts.
04Golmud: A Sci-Fi City on the Salt Bridge
Golmud resembles a city from science fiction—‘all objects suddenly swelled several times larger.’ The train crosses the Ten-Thousand-Zhang Salt Bridge over Qarhan Salt Lake, white salt crystals gleaming like a military base. Returning by road along Qinghai’s south shore, passing Black Horse River in pitch darkness at 2 AM—remembering Xue Rengui’s defeat at Dafeichuan, the wars between Tuyuhun and Tibet, ‘beneath the green grass lie countless unburied bones.’
05Travel Flavors: Naked Carp, Crispy Duck & Luzhou Laojiao
In Xining, tasting Qinghai’s famous naked carp—scaleless, tender, boneless. A restaurant owner packs a crispy duck for the Golmud train journey. Onboard, Lai enjoys it with Luzhou Laojiao liquor as the setting sun bathes the Qinghai gobi—‘this was the richest train dinner I ever had.’ Back in Xi’an with two unexpected extra days, he visits every place he missed before. Returning to Hong Kong, lying on his own bed: ‘I suddenly realized I had slept on other people’s beds for nearly two months.’
💡 Key Insight
From Tibetan Buddhism in Xiahe to Islamic morning prayer in Linxia, from Qinghai Lake’s melancholic blue to Golmud’s sci-fi strangeness—Crossing Qinghai is a journey through religions and civilizations. Lai Ruihe measures the Tibetan Plateau’s edge by rail and road, sips Luzhou Laojiao on China’s highest railway, and feels the crystallizing power of the earth’s salt on the Ten-Thousand-Zhang Salt Bridge. The journey’s end is his own bed—‘so clean, so comfortable’—because he has walked too long, too far across this ancient land.
