The Gay Genius · Ch.10
"The Opposition Storm"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer "If it does not rain within ten days, behead me outside the Xuande Gate." A low-ranking gate official named Zheng Xia used a single painting of famine victims as his weapon, ending the era of the Stubborn Prime Minister under the combined assault of the Empress Dowager, natural disasters, and popular unrest. Wang Anshi had the Emperor's full support and the opposition had all been driven from the capital — so why did he still fall? The answer came from three converging forces: the blood of the people, the judgment of heaven, and the will of the palace.

The opposition storm was ignited by three fuses: the Baojia militia revolt, the collapse of Mount Hua, and a prolonged drought. The death blow came from Zheng Xia — a humble gate official who painted a picture of famine victims "chained and cutting trees to earn money to repay government debts" and had it delivered into the palace. The Empress Dowager's pressure and the conversation with the Emperor's younger brother, Prince Qi, completed the siege from within the palace. Wang Anshi was dismissed, it rained — as if heaven rejoiced. But the deeper tragedy: after the new policies were abolished, Lü Huiqing, Deng Wan, and Shu Dan turned around and purged everyone who had ever criticized the government.

01
Eve of the Storm
A Lifeless Court: Great Ministers Scattered Like Stars
When Su Dongpo left the capital with his family, the court was already dead and lifeless. Ouyang Xiu lived in retirement in Fuyang, Sima Guang and Lü Gongzhu idled in Luoyang; Fu Bi had been demoted, and the old minister Lü Hui was dying — all the great ministers of Renzong's reign had been dismissed and scattered to the provinces.
🏚️

Ministers in Exile

Ouyang Xiu retired, Sima Guang idled, Fu Bi stripped of honors. Lü Hui on his deathbed submitted a riddle comparing the reform to a quack doctor prescribing poison.

💬

"Knowing People Is Hard"

Fu Bi said to Zhang Fangping: "Knowing people is hard." His old friend replied: "Knowing him wasn't hard — I worked with him. He overturned every rule." The difficulty lay not in seeing, but in being powerless.

02
Popular Revolt
Baojia Rebellion: Fathers and Sons Wept, Broken Wrists to Escape Conscription
A rebellion erupted near the capital over the Baojia militia law — villagers feared military training meant they'd be sent north to war. Required to provide their own weapons, horrific self-mutilation followed: cutting off fingers and wrists to avoid conscription. Wang Anshi lost his last friend, Han Wei, over this.

03
Heaven's Judgment
Mount Hua Collapse & Drought: Heaven's Verdict on the Stubborn Minister
In 1073, the sacred Mount Hua collapsed, terrifying the Emperor. Then from summer to the following spring, not a drop of rain fell. In the traditional cosmology of "heaven-human resonance," these were not natural disasters — they were heaven's direct judgment on human affairs.
Wang's Response
→ severing the link →
"Droughts and floods are natural calamities. They happened even in the age of Yao and Shun. We just need to continue good governance."
Emperor's Fear
→ troubled conscience →
"That's precisely what I fear — that we are NOT practicing good governance. Even the Empress and the Empress Dowager know about the complaints."
Feng Jing's Retort
→ direct confrontation →
"I have heard the complaints too." Heaven's wrath = Heaven's judgment — Wang could no longer evade.
04
The Death Blow
Zheng Xia's Painting: One Humble Official's Act of Greatness
Zheng Xia, a powerless palace gate official, painted famine victims "chained, cutting trees to repay government debts" and submitted it with a signed pledge: "If it does not rain within ten days, behead me outside the Xuande Gate." The painting was smuggled into the palace as "urgent military intelligence."

Internal Collapse
The Fate of Revolutionaries: Decay After Victory
Su Dongpo named the iron law: "Every revolutionary party displays the greatest unity before gaining power; once in power and having eliminated the opposition, it begins to split through internal strife." After Wang's fall, Huiqing and Deng Wan turned around and purged all critics.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The opposition storm was woven from three threads: the blood of the people (Baojia revolt), the judgment of heaven (Mount Hua collapse + drought), and the will of the palace (Empress Dowager + Prince Qi). Zheng Xia accomplished with a single painting what the entire opposition could not — not because he had more power, but because he was closest to the people. The deeper tragedy after Wang Anshi's fall: the new policies were abolished, but factional strife had taken deep root; the Stubborn Minister was gone, but the treacherous henchmen he promoted remained in power. As Lin Yutang quoted Su Dongpo: the desire to overthrow the powerful brings out humanity's noblest instincts, but the power to rule over others brings out its ugliest side.
