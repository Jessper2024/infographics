01
Habits
Not-to-do List + Micro-habit Dual-Track Method
Habits are a tool that "enables humans to achieve anything" — building them is easier than imagined. Jim Collins was asked by a professor: "If you had only 10 years to live and $2 million, what would you stop doing?" — realizing that deciding what not to do matters more. Just as rotten fruit must be removed before placing fresh ones in a basket, good habits start with eliminating bad ones. Build new habits by breaking them into "ridiculously small" actions, repeating until resistance fades. Haruki Murakami: when unmotivated, just sit at the desk.

Break down until laughably small (e.g., 1 push-up daily) → repeat until zero resistance → gradually increase. Self-control is like spinning plates — one at a time.

02
Diet
Simplicity & Moderation — Foundation of Mental Energy
Those who eat regularly study well. Learners should eat simply and in moderation. In Joseon-era Seonggyungwan, daily breakfast and dinner attendance determined the internal exam score — 300 points qualified you for the civil service exam. Exam village wisdom: "Buy a monthly meal ticket and eat every meal — you will pass." While filming 30 online lectures, the author skipped dinner with only fruit, gaining hours of extra work time nightly. The evolutionary reason: satiety signals "crisis over," lowering alertness and focus automatically.
Eat Simply
→ Principle →
Food as medicine — simple food calms the mind. Spicy food disperses "qi" outward, depleting energy needed for study. Chili was used as chemical weapon during Japanese invasions.
Eat Moderately
→ Principle →
Overeating → satiety → laziness → desire to lie down → "I'll start tomorrow." 1M years of evolution: being full = crisis over = focus drops = survival instinct off.
Historical Proof
→ Evidence →
Franklin at 22 set 13 virtues, first being "Temperance — eat not to dullness." Studies with apes show calorie-restricted individuals visibly younger than those eating freely.
03
Sleep
Three Scientific Methods for Deep Sleep
"Study 4 hours to pass, 5 hours to fail" is outdated — sufficient sleep positively correlates with learning. An Ohio high school delayed start by one hour and grades improved. University of Minnesota study of 9,000 students confirmed this. All-nighters lower IQ by 13 points and halve math accuracy. But oversleeping also degrades brain state — sleep quality = length × depth. Three methods: 4.5h deep sleep, 90-minute cycle, and the "anti-sleep" method.

04
Time
Willpower Is Finite — Priority Determines Success
Willpower is like a smartphone battery — it depletes with use. Prof. Baba Shiv's chocolate cake experiment: students memorizing 7-digit numbers chose unhealthy cake far more than those memorizing 2-digit — their willpower was already exhausted. Israeli prison parole study: judges approved 65% in morning/after breaks, near 0% when fatigued. Therefore, decide priorities before studying and start with the hardest, most important task.

05
Routine
Fixed Patterns That Create Peak Performance
Fluctuating performance is not natural — it is evidence of insufficient ability. Ichiro Suzuki remains an active player past 40, and his secret is routine — fixed training sequence, fixed diet menu, believing "same causes produce same results." Peter Drucker observed: those who produce results all start by "recording how they use their time." Four phases of creating routine: ① list what to do → ② decide order & volume → ③ practice at least one week → ④ evaluate & continuously improve.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The five dimensions of life management form the "infrastructure" of self-study. ① Habits: first build a Not-to-do List to eliminate bad habits, then use micro-repetition to cultivate good ones; ② Diet: eat simply (light and calm) and moderately (avoid satiety to preserve focus); ③ Sleep: pursue depth over duration, using 90-minute cycles and the instant-sleep method; ④ Time: acknowledge that willpower is finite, do the most important work at peak energy; ⑤ Routine: continually practice → evaluate → improve, creating peak performance through fixed patterns like elite athletes. Those who possess the optimal routine will live the optimal life.
