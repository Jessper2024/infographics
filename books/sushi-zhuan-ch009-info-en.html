Su Shi Biography · Ch9
"Under Brilliant Lamps and Flying Canopies, Dwelling in the Capital's Splendor"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer 1085: the old faction returned to power, six years of exile came to an end — but Su Shi felt no wild joy, only the detachment of "wine cup never empty, a hundred acres of fields — returning home to share leisure's delight." Mizhou elders lined the roads welcoming him, Dengzhou's mirage vanished in an instant, and then came the capital's summons under brilliant lights. Returning to official life, how did Su Shi view fame and reclusion? How had his state of mind changed from before his exile?

Core insight: From Huangzhou exile to Dengzhou prefect to capital recall, Su Shi's official career suddenly warmed — but returning after six idle years brought no ecstasy, only the serene clarity of "wine cup never empty, a hundred acres of fields — returning home to share leisure's delight." After Emperor Shenzong died and Empress Dowager Gao took the regency, the old faction was restored. Su Shi was first transferred to Ruzhou, then appointed Prefect of Dengzhou — only to be recalled to the capital after merely five days in office. Revisiting Mizhou after ten years, elders and children lined the roads to welcome him; infants he had saved from famine were now sturdy youths. Atop Penglai Pavilion, a mirage came and went in an instant. Before departing for the capital, he submitted two memorials pleading the people's cause — proof that four years at Huangzhou had not eroded his spirit of service.

01
Turning Point
Career Revival: Old Faction Rises, Exile Ends
"Mountains doubled, waters winding — no path in sight; willows dark, flowers bright — another village appears." Six years of idle exile had ended, the official road stretched smooth and open once more — yet Su Shi felt no great excitement or joy, only boundless reluctance and melancholy. After nearly thirty years of official storms, the Crow Terrace Poetry Trial's brush with death, and Huangzhou's Zen meditation and return to simplicity, youthful competitiveness and desire for fame had all faded to nothing.
🌅

Exile Ends: A New Dawn

In late July 1085, he set out for Dengzhou, crossing the Huai River for the seventh time. Recalling his sigh from a decade earlier — "fame and achievement are truly done" — he felt even more tired and indifferent now.

🍃

What His Heart Held: Reluctance

"Cloud and water wind along the streamside path; layered green hills encircle the stream flowing east. Moonlight whitens the sandbar, rousing roosting egrets — not a speck of dust finds its way here." He clung to Huangzhou's pastoral serenity.

02
Revisiting Old Haunts
Mizhou Elders: A Ten-Year Welcome on Both Sides of the Road
Entering Mizhou after a decade's absence, Su Shi was stirred: "I am the former governor of Jiaoxi." Looking up at Mount Chang, towering and aloof like a proud old man showing no sign of recognition, the poet felt a stab of melancholy. Yet if the hills were heartless, the people were not — the elders of Mizhou had not forgotten the governor who had blessed their land.

03
Pleading for the People
Five Days in Dengzhou: Two Memorials of Civic Concern
On October 15, when Su Shi arrived at Dengzhou, the shore was already crowded with welcomers. Several elderly men stepped forward: "Sir Su, you govern with love for the people — can you be like Prefect Ma?" Immediately upon taking office, Su Shi launched into extensive social investigation, sharply identifying two major abuses. But just as he prepared to implement reforms, the court summoned him back — his tenure as Dengzhou prefect lasted merely five days!

04
Illusion and Reality
Penglai Mirage: The Fleeting Vision of Immortal Isles
After completing the administrative handover, Su Shi used his few remaining leisure days to explore Dengzhou's scenic beauty. Ascending Penglai Pavilion on Cinnabar Cliff, he gazed upon a limitless expanse — five islands emerging and vanishing among the waves, ethereal and indistinct. The mirage came and went in a flash, reminding him of his Red Cliff meditation — "like mayflies between heaven and earth, a single grain in the vast sea."

05
Inner Detachment
Homeward Heart: Clarity Beneath the Capital's Brilliant Lamps
"Wine cup never empty, a hundred acres of fields — returning home to share leisure's delight." Beneath the capital's brilliant lamps and flying canopies, what Su Shi yearned for most was still buying farmland in Changzhou and retiring to the rivers and lakes. From the Crow Terrace Poetry Trial in 1079 to returning to the capital in 1085, six full years of exile had changed him profoundly.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The end of six years' exile brought not ecstasy but detachment — this was the most precious gift Huangzhou's four years had given Su Shi. The Mizhou elders lining the roads confirmed his unchanging commitment to governing with love for the people. Two memorials after merely five days in Dengzhou proved that "in old age, the rivers and lakes are never lost" — his essential character remained intact. The Penglai mirage's fleeting beauty mirrored his thorough disillusionment with fame's ostentation. Returning beneath the capital's brilliant lamps, Su Shi was a transformed man: no longer swayed by honor and disgrace, he found the true immortal realm in song and moonlight. As he had written ten years earlier: "All things have something worth observing. If there is something worth observing, there is something worth enjoying."
