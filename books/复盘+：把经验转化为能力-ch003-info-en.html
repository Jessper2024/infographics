Personal Review: The Essential Path to Growth

Review is the most effective way to learn from experience — it is the core method for converting information into capability. No matter how talented one is, without the habit and skill of review, experience alone cannot translate into real growth. Confucius believed in "learning to know" and Zengzi practiced "daily self-reflection three times" — both embody this principle.

What Deserves Personal Review?

Not everything needs review — but four types of events must be reviewed: new tasks, important matters, valuable experiences, and outcomes that fell short of expectations. These four categories cover the key scenarios most worth investing time in for deep learning, helping you focus your limited energy where it matters most.

Two Approaches to Personal Review

There are two paths for personal review: independent self-review and coach-assisted review, chosen flexibly based on the complexity of the event. Simple self-review suits everyday events and can be done in 10–15 minutes; complex matters may require half a day or more of systematic analysis. A coach helps break through blind spots and improves review quality.

📝

Simple Self-Review

Find undisturbed time and space, follow the review logic step by step. Use templates as guides or write down your thoughts. 1 hour monthly, half a day annually.

🤝

Find a Coach

Break through blind spots via structured questioning. A good coach stays neutral, focuses on the process, stimulates thinking, and improves review efficiency.

Five Key Precautions for Personal Review

Doing it well requires five key principles: be honest with yourself, formalize first then optimize, record and revisit regularly, maintain an open mind, and make it a habit. These five principles are interlocking — from attitude to method to habit — forming a complete safeguard system for personal review.

1
Be Honest with Yourself — Stay calm, objective, and truthful. Ask "why" multiple times, consider different perspectives, and avoid self-deception.
2
Formalize First, Optimize Later — Follow steps strictly at first; once familiar, adapt flexibly to your actual situation.
3
Record & Revisit Regularly — Written records beat memory. Connect related events to discover patterns and deeper issues.
4
Stay Open-Minded — Avoid the "experience trap." Stay Hungry, Stay Foolish. Question your existing assumptions.
5
Make It a Habit — "Reflect three times daily." Continuous reflection becomes a natural, subconscious practice.

How a CEO Was Forged

Liu Chuanzhi used review to turn every management chaos into a leadership upgrade, proving that "diligent review" is the core habit of outstanding leaders. In his early创业 days, his office was constantly interrupted by colleagues. That evening, he reviewed the whole situation and realized his work style was inefficient — leading him to the principle of "thinking clearly about priorities first."

Liu Chuanzhi said in an interview: "My habit is to take about five days every month or so, calm down, go through everything I've done recently, and write things down."
Liu vigorously promoted review within Lenovo, making it an organizational habit. This is a key reason why Lenovo has strong organizational learning capability and can tackle one challenge after another.
▎ KEY TAKEAWAY
Personal review is the essential path to capability growth. Focus on four types of critical events (new, important, valuable, below expectations), choose the right operating approach, face yourself with honesty, openness, and consistency — and make review a natural, subconscious habit. This is the dividing line between "having fought battles" and "becoming a general."
