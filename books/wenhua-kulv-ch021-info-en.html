Cultural Bitter Journey · Ch.21 "Ba Jin's Century"

A writer who lived a full century, carrying forward the most precious words of 20th-century Chinese culture

CORE PROPOSITION

Ba Jin outlived all other major Chinese and world writers—across his century, he carried four essential keywords for Chinese culture. In his first half-century, he used fiction to 'oppose feudalism and fight for humanity.' In his second half, through non-fiction, he 'denounced the sticks and spoke the truth.' In 1962, he precisely predicted the 'stick' nature of the Cultural Revolution. His Random Thoughts pioneered a tradition of intellectual self-reflection—his century is a history of Chinese cultural awakening.

01
Longevity

The Centenarian First: The Cultural Meaning of a Writer's Long Life

Ba Jin lived to 100—among all major Chinese and world writers, he lived the longest.

Chinese writers: Cao Xueqin died in his 40s, Sima Qian in his 50s, Qu Yuan, Li Bai, Su Shi in their 60s, Pu Songling, Li Qingzhao in their 70s, Lu You in his 80s. World writers: Dante, Shakespeare died in their 50s, Hemingway in his 60s, Tolstoy in his 80s, George Bernard Shaw in his 90s. Ba Jin is the endpoint—all writers across time and space turn to gaze at this Chinese elder.

Great writers' longevity differs from others—they make elapsed time linger, wilted time regreen, cooled time warm. With his hundred years, Ba Jin sensitively watched an entire century.

02
Prophecy

Denouncing the Sticks: A Precise Prophecy in 1962

On May 9, 1962, Ba Jin precisely described the 'stick' nature of the coming Cultural Revolution—his words remain applicable today.

'I'm somewhat afraid of those people who hold a frame in one hand and a stick in the other, looking everywhere for faults. They love making simple frames. If someone refuses to squeeze into their frame, they rise in fury, raise their sticks, and deliver a head-on blow.' When AP reported this, Ba Jin became one who 'provided ammunition for imperialist attacks on China.'

🔑
Ba Jin's 'Stick Theory' (7 Points): ① These people create their own frames ② Their method: make frames → trap people → seize pretexts → attach labels → strike with sticks ③ They are hidden and ambiguous ④ Their strikes are swift ⑤ Few in number but loud in all media ⑥ Primal drive is jealousy ⑦ Source of cruelty and cowardice in cultural circles

A few years later, Yao Wenyuan and Zhang Chunqiao rose to power, calling themselves 'proletarian golden sticks'—Ba Jin's term for them, which they adopted and gilded.

03
Resilience

Dante's Inferno: Spiritual Perseverance in Catastrophe

In the darkest years, Ba Jin sat alone in his courtyard reciting Dante's Divine Comedy in Italian—a Chinese writer's desolate yet resilient recitation.

During the Cultural Revolution, almost no one at the Shanghai Writers' Association dared speak to Ba Jin. His wife, Xiao Shan, died of illness; he wailed, clutching her ashes. Zhang Chunqiao declared: 'For Ba Jin, not executing him IS implementing policy.' Yet in this abyss, he connected to the world's finest sensibility and thought.

“
O kindly living souls, you who come to visit us, the shades who have stained the earth with blood... since you pity our cruel punishment...
—— Dante, Divine Comedy, recited by Ba Jin

'Falsehood, evil, ugliness; truth, goodness, beauty—confronting and entwining each other, with hell and heaven spanning between them.' The author says the memory of hearing Ba Jin recite the Divine Comedy became the starting point for many things.

04
Reflection

Speak the Truth: Random Thoughts and Self-Examination

In his later years, Ba Jin pioneered Chinese intellectual self-reflection with Random Thoughts—'speak the truth' became the most important three-word maxim of late 20th-century Chinese culture.

After the catastrophe, the very writers who had attacked Ba Jin suddenly claimed they had always fought alongside him. Too kind to expose them, Ba Jin issued three calls: establish a 'Cultural Revolution Museum,' write Random Thoughts to examine himself, and urge everyone to 'speak the truth.'

✅ Ba Jin's First Half

Used fiction for two causes: 'oppose feudalism' and 'fight for humanity.' Works like Family, Spring, Autumn built a bridge between profundity and popularity.

⚠️ Ba Jin's Second Half

Through non-fiction, championed two causes: 'denounce the sticks' and 'speak the truth'—he led alone, shaking the land. Random Thoughts silenced China's least self-reflective cultural circles.

Chinese culture severely lacks mechanisms for distinguishing truth from falsehood. Ba Jin's repeated call to 'speak the truth' carried tremendous cultural challenge.

05
Witness

The Mailbox on Wukang Road: A Cultural Delivery

All of the author's manuscripts written during his expeditions among ruins and wastelands were deposited into the mailbox of Ba Jin's home—a mailbox that had witnessed too much suffering.

In 1962, at seventeen, the author first entered Ba Jin's home at 113 Wukang Road. Over twenty years later, manuscripts of Cultural Bitter Journey and other works all reached readers through that same mailbox. Ba Jin still edited Harvest magazine, with his daughter Li Xiaolin managing it after he fell ill.

“
Because I had witnessed what Ba Jin endured, I was able to stand unmoved amid large-scale slander, smiling.
—— Yu Qiuyu

In the hospital, Ba Jin, wearing a bib at mealtime, told Li Xiaolin in Sichuan dialect: 'Eating like this, I'm losing face in front of Yu Qiuyu!'—When the author heard this, he arranged for someone to deliver hamburgers to the old man daily.

💡
KEY TAKEAWAY

Ba Jin's century encapsulates the fundamental question of 20th-century Chinese culture: how to insist on 'speaking the truth' in a culture lacking mechanisms to distinguish truth from falsehood; how to maintain a humanist's gentle gaze in an era of flying sticks. Across his lifetime, he carried four keywords—oppose feudalism, fight for humanity, denounce the sticks, speak the truth—each remaining unfinished homework for Chinese culture.
