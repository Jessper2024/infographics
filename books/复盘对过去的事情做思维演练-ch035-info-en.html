Fupan · Ch.35 The Key Points Method

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer When an event's information volume is overwhelming — like fupan-ing 60 years of CPC history — the Situation Recreation Method becomes nearly impossible. How can we fupan efficiently while maintaining an overall grasp of the event? The Key Points Method: first identify pivotal nodes, then recreate, reflect, and reason around them. It divides into Forward (milestone moments), Reverse (key success factors), and Question methods (starting from doubts).

The Key Points Method is the second fupan method. When drafting the Resolution on Party History, Deng Xiaoping said: "Avoid a purely narrative approach; write more concisely. Make judgments on important issues." When facing massive information, first identify key nodes, then fupan around them. Like the mathematical concept of "complete coverage" — using the union of subsets to achieve full coverage of the superset.

01
Method Definition
The Key Points Method and Inspiration from the 1981 Resolution
The Key Points Method: first identify pivotal nodes of the fupan event, then recreate, reflect, and reason around those nodes. The 1981 Resolution on Party History is the classic case. Facing 60 years of CPC history, Deng Xiaoping proposed "keep it rough, keep it concise, don't go too detailed" — dividing 60 years into benchmark time nodes, then reviewing, evaluating, and summarizing each period.

📋 Resolution's 8 Parts

Pre-1949 28 years → 32 years assessment → Socialist transformation 7 yrs → Full construction 10 yrs → Cultural Revolution 10 yrs → Historic turn → Mao's evaluation → Unity. Eight parts achieving "complete coverage" of 60 years of Chinese history.

💡 Core Idea

Step outside the event itself, don't drown in the sea of information. Start with an overall grasp, seize the main aspect of the main issue — grab the bull by the horns — achieving twice the result with half the effort.

02
Forward Method
Forward Key Points: Milestone Moments and Milestone Events
Forward Key Points finds nodes chronologically and fupans around them. Key nodes come in two types: temporal nodes (milestone moments) and content nodes (milestone events). Normally they align, with content nodes taking priority — because content nodes reflect the event's natural internal progression. When an event reaches a certain timeframe without milestone results, temporal nodes alone won't work.

Reverse Method
Reverse Key Points: Lenovo's Classic Fupan of the Dell Competition
Reverse Key Points first identifies the key success factors the event must satisfy, then examines performance around those factors. When Dell launched a frontal assault on Lenovo in 2001, Lenovo didn't get stuck on the "direct sales vs. distribution" surface debate — it saw the core: this is a competition of process excellence and efficiency. After identifying each side's strengths and weaknesses, Lenovo took four measures: strategic contraction, inventory compression (15→7 days), organizational flattening (7→18 sales regions), and building a phone-sales + key-accounts division. Dell also pivoted, putting PCs in more retail stores.

04
Question Method
The Question Method: Starting from Doubts — Questions Are the Best Fupan Catalyst
The Question Method raises questions about doubtful points, then digs into logic and causes around those questions. This resembles Go players replaying a game: they first place the "questionable moves" on the board, then think and recreate around those moves, gradually reconstructing the entire game. Questions should target results and facts, not personal psychology — results are measurable and consensus is easy. Questions must be evenly distributed across every process or time node.
"Scholars were puzzled by one phenomenon: why do people scatter in panic during a cinema fire, while family members help each other evacuate orderly during a house fire? By reviewing this phenomenon, scholars developed the concept of social networks, creating a whole set of analytical tools for social phenomena."
— The power of a question: one seemingly simple question can open an entirely new research space
05
Mathematical Basis
Complete Coverage Principle: The Mathematical Foundation of Key Points
The Key Points Method works because of its mathematical alignment with "complete coverage" theory. The entire event is the superset; each time or process node is a subset. By taking the union of these subsets, we achieve full coverage of the superset. Like the line segment [0,5] can be completely covered by subsets [0,1), [1,2), [2,3), [3,4), [4,5] — as long as divisions are reasonable, non-overlapping, and cover completely, the Key Points Method enables efficient and comprehensive fupan.

🔑 Core Takeaway
The Key Points Method is the most efficient fupan approach when information volume is massive. The Forward method seizes milestones chronologically, the Reverse method deduces gaps from key success factors, and the Question method digs into logic starting from doubts. Its mathematical basis — complete coverage — ensures that with reasonable division, node-by-node fupan covers everything without omission. The method has proven broadly applicable and remarkably effective through cases like fupan-ing Party history, Lenovo vs. Dell, and book publishing strategy.
