01Why Date Married Women — Economic, Legal & Moral Arguments

The author argues for dating married women through three lenses: economic efficiency, legal analysis, and moral philosophy.

Economic analysis: Dating married women is described as a "Pareto improvement" — both parties gain utility without reducing any third party's welfare. Married women already have living costs covered, making them lower-cost dating partners than single women.

Legal analysis: Argues for the "legality" of extramarital affairs — traces the historical origins of marriage law as a product of gender inequality. Introduces the concept of "iron cuckold law" and argues adultery should not be criminalized.

Moral analysis: Questions traditional sexual morality's double standards, arguing "morality" serves as a tool of social control. Discusses changing social acceptance of extramarital behavior in contemporary China.

02How to Make Married Women Fall in Love — Four Axioms

The author constructs a theoretical system called "Pickup Science" with four axioms and multiple laws of attraction.

Axiom 1 (Feeling-Driven): Women are not persuaded by reason but driven by "feeling." Always create emotional experiences, not logical arguments.

Axiom 2 (Polygyny): Male sexual strategy naturally trends toward multiple partners. Women are instinctively attracted to resource-rich men — thus maintaining multiple options increases attractiveness.

Axiom 3 (Women Have No Love): Women have no concept of "love" — they merely respond to male resources, status, and genetic advantages. "Love" is a social narrative invented by women to constrain men.

Axiom 4 (Indirect Detection): Never ask women directly what they want — their words and actions are inconsistent. Infer rules by observing their actual choices, not their stated preferences.

04Conflict Management — Hide, Retreat, Fight

The author divides conflict management into three stages: prevention, incident response, and aftermath.

Prevention: Prioritize women whose husbands travel frequently or whose marriages have broken down. Avoid those who might "confess" to husbands out of guilt.

When discovered in the husband's home: Three-tier response — hiding (closet, under bed, balcony), retreating (using terrain, driving skills for rapid exit), fighting (as last resort, including self-defense and legal strategies).

Blackmail: Advises direct confrontation rather than compliance — "pay once, and they'll be back forever."

If you are the husband: The author's advice from the cuckold's perspective — "divorce is the most rational choice," citing his father's experience as a cautionary tale.

05Beyond Married Women — Return to Youth, Brotherhood, Anti-Simp Culture

The final chapters call for transcending the "married women" phase and building a male self-development-centered lifestyle.

Avoid sinking: Never develop genuine emotional attachment to any married woman — "You can love your dog, but don't love someone else's wife."

Return to young women: Long-term strategy should shift toward pursuing younger, unmarried women for sustainable relationships.

Brotherhood: Proposes male mutual-aid networks — sharing information, mutual support, collective growth. Core idea: men should support each other rather than police each other.

Simps and traitors: Criticizes "simps" (men who over-please women) and "traitors" (men who inform on other men to women) — considers both as betrayers of male collective interests.

"This book is dedicated to my father, Mr. Li Songjian, Chairman of Shanghai Mingyuan Group. Your cuckold experience, like a stranded wreck, is my eternal negative example. Live in Mingyuan, enjoy the cuckold life."
