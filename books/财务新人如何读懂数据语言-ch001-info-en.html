The VT-ST Two-Dimensional Framework
Professor Sorensen's two tests for strategic decisions: VT (Validity) checks whether "if A holds, does B necessarily follow?" ST (Soundness) checks whether "A itself is true." Only if both pass should a proposal be seriously considered. Example: semiconductor capacity investment—VT: does more capacity necessarily increase margins? Depends on existing facility space. ST: can you actually increase capacity? Competitors may expand simultaneously, triggering price wars.
VT: Does Knowledge Bring Competitiveness?
Does grad school → enhance knowledge → greater competitiveness hold logically? The author argues companies need capability, not just knowledge. Level-8 English doesn't guarantee leading a cross-border meeting—it also requires communication skills, adaptability, and hands-on experience. The workplace is a better learning environment than university—what's missing is practical ability, not theory.
ST: Can Grad School Deliver Practical Knowledge?
Universities still teach structured, static knowledge. Companies need dynamic, practice-linked knowledge—integrated with legal, accounting, and business contexts. Application trumps concepts. Studying internal control theory endlessly is less useful than examining how a company configures USB permissions in online banking. Both test rounds: the intern's choice couldn't withstand scrutiny.
Knowledge vs. Capability: What Employers Need
Competitiveness is market-determined, specifically by employers. Left-column positional knowledge is just the baseline. Right-column comprehensive abilities—communication, decision-making under pressure, cross-departmental collaboration—determine career trajectory. A graduate with zero experience is better off learning on the job than returning to school that shields them from reality.
Foresight in Practice: VT-ST for Life Decisions
The author laments: all the intern's roommates blindly chose grad school the same way. If university graduates never use strategic thinking to navigate their life direction, that's the real tragedy. Help one at a time—the hope is that the VT-ST toolkit empowers more people to think foresightedly and make life decisions that withstand rigorous scrutiny.
KEY TAKEAWAY
Apply the VT-ST framework to every major decision—both validity and soundness tests are essential. Replace "everyone does it" path dependency with foresightful thinking that navigates your life direction.
