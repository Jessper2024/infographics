How to Trade in Stocks · Ch.9 "Livermore's Trading Rules"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer The market constantly cycles between upward trends, downward trends, and natural adjustments. Traders operate on gut feelings, lacking objective criteria to identify trend changes. Can strict recording rules automatically reveal trend reversals and trading timing? The answer: use 6-point amplitudes to trigger column transitions, 3-point breakouts to confirm trend continuation or termination, and pivotal point markings to issue buy/sell signals — a fully objective trading system.

Livermore distilled decades of trading experience into 10 core trading rules, constructing a complete market recording and signal identification system. Three core mechanisms: using black, red, and pencil to distinguish trend direction (Rules 1–3), using the 6-point reversal rule to trigger column transitions and pivotal point generation (Rules 4a–4d), and using 3-point breakouts to confirm trend continuation or termination (Rules 5a–5b). The record itself becomes a speaking tool — column transitions, ink colors, pivotal point markings — every detail emits a clear market signal.

Rules 4a–4d
The 6-Point Reversal Rule — Triggering Column Transitions and Pivotal Points
When the market makes a counter-move of approximately 6 points, a column transition is triggered. At each transition, draw two horizontal lines below the last figure in the original column — that price immediately becomes a pivotal point. The 6-point rule is the first filter for identifying trend changes.

Rules 8–9
The Pivotal Point System — An Automatic Buy/Sell Signal Mechanism
It is the formation of pivotal points that gives the record its great predictive value. Once two pivotal points form — the upper extreme and lower extreme — the market's subsequent action revolves around them, issuing clear buy and sell signals. Pivotal points are the dividing line between bullish and bearish forces.

05
Rules 10a–10f
Danger Signals of Trend Termination — An Objective Risk Management Framework
The highest value of the entire rule set lies in determining whether a trend has ended. When the market breaks beyond a pivotal point by more than 3 points, trend continuation is confirmed; conversely, if the breakout fails and reverses by more than 3 points, a danger signal of termination is issued. Rules 10e–f reveal an even stronger double danger signal: price stalls near the pivotal point and reverses without reaching it.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The essence of Livermore's trading rules: 10 rules construct a fully objective market analysis system — using black/red/pencil ink to distinguish trend direction, 6-point amplitude to trigger column transitions and pivotal point generation, and 3-point breakout to confirm trend continuation or termination. The record itself becomes a speaking tool: column transitions, ink colors, pivotal point markings — every detail emits a clear market signal. The ultimate value of this system: it does not depend on the trader's subjective judgment, but lets the market automatically reveal its true intentions through price behavior.
