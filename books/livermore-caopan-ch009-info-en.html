Rules 1-3

The Three-Color Recording System

The foundation of Livermore's trading rules is a three-color recording system that visually distinguishes market trend direction. Every trader must personally maintain the price record—this is the starting point from which the entire rule framework operates.

⬇️ Red

✏️ Pencil

All Other Columns: record in pencil, representing corrective and transitional states
📋
Six Columns: Uptrend Column · Downtrend Column · Natural Rally Column · Natural Reaction Column · Secondary Rally Column · Secondary Reaction Column — the first four are core columns; secondary columns record fluctuations within the Key Point range.
💡 BLUF: Ink color is the trend signal—black for uptrend, red for downtrend, pencil for transition—clear at a glance.

Rules 4a-4d

The 6-Point Transition: Column Shift Signals

When the market moves in the opposite direction by approximately 6 points, a column transition is triggered. Each transition is marked by drawing two horizontal lines beneath the last recorded figure in the original column—that price immediately becomes a Key Point. The 6-point rule is the system's first filter for identifying trend changes.

RuleTransitionTrigger ConditionLine Color
4a Uptrend → Natural Reaction Decline ≈6 pts from last Uptrend figure Two Red Lines
4b Natural Reaction → Natural Rally / Uptrend Rally ≈6 pts from last Natural Reaction figure Two Red Lines
4c Downtrend → Natural Rally Rally ≈6 pts from last Downtrend figure Two Black Lines
4d Natural Rally → Natural Reaction / Downtrend Decline ≈6 pts from last Natural Rally figure Two Black Lines
🔑
Annotation Key: Draw red lines when transitioning from an uptrend, black lines when transitioning from a downtrend—the line color contrasts with the source trend for conspicuous reversal marking. The price marked by two lines immediately becomes a "Key Point" (Rule 8).
💡 BLUF: The 6-point turn is the early warning of trend change—every column transition generates a Key Point.
03
Rules 5-7

3-Point Breakout Confirmation & Trend Continuation

After a 6-point turn triggers a column transition, the market must validate through a 3-point breakout to confirm the new trend (for composite prices: 12-point turn, 6-point breakout). Meanwhile, as long as prices continue in the original direction without a 6-point reversal, entries remain in the current column.

▲ Rule 5a: Uptrend Confirmation

When recording in the Natural Rally column, if the latest price is 3+ points above the last price marked with black lines in the Natural Rally column → record in black ink in the Uptrend column. Uptrend confirmed.

▼ Rule 5b: Downtrend Confirmation

When recording in the Natural Reaction column, if the latest price is 3+ points below the last price marked with red lines in the Natural Reaction column → record in red ink in the Downtrend column. Downtrend confirmed.

Rules 6a-6h detail the recording procedures during trend continuation: in the Uptrend column, continue recording as long as new prices remain above the column's last recorded price (6a, 6d); the reverse applies in the Downtrend column (6c). A crucial detail is the use of Secondary columns: when a 6-point reversal occurs but the price does not surpass the last entry in the Natural Rally/Reaction column, entries shift to the Secondary Rally or Secondary Reaction column (6g, 6h).

📐
Rule 7 — Composite Prices: The same principles apply to composite indices, but with doubled magnitudes—use 12 points instead of 6 for turns, and 6 points instead of 3 for breakouts. Individual stocks and the broader market follow the same logic at different scales.
💡 BLUF: The 6-point turn provides the initial signal; the 3-point breakout delivers confirmation—together they form Livermore's complete logical chain for trend assessment.

05
Rules 10a-10f

Danger Signals of Trend Termination

The highest value of the entire rule set lies in judging whether a trend has ended. When the market breaks through a Key Point by more than 3 points (individual stocks) or 6 points (composites), the trend continuation is confirmed; conversely, if the breakout fails and the price reverses by 3+ points in the opposite direction, it emits a trend termination danger signal.

✅ Rules 10a-c: Trend Confirmed

Uptrend resumes → breaks above prior Key Point +3 pts
Downtrend resumes → breaks below prior Key Point -3 pts
Breakout succeeds → continue operating in the original trend direction

⚠️ Rules 10b-d: Trend Terminated

Uptrend fails → declines below Key Point -3 pts
Downtrend fails → rallies above Key Point +3 pts
Breakout fails → trend may have ended

🚨
Rules 10e-f · Double Danger Signal: If the price stalls near a Key Point (reversing before reaching it) and then moves 3+ points in the opposite direction, this constitutes an even stronger danger signal. Example: rally stalls below the Key Point → declines 3+ points → uptrend likely terminated. Reverse applies to downtrends.

Core Conclusion

The essence of Livermore's Trading Rules: Ten rules construct a completely objective market analysis system—three ink colors (black, red, pencil) distinguish trend direction; the 6-point magnitude triggers column transitions and Key Point generation; the 3-point breakout confirms trend continuation or termination. The price record itself becomes a speaking instrument: column shifts, ink colors, Key Point annotations—every detail emits a clear market signal. The ultimate value of this system is that it does not rely on the trader's subjective judgment, but lets the market automatically reveal its true intentions through price action.
