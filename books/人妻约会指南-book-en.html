1 Economic Analysis

The author uses a "car market" analogy for contemporary male-female relationships: new cars (bride prices) grow increasingly expensive; at new-car prices you might get a wreck with hidden damage; after purchase the car may "drive itself away" (divorce asset seizure). Renting or ride-hailing (prostitution) is punished by law. In this dilemma, dating married women is likened to "driving away the neighbor's car" — purchase cost, mortgage, and insurance all paid by someone else, and entirely legal.

Traditional Marriage Path

High bride price + high housing + divorce risk + one-sided loyalty obligation

Author's Alternative

Zero cost + legal + no support obligation + access to "wealthy men's wives"

3 The "Iron Cuckold Law"

The author introduces the concept of the "Iron Cuckold Law": in France and China, husbands are legally obligated to support children born from their wives' extramarital affairs — like a Qing dynasty "Iron Hat Prince," it can never be removed. The US allows husbands to disclaim paternity through DNA testing. China defaults to the Iron Cuckold system with supplementary compensation mechanisms.

4 Moral Analysis

The author argues moral legitimacy from three angles: single men's right to satisfy physiological needs when costs are prohibitive; women's suffering within marriage; and the husband's supposedly damaged rights lacking legal or moral support. The chapter cites Pew Research Center data showing Americans under 35 hold a historically low share of national wealth, arguing that "redistribution" through relationships with wealthy men's wives has moral justification.

5 Modern Chinese Values Critique

The author characterizes China's mainstream values as "conservative progressivism" — radically conserving century-old progressivism, essentially a "diluted Prussian-total-war value system." After ideals collapsed, it devolved into materialism. The author uses a Beijing-based Hunan sex worker's home-buying story and Guo Juyang's mansion-bragging as specific case studies.

"Looking at her once-familiar yet now strange face, like in The Great Gatsby, I felt her voice is full of money."

Key Takeaways

This chapter constructs a complete argumentation framework from four dimensions: economics (cost-benefit analysis), legal theory (legality of affairs, sexual autonomy, Iron Cuckold Law), moral philosophy (resource redistribution legitimacy), and value-system critique ("conservative progressivism" and materialism). Drawing on the French Declaration of Rights, New China's first Marriage Law, and Pew Research data, the conclusion points to: in the current social structure, dating married women is legal, morally defensible, and economically rational.

1 Dating Is a Science

The author draws an analogy with quantitative trading: just as quant trading is based on the belief that "markets are predictable," dating should also be grounded in empirical scientific methods. The author criticizes most Chinese people for never truly mastering scientific thinking despite formal education. He claims to have studied game theory under two Turing Award laureates — Prof. Andrew Yao and Prof. Silvio Micali — using this as the theoretical foundation for the book.

"The first step to learning dating is to recognize that it is a science — women's psychology and behavior are predictable, even very easily predictable."

Women are innately attracted to aggressive men. Research data shows pregnancy rates increase significantly after domestic violence. Verbal aggression serves as a legal alternative.

Law 4: Revolutionary & Artist

Revolutionaries (aggression + justice + courage) and artists (primal emotional expression) never lack women. Examples: Che Guevara, Wang Feng, Cui Jian.

1 Date Location Risk Management

This section analyzes risk levels of different date venues from a practical standpoint. For hotel dates, it recommends one person check in alone to avoid dual registration in the public security database (which is prone to leaks). Weekday daytime office dates are viable at tech companies with nap rooms (Amazon Twitch, Google offer nap rooms and free meals).

Outdoor dates should avoid the husband's activity radius — e.g., if the husband frequents Shenzhen Nanshan's Fangda Center, choose Futian's Coco Park or Ping'an Finance Center. If the husband returns to Shantou Chaonan for Spring Festival, date in Longhu or Jinping districts. The author advises against using one's own residence, primarily to prevent the woman from developing a "hostess" mentality and post-breakup disputes.

"Observe the husband's life rhythm like a naval commander observing tides — schedule dates when his probability of returning home is at its lowest."

2 Three-Step Response When Discovered

Drawing from professional Active Shooter safety training received in the US, the author proposes a three-step response to the husband's sudden appearance: Hide → Retreat → Fight. The psychological foundation rests on three legal principles: women's 100% sexual autonomy, both parties' 100% right to health, and 100% right to self-defense (from the Universal Declaration of Human Rights).

3 Hiding Spot Comparative Assessment

The author conducts a systematic evaluation of five common hiding spots, assessing concealment quality, physical endurance, and transition flexibility (ability to switch between retreat and combat).

4 Extortion Response Strategy

When facing extortion, the author notes that husbands who choose extortion over violence essentially view their wife as exploitable property — physical safety risk is actually lower. The core task is minimizing financial loss.

If the husband's ask is below your mental price, maintain composure, appear troubled, then counter ~20% lower for quick settlement. If above, it's likely an inflated ask — counter at your target price. At the scene, you may overpromise to exit safely, then renegotiate afterward on the legal basis that consensual relationships are not illegal.

The fourth step is confrontation: ensure all controllable assets are secured, remove the wife from the home, then initiate legal proceedings. If children are involved, a paternity test is recommended first, and children's welfare should be protected.

Chapter 5 constructs a complete conflict resolution framework: pre-date location risk management, three-step emergency response when discovered (Hide → Retreat → Fight), post-incident extortion negotiation and legal strategy, and the husband's rational four-step process. 1 Avoid Sinking: Don't Fall in Love with Married Women

"Sinking" is a Hong Kong Cantonese slang term originally meaning falling in love with a prostitute, later extended to loving anyone one shouldn't. The risk is extremely high when dating married women: they are more experienced, better at flattery, demand less, and are more sexually experienced — men easily develop feelings. But once sunk, the woman's security needs and child-rearing responsibilities transfer to you.

The author uses Ling Feifei draining Li Songjian of all time and money as an extreme example. The remedy is to step back and reassess — wasn't the original reason for choosing married women their low cost? Clarity on this point prevents sinking.

2 Return to Young Women: Time Cost Determines Choice

As personal development progresses, "returning to young women" becomes the rational choice when time cost exceeds a threshold. The author illustrates: in 2021 Shenzhen, a married woman couldn't coordinate schedules, so he sent ¥30,000 via WeChat to "hire a nanny and come out" — because waiting an extra day or two in Shenzhen would cost more than ¥30,000.

Hidden cost (married woman)
¥30,000 (time + coordination + opportunity)
Direct cost (young woman)
¥5,000 (Disney + steakhouse + freedom)

In 2023, the author took a young Fujian programmer on a date — Mercedes S680 from Century Avenue to Disneyland to Ruth's Chris on the Bund — total ¥5,000. "Isn't this ¥5,000 worth many times more than that ¥30,000?" Core logic: when your hourly economic output is high enough, the time cost of sharing a married woman exceeds direct dating expenses.

3 Transcend Women: Recognizing the Extraction-Drain Dynamic

The author observes that proximity to women inherently produces energy and financial drain — like fox spirits in "Strange Stories from a Chinese Studio" absorbing yang energy. This drain escalates from school years (homework help, carrying water coolers) to relationships (allowance demands, salary card surrender, bride price, property co-naming).

Direct Economic Extraction

Allowances, bride price (Jiangxi highest near ¥1M), property co-naming (metro areas: millions to tens of millions, globally defaults to 50% value gift)

Covert Psychological Control

No sleep without red envelopes, deliberately leaving feminine items to claim territory, using tantrums to drain energy — high-value men often have to pay to de-escalate

The author compares this to corporate management: high-value men should screen entrants as rigorously as companies interview. Alpha Star conducts 8 rounds, rejecting 99%+ of applicants — "How dare you casually date or marry?"

4 Brotherhood: The Highest Form of Male Alliance

The author argues that humanity's truly great emotion is brotherly bond between unrelated men. Three cultural dimensions: Chinese tradition (Oath of the Peach Garden, Baidicheng deathbed trust), American fraternity culture (MIT fraternity housing), Silicon Valley startup culture (wealthy hosts provide houses for young entrepreneurs in exchange for equity).

Core case: Shantou Jinshan High School Computer Olympiad class. When the author joined, no one from Jinzhong had ever made the provincial team. Under his leadership (obtaining US team training materials from the American coach, resisting principal Li Lili's bullying), in 2009 NOI: 2 of 20 national gold medals went to Shantou (1/10), becoming Guangdong's #1. The class later produced Moonshot AI founder Yang Zhilin and "Brain King" Zheng Linkai.

"If a group can unite through faith, loyalty, or friendship — transcending the prisoner's dilemma — its combat power far exceeds that of a group composed of petty people."

5 Simps and Petty People: A Confucian Analysis

Using the Confucian framework of "the gentleman is guided by righteousness (义), the petty person by profit (利)," the author argues that simps and petty people are fundamentally the same. Tsinghua University serves as the counterexample: Girls' Day requiring collective gift-buying (students with <¥1,000 monthly allowance forced to contribute hundreds), classmates deliberately providing wrong exam information, and the parallel between Chicago Loop's atmosphere and Tsinghua's culture.

Key Takeaways

Chapter 6 constructs a complete path for male self-actualization: avoid sinking (don't fall for married women) → return to young women (time cost determines choice) → transcend women (recognize the extraction-drain dynamic) → brotherhood (the highest form of male alliance). 1 A Generation's Collective Predicament

The author uses intense personal emotion to describe the situation of a generation of young Chinese men: locked in schools for 20 years or more, then forced to marry, buy a house, and take on a 30-year mortgage. He likens this to being released from "prison" only to enter "forced labor" — Bao'an propping up prices near Nanshan Science Park, Tangzhen near Zhang Jiang, Xinzhuang near Caohejing, Changping near Haidian, and Yuhang propping up all of Hangzhou.

The author describes seeing Chinese-made industrial products in stores worldwide, hearing white colleagues and ABCs at top AI companies in the Bay Area cry about "can't keep up with Tsinghua graduates," and having to wait until 8 PM to meet friends for dinner in Nanshan Science Park or Zhang Jiang — "Wanting to go out for barbecue and beer with an old friend at 8 PM, and having to apologize and request approval from some damn boss."

"Why is our generation — the most excellent, hardest-working, most capable generation of Chinese men — locked in schools like prisoners for 20 years or more, then married off, forced by some woman to buy a house in 2021, forced to apply for a 30-year mortgage?"

3 Baishizhou: Another Side of Guo Juyang

During Shenzhen's 2022 real estate frenzy, in a rental apartment in Baishizhou Xincun Village, the author and Guo Juyang lay together on the sofa afterward. He told her "I'm so envious of your Shenzhen hukou," and Guo Juyang replied in Chaoyang dialect: "Xin Ye, stop mocking me. How can a Shenzhen hukou compare to your American identity?"

In that moment, the author felt that the person before him was no longer the vulgar woman who traded her body for a mansion, but more like the 14-year-old girl who confessed to him in Chaoyang dialect 14 years ago. Compared to the landlady downstairs and the tea-shop owner across the street who proudly boasted about their Shenzhen properties, Guo Juyang "at least had that one moment where, beyond money, her eyes still aspired to ideals and love — otherwise why would she walk from the Portofino mansion to a Baishizhou rental?"

5 The Book's Final Close

The author concludes with the book's final emotional declaration. He knows many will want to criticize him just from seeing the title, but poses a counter-question: "Without these young men stepping up to date married women, these married women's lives would be too tragic! If dating married women were illegal, then this already so corrupt society would be too hopeless!"

This epilogue elevates the book from an operational manual to an emotional indictment of social reality, carrying a generation's fate within personal narrative. From the high school memories in Chapter 1 to the Baishizhou scene in Chapter 7, Guo Juyang serves as the narrative thread running through the entire book, completing an arc from girlhood to married woman to "at least having one moment of aspiring to ideals and love."

Chapter 7 is the emotional climax of the book, sublimating personal pain into a generation's collective predicament. The creation of Alpha Star's automated trading robot (codename Sirius, 2023.2.21) becomes the vehicle for transformation from hatred to creation. The scene in the Baishizhou rental where Guo Juyang responds in Chaoyang dialect is the book's most literarily powerful moment. The epilogue closes with "without the young men's action, married women's lives are too tragic," completing the elevation from operational guide to socio-emotional narrative.

2 A.2 On Zhang Guimei

3 A.3 My Father Li Songjian

Third Face: Contempt for Servants

Young men are all servants. Hates highly educated people — "Fudan and SJTU grads still work for me." In 2021 told author to sell beef balls on Taobao; in 2025 raged at unfinished buildings: "Why aren't you servants buying my houses?"

4 A.4 My Sister Li Xinying

Written July 19, 2025. Tells the tragic life of sister Li Xinying: born in 1989 in Shantou, originally holding a "strong female lead script" (grandfather was town mayor, grandfather-in-law was school principal, father was worth over 100 million). After the father went to prison, Ling Feifei backstabbed and divorced, taking half the assets. The father later found Xu Jie ("Professor Chicken"), who bore a half-brother Li Xinquan. The sister was kept by the father as a "servant," at 37 years old running a Chaoyang beef hotpot restaurant alone, daily serving as "Professor Chicken's dog" accompanying her shopping.
