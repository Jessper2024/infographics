After establishing independence with barely 1,000 men, Zhu returned to Dingyuan with urgent need to expand. He tricked the Zhang family camp commander with a false banquet invitation—seizing 3,000 men—then launched a night assault on Miao Daheng's 20,000-strong garrison, adding them without major resistance. His troops were no longer a rabble: he rebuked the surrendered soldiers for lacking discipline, demanding rigorous training—a unprecedented standard. Recruited Feng Guoyong and Feng Guosheng brothers, plus Li Shanchang who urged him to "emulate Liu Bang and conquer the empire in five years." Captured Chuzhou, a strategic stronghold. When Guo Zixing was ousted and sought refuge, Zhu ceded Chuzhou and gave him command of 30,000 elite troops—magnanimity that cleared every obstacle to his true empire.

Military Expansion
First Capital: Deception and Night Assault
Deceived by a table promise, Zhu turned the trick against the trickster. He invited the Zhang camp commander to a "prepared feast"—instead, he had him bound. Using the commander's seal, he ordered the 3,000 men to relocate—absorbing them whole. At Henganshan, 20,000 troops were taken by night assault with barely any resistance. Zhu's force ceased to be a rabble—discipline and training became the new standard.

02
Core Team
Talent Recruitment: Feng Brothers & Li Shanchang
Every recruit changed history—Zhu's eye for talent was flawless. The Feng brothers came from Dingyuan to serve; Feng Guosheng (Feng Sheng) became one of the greatest generals of the era. Li Shanchang—originally a mere scribe—answered Zhu's rhetorical sigh with a single line: "Emulate Liu Bang and the empire will be yours." Zhu immediately rose and performed the ceremony of respect. Li Shanchang became the founding's first great minister.

Strategic Stronghold
Chuzhou: The First Strategic Asset
Chuzhou—"surrounded by mountains"—an impregnable southeastern fortress. At the opening battle, warrior Hua Yun led 1,000 cavalry in a direct central breakthrough—Yuan forces collapsed instantly. Zhu's army captured Chuzhou in one stroke. With strategists clouding around him, elite troops, and a stronghold for advance or retreat, his vision had already shifted from Bozhou or Chuzhou to the entire realm. Age 26.

04
Magnanimity
Guo Zixing Arrives: Yielding 30,000 Troops
"Without you, there would be no me today—I cannot forget your grace." Guo Zixing, ousted and desperate, sought refuge with the son-in-law he once tried to destroy. Zhu ceded not only Chuzhou but the command of 30,000 elite troops—a gesture that stunned everyone. True strength shows not through violence but through magnanimity—those who suspected and plotted against him became his admirers. Guo died resentful months later; Zhu, unburdened, was finally free.

05
Turning Point
Hezhou Crisis: Last Grinding Before Empire
A small city stuffed with 100,000+ men could not fight—only negotiate. Sun Deyay agreed to leave; Zhu escorted his soldiers outside the gates. Guo Zixing—unable to resist old hatred—arrested Sun inside the city, dooming Zhu outside. Sun's men seized Zhu; Xu Da volunteered to trade himself for Zhu. Guo, resentful over letting Sun escape, fell ill and died within a month—narrow-mindedness, historians agree, prevents great achievement. Zhu was finally unburdened.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Zhu's capital-building journey proves: military strength grows through bold strategy, talent through sharp judgment, but true reach depends on character. Yielding 30,000 troops was not weakness—it was strength through magnanimity, transcending the petty suspicions of the era. When all old burdens are shed, real empire begins.
