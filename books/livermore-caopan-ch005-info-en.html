01
Core Concept

What Are Pivotal Points

Pivotal points are the critical psychological price levels where trends launch — especially integer levels like 50, 100, 200, and 300. Once effectively breached, they almost invariably trigger straight-line rapid movements. Livermore calls these "the critical psychological moment marking the beginning of a move." Trade at pivotal points, and you never have to worry about losses.

If a stock does not behave as it should after crossing a pivotal point — no rapid advance, instead an oversupply of stock — that is a danger signal demanding immediate attention.

02
Signal Recognition

Dual Signals: Breakthrough Confirmed & Failed

Using the concrete example of $40→$45→$49½, Livermore reveals the two classic trading signals around pivotal points — the market gives both positive entry signals and negative exit signals, provided you have the patience to wait.

✅ Failed to Break Pivotal Point

Price rallies 3 points from the latest downside reaction low — buy immediately. The market's refusal to break the pivotal point signals exhaustion of selling pressure and the birth of a new trend.

⚠️ Broke Pivotal Point but Under 3 Pts

Price dips below $40 but by less than 3 points, then rallies back to $43 (pivotal point + 3) — also buy. The inability to sustain the break confirms the uptrend instead.

🔑
Core Rule: If the market validates the new trend decisively, price will continue rising past the next pivotal point at $49½ — and exceed it by 3 points or more.

05
Trading Discipline

The Trader's Path of Discipline

Patience and discipline are the core of the pivotal-point method. Livermore confesses: "Every time I lost patience and traded recklessly instead of waiting for the pivotal point, I always lost money."

✅ The Right Way

Wait patiently for pivotal points
Discover opportunities independently
Watch closely for danger signals
Maintain records by hand
Profit through your own judgment

❌ The Wrong Way

Trade recklessly without patience
Rely on tips or others' advice
Ignore danger signals
Neglect price record study
Depend on others for guidance

💡
Livermore's Advice: You might also profit through tips or others' guidance — but you will find that independently earned profits bring far greater satisfaction. If you discover opportunities on your own, trade independently, wait patiently, and watch for danger signals, you will enter a virtuous cycle of thinking.

Consider the anecdote: a lady pestered Livermore at a party for a tip. He recommended Cerro de Pasco (which had just broken its pivotal point). The stock rose 15 points in a week. When he called to advise selling, she admitted she had never bought it — "wanted to see if his tip was accurate first." Such is the fickle world of tips.

🔑
Key Takeaway

The Essence of the Pivotal Point Method: The market will, at the proper time, give you both positive entry signals and negative exit signals — if only you have the patience to wait. By combining the time element with price records and maintaining them by hand, you will discover pivotal points and use them to build positions. When a stock lacks follow-through after crossing a pivotal point, the market easily reverses — close your position immediately. Studying pivotal points is like mining gold — incredibly fascinating, and the profits you earn independently will bring unmatched satisfaction.
