Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance · Ch.19 'The Dilemma of Subject-Object: The Birth of the Trinity'

Quality is neither objective nor subjective — it creates subjects and objects

A Bozeman English professor poses a dilemma: Does undefined Quality exist in the things we observe, or does it exist only subjectively in the observer's mind? It's a bull's two horns — answer either way and you're impaled. Phaedrus, trained in logic, knows three ways to refute a dilemma. He analyzes each: Left horn (if Quality is objective, why can't instruments detect it?), Right horn (if Quality is subjective, it's 'just what you like?' — he seizes on the word 'just,' recognizing it as Squareness trying to devalue pleasure). Finally, he enters territory unprecedented in Western thought: Quality belongs neither to mind nor to matter. It is independent of both. Quality is not an object — it is an event, the event at which subject becomes aware of object. And most importantly, Quality is the cause of subjects and objects, not their effect. 'Quality is like a sun — it creates subjects and objects; they are subordinate to it.'

01 T 01 The Bull's Two Horns: The Dilemma Trap
The professor asks: 'Does this undefined Quality exist in the things we observe? Or does it exist only subjectively in the mind?' In Greek, 'dilemma' originally means the two horns of a fierce bull about to attack. If Phaedrus says Quality is objective, he is impaled on the left horn — how do you measure it with scientific instruments? If he says it's subjective, the right horn gets him — it's merely a self-bestowed title. The professors' eyes hold benevolent smiles. But Phaedrus knows there are three rigorous ways to refute a dilemma: attack the left horn, attack the right horn, or deny that subjective and objective are the only choices.

02 R 02 Refuting the Left Horn: Critique of Scientific Materialism
If Quality is objective, why do scientific instruments always fail to detect it? Phaedrus reflects: Locke once said you can only understand a thing's quality, nothing else. Scientists fail to detect Quality because Quality is ALL they detect. But this isn't enough — Quality observed in the classroom differs from the physical properties measured in labs. Physical properties are measurable; Quality (excellence, value, goodness) is not. He attacks scientific materialism with reductio ad absurdum: take the number zero — Greeks and Romans didn't know zero. Is zero therefore 'unreal'? If everything unmeasurable is unimportant, the entire edifice of science collapses.

03 R 03 Refuting the Right Horn: The Trick of the Word 'Just'
The right-horn argument: 'Quality is just what you like.' This infuriates Phaedrus — were great artists like Raphael and Beethoven merely expressing what people like? He finds the crux: the word 'just.' Why is Quality 'just' what you like? 'Just' is a contemptuous tone, contributing nothing to the sentence. Remove 'just,' and it becomes: 'Quality is what you like' — the meaning transforms entirely, into a harmless fact. 'Just' implies: what makes you happy is not good, or at least unimportant. This is the very essence of Squareness: training you to despise your own preferences, to become a better slave.

04 T 04 The Birth of the Trinity: Mind · Matter · Quality
Phaedrus escapes the left horn (Quality is not objective) and avoids the right horn (Quality does not exist solely in the mind). He enters territory unprecedented in Western thought — the region BETWEEN the two horns. Quality belongs to neither mind nor matter; it is independent of both. He hums softly in the stairwell: 'Holy, holy, holy... a trinitarian existence.' Further: Quality is not an object, it's an EVENT — the event at which subject becomes aware of object. But the crucial final leap: Quality is not the RESULT of subject-object meeting; Quality is the CAUSE — it creates subjects and objects.

💡 Key Takeaway

The professors corner Phaedrus with a dilemma: Is Quality objective or subjective? This has been Western philosophy's deadlock for centuries. Phaedrus dismantles the left horn with logic (just because scientific materialism can't measure it doesn't mean it doesn't exist) and dissolves the right horn with rhetoric (the word 'just' is Squareness's trick). He finally enters unprecedented philosophical territory: Quality is neither subjective nor objective — it is the SOURCE that creates both. 'Quality is like a sun; it does not revolve around subjects and objects. Subjects and objects are created BY it.' At this very moment, he and Chris both reach the summit.

Source: Robert M. Pirsig, Zen and the Art of Motorcycle Maintenance
