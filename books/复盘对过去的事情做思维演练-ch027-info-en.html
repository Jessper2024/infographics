FuPan · Chapter 27「Team FuPan」

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer Team FuPan is a multi-person truth-seeking discussion, requiring venue, time, and coordination. Yet team FuPan often falls into two extremes — silent paralysis (no one speaks) or blame-shifting battles (responsibility domino). How to make team FuPan a 'victorious, united assembly'? The answer: build a positive FuPan culture, define three roles (Facilitator·Questioner·Narrator), and follow a clear 8-step process.

Team FuPan is a discussion, not a lecture — requiring everyone's intelligence to approach truth. In reality, it often falls into silence (no one speaks, eyes averted) or noisy blame-shifting (the responsibility domino: Sales→Design→Finance→Procurement→Russian mine). Root causes include Chinese cultural inhibition of self-expression, factionalism, and lack of a healthy debate culture. When team FuPan fails, the greatest responsibility lies with the FuPan culture, not the review itself.

01
Constraints
The Cost of Team FuPan: Why It Cannot Happen Anytime
Team FuPan requires multiple people, hence venue (undisturbed space) and time (coordination of schedules). Behind both lies cost. Therefore, team FuPan cannot and should not happen anytime — it must occur after project completion, at key milestones, or when major questions arise. It is a discussion, not a lecture; it requires everyone's intelligence.
⏰

Right Timing

After project completion / Key milestones / Major questions arise. Not ad-hoc — ensure sufficient preparation and value.

🎯

Core Nature

Discussion, not lecture. Goal is collective intelligence — reaching consensus close to truth.

02
Silence Trap
Why No One Speaks: Cultural Roots of the Silence Trap
The author witnessed a bid-loss FuPan: after the manager invited free discussion, eyes swept the room — no eye contact returned. Some buried heads, some stared. After ~1 minute of silence, forced roll-call began. Former HP CEO Carly Fiorina found executives 'polite but not candid.' Chinese culture's 'tall poppy syndrome' and lack of issue-focused debate tradition hinder team FuPan speaking.
"They were polite but not candid. No one would directly contradict others, yet faces showed suppressed anger, and afterward they would report on each other. Underperforming bureaucracies always end up appearing harmonious."
—— Carly Fiorina, former HP CEO
03
Blame Domino
Responsibility Domino: Sales→Design→Finance→Procurement→Russian Mine
A year-end loss FuPan revealed the classic blame-shifting chain: Sales→Lack of new products (Design)→Reduced R&D budget (Finance)→Procurement squeezed budget (Procurement)→Raw material price hike→Russian mine earthquake. A complete domino pushed responsibility to a remote mine — no one accountable. Silence and noisy blame-shifting share one origin: poor FuPan culture.

04
Factionalism
The Cover-Up Game of Hidden Factions: The Real Case of A & B
The author witnessed a classic scene: A (Knowledge Research) and B (Program Promotion) formed a hidden faction. When the boss questioned B why a concept was not promoted, A jumped in to take blame. B then provided 'justification' for A's actions. Responsibility shifted entirely to 'competitors.' Truth: B was busy with visa procedures and forgot the task. Hidden faction cover-up rendered FuPan completely ineffective.
🛡️

Overt Faction (Department)

Open defense of department, with legitimate cover — praised by leadership, resources tilted. Every department head wants members to defend the department.

🕵️

Hidden Faction (Alliance)

Appears as support and corroboration — mutual point support, rebuttal of attacks, sharing blame — diverting FuPan completely from truth.

05
Success Framework
Three Roles + 8 Steps: The Success Framework for Team FuPan
Successful team FuPan requires three elements: 1) Positive FuPan culture — truth-seeking assembly, not reckoning or scapegoat hunt; 2) Three role divisions — Facilitator (ensures direction), Questioner (guides thinking), Narrator (describes events + answers questions); 3) Clear process — Review Goals→Compare Results→Narrate Process→Self-Analysis→Collective Questioning→Summarize Patterns→Case Corroboration→Archive FuPan.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The core challenge of team FuPan lies not in method or process — Lenovo's 8-step method is sufficiently clear — but in culture and human nature. When team FuPan fails, the root cause is often not 'this particular session' but the enterprise's FuPan culture. FuPan cannot be a reckoning assembly or a blame-shifting session — it must be a truth-seeking assembly. When everyone feels 'safe' during FuPan, the three roles perform their duties, and the 8 steps proceed steadily, team FuPan truly delivers its value: 're-examine what was done and discover patterns.'
