Finance professionals bridge the entire data flow—from frontline raw data to management decision reports. This chapter identifies the core dilemma: constrained by time, business knowledge, and management scope, finance easily falls into data silos. The solution isn't advanced technology or complex functions—it's first-principles thinking. Through data comparison (this week vs. best week, internal benchmarking), layer-by-layer dissection (financial numbers → business insights → improvement measures), cross-departmental collaboration, and knowledge multiplier effects, data is truly transformed into decision-driving force.

Comparison · Benchmarking
Data Comparison: Uncover Hidden Problems from Relative Position
Relying solely on business departments isn't enough—finance must proactively engage. Through data comparison, understand your position relative to industry benchmarks. Internal benchmarking is most common: this week vs. last week may look \"not bad,\" but adding the \"best week\" comparison exposes 6 products with yield rates below historical best. Three comparison tools: vertical (vs. last week), horizontal (vs. best week), industry benchmark.

Labor Efficiency Variance: Truth Hidden Behind Surface Numbers

04
Dissection · Translation
From Numbers to Business: Don't Let Financial Jargon Hijack Data Value
Bosses don't like financial reports because most just go from numbers to numbers. Pure number analysis can mislead: \"Total sales up 6% sequentially\" looks nice, but breaking it down reveals \"shipments +9%, transfer pricing −3%\"—so materials and expense analysis should anchor to the +9% shipment figure. Finance must translate numbers to business: financial numbers → business insights → cross-department improvement measures.

05
Talent · Cross-Functional
Cross-Functional Finance Talent: Let Engineers & IT Be Your Insiders
To understand raw source materials and trace causes layer by layer, finance teams need to go deep or even rotate into business departments. A more efficient approach: embed engineers and IT professionals within the finance department. When unfamiliar technical territory is reached, these \"insiders\" work magic. The future of finance is not specialist but cross-functional talent—fluent in both finance and diverse business knowledge.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Data-driven decision-making doesn't need advanced technology—the core is a shift to first-principles thinking. Through three-dimensional comparison (vertical · horizontal · benchmark), build data correlation systems; through layer-by-layer dissection, translate financial numbers into business insights and improvement actions; through cross-functional collaboration, give the finance team the eyes of engineers and IT. Knowledge produces multiplier effects—the more you accumulate, the more cross-connections burst forth.
