Fupan · Chapter 21「From Shooting Blind to Aimed Fire」

Through classic cases — Lenovo's "sell first, buy back later" mobile strategy, Zhou Hongyi's 3721 failure, and the secretary who mistook phone calls for the goal — this chapter demonstrates: most failures aren't from lack of direction or effort, but from drifting off course mid-journey. Liu Chuanzhi's "step back to see the picture," Lenovo's "extreme purposefulness," and Sun Taoran's "from shooting blind to aimed fire" — all three converge on fupan's direction-calibration function: review as you act, always lock on target.

01
Strategic Calibration
Sold for $100M, bought back for $200M: Lenovo's mobile deal as precision aiming after fupan
In 2008 Lenovo sold Lenovo Mobile for $100M; in 2009 bought it back for $200M — seemingly a $100M loss, but in reality two precise decisions after fupan. The sale: Lenovo's core PC business faced immense internationalization pressure, couldn't spare focus for mobile; fupan led to refocusing on core business. The buyback: iPhone had ignited the smartphone revolution, iPad revealed convergence; fupan showed owning mobile was essential for the future. HP entered mobile in 2012, confirming the buyback logic. Each step was "stepping back to see the picture" at the strategic level.

02
Lenovo's Methodology
Extreme purposefulness: for every action, ask "what is the purpose?" — never deviate from the higher-level goal
Lenovo's methodology has two core weapons: fupan + extreme purposefulness. For every action, ask: what is its purpose? Does this purpose align with a higher-level requirement? By relentlessly questioning purpose layer by layer, you firmly grasp the direction and ultimately achieve the goal. Fupan plays the calibration role — checking whether each step's purpose is correct and direction is accurate, correcting deviations immediately. Together, they form the complete methodology of "aimed fire."
🎯

🔄

Fupan (the calibrator)

03
Cautionary Tale
3721's collapse: eyes only on rivals, not users — the classic lesson of forgetting business = human nature
When Zhou Hongyi reviewed 3721's failure, he realized: it wasn't rivals who defeated him, it was users. 3721 pioneered address-bar search, installing its client through pop-up windows. But for users clicking "NO," it kept popping up on every new page — hugely irritating. Worse, 3721, CNNIC, and Baidu fought wars inside users' computers — deleting each other's clients without user knowledge. Three companies sued each other while users' PCs became battlefields. Zhou's painful lesson: business is about human nature; you must respect user interests and experience. This insight directly shaped Qihoo 360.
💡
Good start
Address-bar search innovation, simplified internet for everyday people, brought Zhou his first fortune
💥
Fatal mistake
Three-way client-deletion war with CNNIC and Baidu on user computers. Users became the battlefield.
🧠
Fupan insight
Business = human nature. Rivals don't defeat you — losing users' hearts defeats you.
04
Common Pitfall
Mistaking means for ends: the secretary who kept calling and forgot why she was dialing
"The boss asked his secretary to contact a client about meeting, required a reply by 5 PM. At 5 PM she said: 'I called him, no one answered.'" This classic dialogue exposes the most common workplace deviation: mistaking the means (calling) for the goal (scheduling a meeting). The boss pressed: did you text? Weibo DM? Email? She hadn't tried any — because calling was her imagined "goal." JDB vs. Wanglaoji employees fighting, Lianjia vs. Woaiwojia staff brawling — all the same error: mistaking competitive tactics for the goal, forgetting the goal is winning customers, not beating rivals.
❌

Blind shooting (means substituting for ends)

Call → no answer → idle wait → try nothing else. The act of "calling" became the entire mission.

✅

Aimed fire (locked on target)

Call → text → Weibo DM → email → IM message. All channels serve one goal: reach the client.

Ultimate Mindset
Sun Taoran: entrepreneurship is blind shooting — fupan is critical to turn it into aimed fire
Lakala CEO Sun Taoran said: "Entrepreneurship is a process of shooting blind. In this process, fupan is crucial. Review as you go, constantly reflect on the process — only then can you effectively extract lessons from success and failure, making the past valuable for the future, turning it into momentum for continued growth." From blind shooting to aimed fire: with a plan, with steps, with crosshairs, with results. Fupan turns every action into aiming practice, continuously calibrating, continuously refining.

🔑 Key Takeaway
"People fail not because they lack direction or effort, but because midway through, they suddenly find they've deviated from the planned path." Fupan is your GPS navigation — helping you step back to see the whole picture, checking whether each step has drifted off course, ensuring means always serve ends rather than substitute for them. From Lenovo's sell-first-buy-back-later, to 3721's bitter lesson, to the secretary's classic blunder — all cases point to one conclusion: review as you go to transform from shooting blind to aimed fire.
