01
Materials · Quality Cost
Quality Cost Causality: Prevention First, Losses Later
The best control is front-end control. When sufficient investment is made in prevention, later costs for inspection, rework, and claims decrease. Prevention costs are proactive; inspection costs, internal losses, and external losses are reactive. A well-controlled enterprise shows two trends: rising prevention cost share and falling total quality cost—causality perfectly embodied in the data.

Labor · Efficiency
Labor Efficiency & Overtime: The Truth of Positive Correlation
Labor efficiency and overtime should be positively correlated—time spent should be proportional to results. In reality, more overtime does not equal higher efficiency. By comparing output hours (units × standard hours) against paid hours (headcount × attendance hours), you can precisely calculate efficiency and build internal positive-correlation data sets to pinpoint resource consumption anomalies.
📊

Output Hours

100 units × 10 std hrs = 1,000 hours, reflecting actual value-creating labor

⏱️

Paid Hours

10 people × 167 hrs = 1,670 hours, efficiency = 1,000/1,670 = 59.9%

03
Overhead · Bottleneck
Bottleneck Resource Allocation Logic
When the least-invested "pressing station" becomes the bottleneck, the most-invested "testing station" is forced to idle—this is the greatest resource efficiency waste. Core financial logic: letting expensive equipment wait for cheap equipment is foolish. The bottleneck should appear at the most expensive equipment, forcing managers to prioritize solving its efficiency issues—this is the optimal resource configuration.
❌

Wrong Configuration

Bottleneck at cheap station (press 650/hr) → expensive station (test 800/hr) forced idle → "unrealized potential capacity"

✅

Right Configuration

Bottleneck at expensive station → forces management to improve it → drives entire line capacity upward

04
Negotiation · Data Leverage
Data-Driven Cross-Functional Collaboration & Supply Chain Gaming
Externally, the company is one entity, not isolated soldiers. Overall advantage is backed by data—when procurement brings finance-prepared payment records to negotiation, the 2.3-day weighted average delay instantly rebuts the supplier's "you always pay late" accusation. Internally, finance should bring procurement and sales together to share best practices, using data to connect the entire value chain.
Purchasing: "Can you go cheaper?"
Supplier: "Your finance always pays late—I've been fronting the money."
Purchasing: "Per our data, we've made early payments several months; weighted average is only 2.3 days late."
→ Data silences the supplier. Negotiation confidence comes from the finance back office.
05
Mindset · Data Wisdom
Big Data Is a Mindset Issue, Not a Technical One
German insurers in the 1990s already used dozens of questions—"have children?" "own a garage?"—to profile driver risk. This is a mindset issue, not a technology one. Data is like low-hanging fruit on the ground—reach down and pick it. Finance professionals must first establish a "data as asset" awareness, starting with basic ERP data and progressively building data-correlation insight.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Data wisdom is the essence of finance. Approach from Materials-Labor-Overhead: use the quality cost model to establish prevention-first causal logic, use labor efficiency metrics for positive correlation validation and anomaly detection, use bottleneck analysis for optimal resource configuration. When finance participates in every negotiation and decision armed with data, numbers truly turn into gold.
