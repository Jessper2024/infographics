How to Trade in Stocks · Ch.7 "The $3,000,000 Profit"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer In summer 1924, wheat reached a pivotal point and Livermore patiently entered in two tranches. But he took profits too early, missed the continuation, then courageously re-entered at higher prices. How to accurately identify the trend's end and short massively? The answer lay in cross-verification with the correlated rye market — when rye stopped leading wheat, it was the danger signal.

This chapter documents Livermore's most brilliant campaign: from patiently waiting for the pivotal point to enter 10 million bushels of wheat in two tranches, to prematurely taking 25 cents profit then courageously admitting error and re-entering at higher prices, to detecting divergence in the correlated rye market as a danger signal, to decisively establishing a 15-million-bushel short position, ultimately netting over $3 million in the panic decline. This victory was not the product of luck — it was the perfect execution of the "Pivotal Point Law + Correlated Market Cross-Verification + Patient Holding Until Signals Appear" system.

In summer 1924, after wheat reached the pivotal point, he entered in two tranches of 5 million bushels each — the second purchase being considerably harder to execute, precisely proving the market was bracing for a strong move.

02
Human Weakness
Premature Profit-Taking, Courageous Re-Entry
After banking 25 cents per bushel, Livermore liquidated — but the market advanced another 20 cents in days. His searing self-reflection: "Why did I fear losing something I had never really possessed?" Yet unlike the cotton disaster, he didn't sulk and quit — he admitted his error and re-entered at higher prices with half his original position.

03
Cross-Verification
The Rye Market's Secret Warning Signal
Throughout the great bull market, rye had always led wheat by a step — but near the end, a critical divergence appeared: wheat recovered to within 3⅞ cents of its high, while rye only recovered to within 12⅛ cents of its high! Rye had stopped leading and was lagging instead. Livermore conducted three probing sales of rye — the third drop wasn't followed by a bounce, confirming the decline had genuine momentum.

04
Execution
Massive Short: The 15-Million-Bushel Epic Campaign
With the signal confirmed, Livermore launched without hesitation: Step 1, sold 5M bushels driving price from $2.01 to $1.99; Step 2, built to 15M bushels after breaking $2.00; Step 3, March 16 close at $1.64½; net profit over $3 million. This also proves short-sellers are natural "shock absorbers" — those holding short positions become active buyers during panics, stabilizing the market.

Looking Ahead
The "Semi-Investor" — The Future Belongs to the Patient and Well-Informed
Livermore prophesied: the old-time speculator's day is gone. The Commodity Exchange Authority now limits individuals to 2 million bushels, and stock short-selling rules restrict scale — campaigns of this magnitude can't be replicated. The future belongs to "semi-investors" who don't rush to accumulate enormous profits quickly, but operate only at pivotal psychological moments, ultimately capturing a higher percentage of profit from market movements of every size.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Chapter 7 presents the highest expression of Livermore's trading philosophy — from patiently waiting at pivotal points to enter, to courageously admitting error and re-entering after premature profit-taking; from detecting divergence signals in the correlated rye market, to decisively shorting wheat at massive scale; from exploiting short-selling for enormous profits, to soberly reflecting on human weakness. This $3 million victory was the perfect execution of "Pivotal Point Law + Correlated Market Cross-Verification + Patient Holding Until Signals Appear."
