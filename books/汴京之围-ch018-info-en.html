Siege of Bianjing · Chapter 16 "Women in War"

Men's Crimes Paid by Women · Using Women's Bodies to Repay Men's Debts Gold and silver ran short, and the emperor signed a pact to sell all women. Imperial princesses, consorts, clan daughters, and palace maids were priced like commodities. Consort Maode was drugged and delivered to the Jurchen camp; chaste women were impaled on iron poles and displayed. The most detailed report on the imperial harem turned out to be the inventory Kaifeng Prefecture handed over to the Jurchen army. Bianjing was not saved by the emperor — it was the women and Zhang Bangchang who together pulled the city back from the abyss.

The most harrowing scene of the Jingkang Humiliation: when gold and silver could not be raised, Emperor Qinzong signed a gamble agreement with the Jurchen army — using the bodies of royal women as discounted payment for the war indemnity. Imperial princesses and consorts were priced at 1,000 gold ingots each, clan daughters at 500, collateral daughters at 200... a total of 11,506 women were priced like commodities. Consort Maode was disguised as a singing girl and sent to Wolibu's camp; three chaste women were impaled on iron poles and displayed for three days; Consort Xin took her own life, Consort Yun took her own life, and Princess Renfu died of illness in the Jurchen camp. Kaifeng Prefecture, with bureaucratic hyper-efficiency, registered and dispatched every woman of the harem. Their fate proves one thing: in the collapse of an empire, women are always the final form of payment.

01
The Bet Agreement
Gold and Silver Ran Out: The Emperor Signed a Pact to Sell All Women
On the 22nd day of the first month, Xiao Qing negotiated a fatal condition with Wu Jian and Mo Chou. The Jurchen demanded one million ingots of gold and five million ingots of silver as war indemnity, but Kaifeng Prefecture's actual capacity fell far short. The agreement stipulated: if gold and silver could not be raised within ten days, all women of the Zhao imperial clan would be offset at fixed prices — princesses and consorts at 1,000 gold ingots each, clan daughters at 500, collateral daughters at 200, clan wives at 500 silver ingots, collateral wives at 200, and noblewomen at 100. Even more tragic: as early as the eighth day of the first month, the Jurchen had already begun demanding women — 500 common women and 500 entertainers as "marriage alliance" gifts for the two commanders. The emperor was forced to sign his consent.

02
Consort Maode
One Man's Lust That Changed History: Wolibu and Consort Maode
The fate of Consort Maode Zhao Fujin directly determined the survival of Northern Song. Wolibu had long heard from envoys that the Zhao princesses were beautiful; after a Cai family maid confirmed that Consort Maode was the most beautiful, he repeatedly demanded a marriage alliance. On the 28th day of the first month, Kaifeng Prefecture disguised Consort Maode as a singing girl and sent her to the Jurchen camp — when she saw Wolibu, her face turned waxen; she was drugged by her former maid Lady Li and sent into his chambers. Wolibu had done this behind Nianhan's back. On the fourth day of the second month, news leaked and Nianhan flew into a rage. Having previously agreed to preserve the Zhao clan as a vassal state, he now changed his mind and decided to completely depose the emperor. One man's lust rewrote the fate of two dynasties.

03
Hell in the Camp
Hell in the Jurchen Camp: The Death of Chaste Women and the Conquerors' Revelry
The Jurchen camp became a collective nightmare for the Zhao women. On the seventh day of the second month, Nianhan ordered the palace consorts to change into entertainer costumes and serve wine. Three palace women refused and were beheaded; another stabbed herself in the throat with an arrowhead. In Wolibu's camp, the chaste women Lady Zhang, Lady Lu, and Lady Cao were impaled on iron poles and stood before the camp gate — it took three days for them to die. Wolibu used this to warn the consorts and princesses yet to arrive. The thirteen-year-old Consort Zhu, facing the humiliation of "you were bought for a thousand ingots of gold," defiantly asked: "Who sold me? Who took the gold?" On the 16th day of the second month, as the women were assembled, the commanders ordered them stripped of Han dress and forced into Jurchen clothing; pregnant women had their pregnancies terminated by physicians. From this day on, the death roll inside the Jurchen camp grew longer: Consort Xin took her own life, Princess Renfu died of illness on the 25th of the second month, Princess Xianfu on the 28th, Princess Baofu on the 7th of the third month.

04
Peace & Harmony Banquet
The Peace & Harmony Banquet: Emperor and Empress Forced to Watch Wives and Daughters Divided
On the 18th day of the second month, Wolibu held the "Peace and Harmony Banquet" at Liujia Temple, pushing humiliation to its extreme. Nianhan, Wolibu, and eight other top Jurchen commanders sat at the same table as the Retired Emperor, Empress Dowager Zheng, Emperor Qinzong, and Empress Zhu. Xiebao asked Wolibu to summon twenty consorts and thirty-two entertainers to serve wine — these had all once been Emperor Huizong's concubines. When the two emperors and empresses tried to leave, Nianhan stopped them, and they reluctantly sat through to the end. Before the banquet dispersed, Wolibu requested Consort Fujin be given to Sheyema as wife; the Retired Emperor refused, citing "one woman cannot marry two husbands." Nianhan erupted in fury and ordered each commander on the spot to pick two consorts to take away. Empress Dowager Zheng knelt and begged Nianhan not to take her nephew's wife — Nianhan nodded. This was the sole shred of dignity she managed to preserve.

05
Harem Inventory
The Most Detailed Harem Inventory: Every Woman Finally Had a Name
Kaifeng Prefecture, with bureaucratic hyper-efficiency, turned Emperor Huizong's harem into a museum specimen. Because the women had to be transferred to the Jurchen army, Kaifeng Prefecture registered every woman of the imperial harem one by one. Only then did we learn that beyond five titled consorts, Emperor Huizong had thirty-one named concubines — Jin Nongyu, Chen Jiaozi, Yueli Chang'e, Shen Guanyin, Jin Qiuyue... — along with forty-one favored maids and sixty-seven ordinary maids. These enchanting names belonged to living, breathing human beings, normally submerged by the vast machinery of autocracy. The Jurchen invasion, paradoxically, brought them out from the corners of history and left a mark in the historical record — though that mark was nothing other than their bill of sale.

The Price of Women

51 consorts & princesses valued at 134,000 gold ingots — 2.5× the 49,500 ingots actually surrendered. Sales of women exceeded half the total indemnity.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Bianjing was not saved by its emperor — it was the women and Zhang Bangchang who together pulled the city back from the abyss. Emperor Qinzong used the bodies of royal women as discounted payment for war debts — 51 consorts and princesses plus over 11,000 women whose "sale price" accounted for more than half the indemnity. The darkest truth of the Jingkang Humiliation is this: when an empire collapsed, the male rulers made the most vulnerable group bear the final cost. And those concubines whose names we know — mere palace ornaments in life — were first remembered by history only because of the Jurchen invasion.
