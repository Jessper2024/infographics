Finally, it introduces Kondratiev wave theory to help readers understand how to position themselves amid the ebb and flow of cycles—moving from tip-driven to system-driven, and from a gambler's mindset to compounding thinking, marks the essential path to maturity for every ordinary investor.

01
Tip Trap
Money Earned by Luck, Lost by Chasing Tips
The single greatest source of retail investor losses: treating "tips" as everything while never asking the critical questions. During the 2020 stock market frenzy, only 30% of retail investors were profitable; after the bubble burst, fewer than 10% remained. People blindly worship tips but never ask: how many hands has this tip passed through? Is it already priced into the market? When should you exit? Where is the margin of safety? Technical, fundamental, and macro schools all have their merits—but what truly matters is building your own closed-loop investment system. Many successful business owners treat the stock market as a casino—"throw a few million in, if I lose it, no big deal"—and this very attitude destroys them. Graham taught us that markets do not reflect real value in real time; you need a framework. Value investing, in part, earns "compensation for enduring the torment." When Tencent split its shares to offer a 100-yuan entry point, most still thought it was expensive—because they didn't understand the moat of social networks. The hardest thing in the world is not discovery, but persistence and perseverance.

02
Temperament Gamble
Those Who Bet It All Make the Best Stories
Identical era, same opportunities—different temperament leads to radically different outcomes. The decade following China's 2001 WTO accession was the golden window. Xiaolin and Laowang co-founded a container logistics business with 40 trucks. Xiaolin was conservative, scaling back after 2005; Laowang went all-in and was crushed by the 2008 financial crisis. The conservative one—Xiaolin—won. Another pair: Xiao Xu joined eBay (EachNet) in 2005 at 400K/year, a decent career but never caught the biggest wave; Xiao Ke joined Tencent and ended up with over 100 million from stocks and Shenzhen property. One million Xiao Kes joined one million small companies—only one became Tencent. Tiny differences at the starting point, amplified by the compounding of time, produce vastly different life trajectories. A top-3 cold chain company in China started as vegetable vendors in the 90s who realized "freight costs more than the produce" and pivoted to logistics—cognition shapes choice, and choice shapes destiny.

03
Era Awareness
Personal Investment Reflections, 2022
The investment world underwent a fundamental transformation during the COVID era—liquidity dominates everything, old and new forces violently alternate. The Harvard virus report made the author feel how small humanity truly is—trends have broken free from any predictable boundary. NIO went from near-bankruptcy to a dramatic reversal; the investor who bought at $1 and sold at 3× felt worse than those who never bought at all. On the A-share battlefield, CATL and Tianqi Lithium saw prices go vertical—liquidity became the single most important short-term indicator. Bitcoin breached $60K; 15-minute 20% crashes vaporizing $20 billion became routine. A new generation of fund managers and retail investors entered simultaneously, while old strategies died rapidly. In the primary market, VCs scrambled madly for a seat at the table—the fear of missing out far exceeded the fear of losing. In such a turbulent era, individual investors must return to fundamental logic: understand the era's dominant theme, respect liquidity, manage emotions, and refuse to be harvested by extreme market volatility.

04
Investment Mindset
Five Core Principles: A Survival Guide for Ordinary Investors
The author distills five inviolable principles from years of investment practice, applicable to every ordinary investor. First, never do things where winning has limited upside but losing means total ruin—asymmetric risk must be avoided. Second, stay clear of liquidity traps—illiquid assets are always discounted. Third, rationally use emotional trading to gain a premium rather than being emotionally harvested—be the hunter, not the prey. Fourth, when higher-level people share investment logic, don't try to prove you're smarter—listen first, digest later. Fifth, your main career is your life's primary uptrend—building compounding effects in your professional domain is far more reliable than gambling in the secondary market. For ordinary Chinese people, the ultimate "Great Compounding" formula is: hold shares in China's best companies long-term (not short-term speculation), connect with insightful people, choose a non-automatable career with compounding effects, and become an expert.

05
Cycle Positioning
Fortune Depends on Kondratiev Waves: Find Your Place in the Cycle
"Fortune in life depends on Kondratiev waves"—Zhou Jintao's famous words are the underlying code of the entire investment world. Each Kondratiev wave has created a generation of immense wealth: Britain through steam and railways, the US and Europe through steel, electricity, and automobiles; now we stand at the tail of the current wave. The seven New Infrastructure domains—5G, UHV, intercity high-speed rail, EV charging stations, big data centers, AI, and industrial internet—form the foundation of the next wave. At the tail of a cycle, the optimal strategy is to stay "half a step behind the frontier thinkers"—two steps ahead is fatal; half a step ahead is just right. Real cases of adaptation: drone sky shows replacing fireworks, legal auction housing agents emerging as a new profession, modular prefab homes (one-week delivery, villa quality for under one million), restaurant equipment recycling companies profiting from COVID closures. In every cycle, find your entry point from subtle supply-demand dynamics—in our lifetime, we may only have two chances to catch the main uptrend. The trend toward extreme value-for-money will intensify as a new era of rational consumption arrives. The author himself abandoned an automotive engineering degree after calculating that the industry would reach saturation by his peak years—and pivoted to new media and branding.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The essence of investing is not "beating the market" but "beating yourself." For ordinary people, the ultimate formula for "Great Compounding" is not betting on a single ten-bagger stock but: holding shares in China's best companies long-term, connecting with insightful people, building compounding effects in a career that cannot be automated, and becoming an expert.
