The Gay Genius · Ch.7
"First Official Post in Fengxiang"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer "Life's journey, what is it like? It should be like a swan treading on snow — leaving claw-prints on the mud by chance." In Fengxiang, Su Dongpo prayed for rain with utter sincerity, debated with mountain spirits, and displayed a fiery vitality. How did a mundane first posting reveal his extraordinary character? Through sincere service, his wife's wisdom, and a spirit of fire that illuminated every post he held.

Su Dongpo's first official post at Fengxiang revealed his "fiery" spirit — he successfully prayed for rain for the people, eloquently debated logic with mountain gods, built the "Pavilion of Joyful Rain" and wrote its inscription to share happiness with the people, while also learning to read human character through the wise counsel of his wife Wang Fu. In late 1061, Su was appointed Assistant Magistrate of Fengxiang. His first taste of officialdom was not pleasant — far from the capital, buried in petty paperwork — but when drought struck, he sprang into action: climbing Mount Taibai to pray, writing a memorial to secure a dukedom for the mountain god, and debating logic with spirits as if he were a lawyer in court.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Fengxiang was the starting point of Su Dongpo's official career and a full display of his character: sincere service (praying for rain), eloquent debate (arguing with spirits), sharing joy through literature (Joyful Rain Pavilion), and humble acceptance of his wife's counsel (judging character). These four qualities — sincerity, eloquence, optimism, and humility — would define his forty years in office. As he wrote: "Life is like a swan treading on snow — leaving claw-prints on the mud by chance."
