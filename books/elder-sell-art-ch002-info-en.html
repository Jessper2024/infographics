Trading for a Living II: The Art of Selling · Chapter 2
"Trading Psychology and Risk Management"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer Traders own computers, software, and data — but technical tools alone don't guarantee success. Emotional swings destroy decisions, and undisciplined money management blows up accounts. How to build a safe trading system? Two pillars: five psychological disciplines + the 2% & 6% dual rules.

Mindset is your real trading tool — your emotions, expectations, and fears directly determine outcomes. Money management is your safety net — if trading is a tightrope walk, risk control is the net below that saves you when you fall. This chapter reveals two cornerstones: five disciplines that restrain the self, and the quantified 2% per-trade cap and 6% monthly circuit breaker. Used together, trading becomes remarkably safe — celebrate when you win, stay calm when you lose.

01
Core Insight
Your Mindset Is Your Core Trading Tool
Your emotions, expectations, and fears directly determine your trading outcomes. Excessive emotional swings will ruin you — decision-making must be clear and free of bias. Elder insists mastering personal emotions is the key. Disciplined traders treat emotions as signals, not commands. Emotional traders swing between fear and greed.
✅

Disciplined Trader

Executes a system-based plan with clear, unbiased decisions. Emotions are signals, not commands. Every trade builds replicable experience.

❌

Emotional Trader

Too afraid to enter, too greedy to exit. Copies others, abandons discipline under stress, trapped in self-punishment loops.

Risk Warning
Five Warning Signs of Doomed Traders
Markets generate endless temptations — certain traders have almost no chance. Elder identifies five patterns. If you recognize any, do not trade until you fix it. Addiction, trading under emotional distress, relying on others' opinions, the self-punishment loop, and emotional heavy positioning — each chips away at your edge.

04
Money Management
The 2% Rule: Iron Triangle of Single-Trade Risk
If trading is a tightrope walk, risk control is the safety net. The 2% rule is like a shark — a single deadly bite that you can guard against: risk no more than 2% of your account on any trade. The calculation: entry-minus-stop spread → 2% of account → max shares. A shark attack not only loses money but destroys trading confidence.

05
Circuit Breaker
The 6% Rule: Monthly Drawdown Circuit Breaker
Piranhas are more dangerous than sharks — they swarm and strip your account bite by bite. Most people add to losing positions trying to turn it around. The right move: stop trading and rest. The 6% rule mandates stopping all trading once monthly losses reach 6%. Lose ⅓ of capital, and you need a 50% gain just to break even — protecting capital beats chasing profit.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Trading success = Disciplined Psychology × Money Management. Mindset is your real tool. The 2% rule guards against shark-like single blows; the 6% rule protects against piranha-style slow bleed. Before every trade ask: does the 6% rule allow me to trade? How much have I lost this month? How much more can I afford to lose? Combined, they form an unbreakable safety system.
