The Gay Genius · Ch.9
"The Stubborn Prime Minister"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer "Heaven's mandate is not to be feared, public opinion is not to be followed, the laws of the ancestors are not to be used" — Su Dongpo's "Three Defiances" label became the most vivid portrait of the Stubborn Prime Minister. How did Wang Anshi go from "universally acclaimed" reformer to "Stubborn Prime Minister"? By branding all critics as "vulgar conformists," suppressing all dissent, until his most trusted ally, Lü Huiqing, betrayed him.

The Stubborn Prime Minister Wang Anshi took the "Three Defiances" as his creed — defying heaven, scorning public opinion, overturning tradition — and pushed through his new policies with Emperor Shenzong's backing, suppressing all opposition, alienating every honest friend, and promoting a clique of treacherous opportunists. The opposition forces were the finest political minds nurtured under Emperor Renzong's atmosphere of intellectual freedom — and the imbalance in their ranks was both pitiful and absurd. Without a proper party system, Song factional strife proceeded by the rule of "one faction in power, the other banished from the capital," raging until the dynasty's collapse.

01
Character Label
Three Defiances: The Stubborn Man's Creed
"Heaven is not to be feared, public opinion is not to be followed, ancestral laws are not to be used" — Su Dongpo's three-line label precisely captured Wang Anshi's entire political philosophy: challenge heaven, scorn public will, overthrow tradition, pitting one man's will against the entire world.
🔴

The Stubborn Man's Defiances

No fear of heaven · No regard for public opinion · No respect for ancestral laws. When a ruler believes he is righter than heaven, the people, and tradition — disaster nears.

🟢

Reasonable Reform Spirit

Respect heaven (public consent), heed all voices (wise counsel), adapt ancestral laws gradually (incremental reform). Wang negated all three.

Abandoned by All
Even His Own Brothers Stood Against Him
Most shocking of all, Wang Anshi's two younger brothers — Wang Anli and Wang Anguo — both stood with the opposition. Wang Anguo once quoted Confucius to his face. Wang Anshi alienated every friend; at the slightest disagreement, he banished them from court.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Wang Anshi became the "Stubborn Prime Minister" not because of his reforms themselves, but because of how he handled opposition: branding all critics as "conformists," identifying himself alone with "reform," suppressing censors, banishing friends, promoting villains. His binary thinking — if you don't support me, you're my enemy — not only destroyed the reforms but planted the seeds of factional strife that doomed the Northern Song. As Lin Yutang said, at the moment of collapse: "Lü Huiqing betrayed Wang Anshi, Wang Anshi betrayed the Emperor, the Emperor betrayed the people." An uncorrupt idealist became the victim of his own paranoia — that is the true tragedy of the Stubborn Prime Minister.
