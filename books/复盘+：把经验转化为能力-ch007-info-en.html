After-Action Review+ · Ch.7 "Getting Reviews Right"

Getting reviews right isn't easy — it requires navigating 25 pitfalls, mastering 3 core skills, and blending 12 "formula" factors

The U.S. Army's AAR Leader Guide states: the most critical element of an AAR is its spirit — an open, honest discussion of every detail, coupled with a strong desire to find opportunities for improvement. Drawing from Lenovo's practice, this chapter systematically presents 25 common pitfalls with countermeasures, 3 core skills (review facilitation, systems thinking, knowledge extraction), and 12 key success factors across 3 dimensions: participants, facilitators, and organizational context — helping you find the right "formula" for your organization.

LENOVO PRACTICE
Lenovo: 5 Misconceptions & 7 Key Success Factors
To get reviews right, you must first overcome 5 mindset traps and cultivate 7 key factors — this is the core finding from Lenovo's years of practice. Common misconceptions include: proving you're right, going through the motions, blaming others, shirking responsibility, and jumping to conclusions. Seven corresponding success factors are needed to guard against these traps.

The U.S. Army National Training Center uses three methods to reconstruct facts: assigning observers/controllers, using measurement instruments and recordings, and collective discussion. While most enterprises can't deploy extensive equipment, cultivating systems thinking capabilities can compensate — enabling teams to "see the whole" rather than getting lost in details.
💡 KEY INSIGHT
Getting reviews right isn't a one-meeting task — it's a systematic endeavor: you must identify and guard against 25 pitfalls, cultivate 3 core skills (facilitation, systems thinking, knowledge extraction), and blend 12 "formula" factors across participants, facilitators, and organizational context. As the U.S. Army puts it, the most critical element of a review is its spirit — openness, candor, and a hunger for improvement. Leaders must lead by example, embedding reviews as an organizational habit and culture, to truly transform experience into capability.
