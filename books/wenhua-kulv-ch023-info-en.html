Three meaningless characters pinned to his name for over a decade—until he decided to turn them into a teahouse

'Shi Yi Ge'—the byline of a Lu Xun research group established in 1972 on Zhou Enlai's directive—was weaponized into a notorious label pinned on Yu Qiuyu during the cultural subversion frenzy at the turn of the century. The instigator was a writer whose professional title application Yu had rejected; the amplifiers were Southern Weekly, Apple Daily, and other media. Yu abandoned self-defense, offered a full year's salary for any evidence—no one claimed it. After over a decade of searching for the 'stone,' he finally found the one 'Shi Yi Ge' article that had drawn attacks—it merely stated that Lu Xun had a secret reading room, consistent with what Zhou Jianren had publicly stated. He turned the three characters into a teahouse name: 'Urge them to be calm, and drink down these forty years of indescribable taste.'

01
Border Alarm
On the Pakistan-Afghanistan border, under the gun, an absurd accusation
The last winter of the twentieth century. Yu Qiuyu was 40,000 kilometers into his survey of ancient human civilizations, having reached the borderlands between South and Central Asia—'guns everywhere, terror at every step, life hanging by a thread.' That night on the Pakistan-Afghanistan border, a companion received a long-distance call and whispered: a Peking University student back home had published an article accusing him of joining a gang called something like 'Shi...' during the Cultural Revolution. Before the sentence finished, armed men approached—'that was the local gang.' After surviving and returning home, he discovered most Chinese newspapers were hyping the accusation. The name was 'Shi Yi Ge' (Stone One Song).
02
The Truth of Shi Yi Ge
A product of Premier Zhou Enlai's cultural reconstruction—the name of a Lu Xun research group
'Shi Yi Ge'—what was it? In October 1971, Zhou Enlai began cleaning up the Cultural Revolution's wreckage in Shanghai, launching cultural reconstruction. Because that year marked Lu Xun's 90th birthday, he required Chinese departments to resume classes starting with Lu Xun's works. Shanghai formed two groups: the Lu Xun Biography Writing Group at Fudan University (Yu Qiuyu was assigned but left halfway), and the Lu Xun Research Group at the Writers' Association—'Shi Yi Ge' was this latter group's name. Yu Qiuyu himself was unclear what it wrote, as he was hiding in Zhejiang's mountains at the time. Decades later, he finally found a 'Shi Yi Ge' article in a magazine: it said Lu Xun had a secret reading room where he read Marxist works—exactly what Zhou Jianren (Lu Xun's younger brother and then governor of Zhejiang) had publicly stated. It was never 'fabrication.'
03
Why Silence
Triply trapped—unable to defend, unwilling to hurt the elderly, unwilling to politically harm a student—silence was chosen
Yu Qiuyu abandoned self-defense for three reasons: ① the frenzy had begun—a hundred mouths could not clarify, only law could help, but that would take too much time. His conclusions from surveying civilizations, especially warnings about global terrorism, had to be published urgently; ② the actual Shi Yi Ge members were mostly elderly retired professors without the energy to debate—clearing his own name would require investigating and publishing their names, which would harm those old people; ③ to explain fully, he would have to reveal that both groups were established on Premier Zhou Enlai's directive—this would bring lifelong political harm to that Peking University student, who was just a babbling child at the time. So he chose silence, traveling the world to lecture on The Contemporary Predicaments of Great Civilizations, while Chinese readers everywhere asked: 'Shi Yi Ge?'

04
Reward and Interrogation
A full year's salary as reward for evidence; nine deadly questions to Southern Weekly
Yu Qiuyu published a 'reward' in the press: anyone who could produce evidence that he wrote even a single line of the kind of article attributed to him under the 'Shi Yi Ge' byline would receive his full annual salary. No one claimed it. Southern Weekly ignored the reward but published a photo of him with the accuser, Mr. Sun, using words like 'investigation' and 'examination' to create the impression of an 'ironclad case.' Yu Qiuyu posed nine questions to Southern Weekly's editor: Who authorized Sun to 'investigate'? Why did he keep 'materials' at home for 27 years? Are they originals or copies? Do they bear any institution's seal? Where are they now? Can I bring my lawyer and archive officials to see them?—'What appeared here was never a "historical question" from the start, but quite possibly a criminal case. Because forging documents, forging archives, stealing archives—these are major crimes in any country.'
05
The Stone's Gift
A three-character rumor gave me three unexpected gifts
The three characters 'Shi Yi Ge' brought Yu Qiuyu three gifts: ① True freedom—the official sphere knew it was rumor but feared the rumor-mongers, so they avoided him, freeing him effortlessly from all titles and posts, giving him almost all of his time; ② Understanding of the environment—contemporary cultural figures could see through it but chose not to, leading him to abandon all hope in cultural opinion. 'Exiled in every direction, I became independent. An independent ecology, independent thinking, independent discourse—from the smallest to the largest, from loneliness to the macroscopic'; ③ Strengthening—he adopted his 'Six No's' principle (no newspapers, no internet, no official posts, no meetings, no mobile phone, no inquiries), growing intimate with natural ecology and ancient giants. 'True strength is not rallying or following the crowd, but opposing the crowd, then influencing the crowd.' He decided to formally adopt the 'Shi Yi Ge' name for a teahouse: 'I will serve hot tea and coffee, pat their shoulders, urge them to be calm, and drink down these forty years of indescribable taste.'

🔑 Key Takeaway
Yu Qiuyu received three gifts from the 'Shi Yi Ge' affair: true freedom (shedding all titles), understanding of his environment (abandoning hope in cultural opinion), and greater strength ('True strength is not rallying the crowd, but opposing the crowd, then influencing the crowd'). At the end of the stone-searching road, he decided to turn those three characters into a teahouse, and invite everyone involved to a cup of tea.
