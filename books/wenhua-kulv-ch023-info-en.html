Cultural Perplexity · Ch.23 "The Road to Finding 'Stone'"

Three characters pinned on a man for two decades, yet no one could show what they wrote — an absurdist play of Chinese media culture

Core insight: The "Shi Yige" affair is a textbook case of collective slander spanning twenty years — zero evidence, zero investigation, zero institutional defense, only a carnival of media and literati conformity. The truth, as always, was laughably simple.

QuestionWhat exactly was "Shi Yige"? How could a meaningless pen name be sensationalized for twenty years with no one pursuing the facts?

Answer"Shi Yige" was a Lu Xun research group established in 1972 under Zhou Enlai's directive — completely unrelated to Yu Qiuyu. The media carnival needed no truth, only a target that could be attacked repeatedly with the written word. When Yu finally saw the "Shi Yige" article that had been most fiercely denounced, he found it was merely a paraphrase of Zhou Jianren's (Lu Xun's younger brother) recollection of Lu Xun's reading room.

1
Storm Origin

How did a hat come flying from nowhere?

At the end of the 20th century, while Yu Qiuyu was risking his life investigating ancient human civilizations on the Pakistan-Afghanistan border, a Peking University student at home accused him of belonging to the "Shi Yige" writing group — a storm whose absurdity required only a single sentence to begin.

Yu Qiuyu later learned the truth: the initiator, surnamed Sun, was a scholar whose title application Yu had rejected; the loudest amplifier, surnamed Gu, was someone who had once lavishly praised Yu and was rebuffed; the latecomer, surnamed Sha, was someone Yu had saved whose subsequent ultra-left statements made Yu sever ties. Three men, plus the media, ignited a nationwide wave.

Yu chose silence for three reasons: legal action would take too long, the elderly professors were too frail to defend themselves, and revealing the truth would politically harm the student, who was merely being manipulated.

2
Truth Revealed

What did "Shi Yige" actually do?

"Shi Yige" was a Lu Xun research group established under Zhou Enlai's 1972 directive, based at the Shanghai Writers' Association — its most damning "crime" was an article stating that Lu Xun had a secret reading room where he read Marxist works.

This fact had been publicly stated many times by Lu Xun's younger brother Zhou Jianren — who was then Governor of Zhejiang Province and Vice Chairman of the National People's Congress. Yu Qiuyu himself had visited that reading room. Chinese intellectuals of Lu Xun's era commonly read Marxist works; Lu Xun even sheltered Qu Qiubai, a wanted former CPC General Secretary, in his own home.

Yu Qiuyu discovered all this in 2010 when he came across a magazine article angrily denouncing a decades-old "fabrication" by "Shi Yige" — he had finally seen what this group's most offensive article was about.

3
Media Operation

How does the media manufacture an "ironclad case"?

Southern Weekend ignored Yu Qiuyu's public reward offer and instead published a photograph and "materials" from a man who claimed to have "investigated" Yu — devoting several full pages to an "investigation" that found nothing, but the impression left on readers was enough.

⚠️ Yu Qiuyu's counter-questions: Are these "materials" originals or copies? Do they bear any institutional seal? Why are they stored in a private home for 27 years instead of an archive? Is this an "investigation" or "document forgery"? — Nine questions, none answered.

Hong Kong's Apple Daily went further, claiming Yu "participated in the Gang of Four's writing group" and "attacked and killed people with his pen" — and the trigger for these attacks was Yu saying after the Wenchuan earthquake: "The Chinese people are among the very finest ethnic groups in humanity" and "The Chinese people solve everything through love alone."

4
Counter-Offer

What did twenty years of "stone-seeking" yield?

Yu Qiuyu publicly offered a reward: if anyone could produce evidence that he had written even one essay, paragraph, sentence, or word under the "Shi Yige" name that matched the accusations, he would pay his entire annual salary. For twenty years, no one has claimed it.

The road to finding "stone" was filled with dark humor: when he called a retired professor who had been in the group, the professor's wife pleaded on the phone not to bring it up — "My husband is frail, with severe hypertension. Mr. Yu, we can only let you bear this injustice."

In the end, Yu Qiuyu decided to formally adopt the "Shi Yige" name — planning to open a teahouse called "The Recovered Pavilion" and a café called "Poetry and Song," their walls covered with the slanderous articles as evidence.

5
Three Gains

Why did he say these characters brought good fortune?

Yu Qiuyu sincerely thanks the "Shi Yige" affair — these three characters gave him three gifts: genuine freedom, environmental awareness, and greater strength.

① Genuine Freedom

The official world kept its distance due to the rumors, freeing him from all titles and positions, granting nearly total free time.

② Environmental Awareness

He saw clearly the ecology where "a colleague's calamity becomes one's own safety certificate; a banished solitary bird becomes the sunset glow outside one's window."

③ Greater Strength

He adopted the "Six No's" principle, becoming intimate with nature and ancient giants, attaining total serenity.

"True strength is not rallying the crowd or following the crowd, but opposing the crowd and then influencing the crowd. 'Great courage resembles timidity.' 'Great compassion has no allies.'"

💡
Key Takeaway

When slander requires no evidence, media requires no verification, and the public requires no thought, a meaningless pen name can haunt a person for twenty years. The "Shi Yige" affair is not Yu Qiuyu's personal tragedy, but a symptom of China's cultural ecology: noise is written into textbooks, silence is taken as consent, and the truth has always been there — no one simply bothered to look down and see it.

Source: Yu Qiuyu, Cultural Perplexity (文化苦旅), Changjiang Literature & Art Press, 2014
