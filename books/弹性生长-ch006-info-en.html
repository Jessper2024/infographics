弹性生长 · Ch.6
「Will the Internet Industry Stop Being Lucrative?」

From Expansion to Contraction, Software Is Still the Boundless Frontier

📖 Chapter Overview

After civil engineering, the author turns to the internet industry. Previously, internet companies pursued scale, not profit—crazy expansion created an ‘arms race’ of high salaries. But antitrust enforcement and economic slowdown changed the logic: abandon monopoly dreams, cut unprofitable projects, ‘spread the chill to everyone.’ Yet the author judges: the internet won’t ‘civil-engineerize.’ Software is the true boundless frontier—just without the wild ride.

🔍 Deep Dive
From Scale Wars to Cash-Flow Focus
The internet industry’s logic has fundamentally changed. Companies used to expand frantically into each other’s turf, launching countless projects hoping for disruptive hits. Now it’s contraction—abandoning monopoly dreams, cutting unprofitable projects. Ren Zhengfei’s essay: ‘shift from expansion focus to cash-flow focus,’ ‘don’t tell stories to deceive the company.’
Big Tech Career Trilogy: Tech → Relationships → Factions
Once a company passes 3,000 employees, career trajectories crystallize: early stage—stand out through technical skill in the first 3 years; middle stage—become a leader’s trusted deputy by years 3-5; late stage—around year 10 in middle management, your fate ties to one senior leader. Want to switch factions? Years of built trust won’t accept you.
Non-Linear Growth: Enduring to the Inflection Point
Our intuition says growth is linear—we quit when efforts don’t show results. But almost all growth is non-linear and explosive: quiet early phase, then sudden exponential breakout. Most people in school, without pressure, quit before the breakout. At work, forced to persist, they finally break through—but one beat behind. Such people may lag in everything.
Why the Internet Won’t ‘Civil-Engineerize’
Civil engineering has physical limits—you can’t build ten Beijing-Shanghai railways or rebuild cities every decade. But the internet has no physical bounds—phone apps are rewritten every 4-5 years. Software ‘eats the world’—cars, homes, entertainment all need massive transformation. The more you use software, the more you need. It will integrate with physical industries, growing smarter.
Talent = Enjoyment + Intelligence
Often what we call ‘talent’ is really enjoyment—some people aren’t smarter at something but do it with joy, reaching high levels anyway. If smart AND joyful, that’s a cheat-code life—big tech’s senior technical leaders are all this type. If work feels like visiting a grave, admit you’re ordinary and just cruise along.
💡 Key Takeaway

The internet’s wild salary era is over, but it won’t become the next civil engineering. Software is the true boundless frontier—no physical limits, continuously generating vast jobs, increasingly integrating with the physical world. The key: take every step solidly, because an industry rising doesn’t mean everyone inside thrives. The internet remains the most poor-friendly profession.
