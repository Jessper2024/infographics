FuPan · Chapter 28「FuPan of Others」

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer FuPan of others is the most cost-effective review — others expend resources to perform before you while you gain experience at zero cost. Yet it faces the natural limitation of incomplete information. How to overcome this and convert FuPan of others into competitive advantage? The keys: use the key-point method to break through information barriers, choose the right competitive strategy (from copycat to pattern mastery), avoid rushing to conclusions, and remember the 5 Pursuits and 5 Pitfalls.

FuPan of others has two types: pure review of others (extracting key patterns from their success or failure) and comparative FuPan (you and competitors do similar things, compare differences in thinking and focus). Lenovo used comparative FuPan to discover foreign firms' gradual price reduction pattern combined with Moore's Law — slashing prices instantly with sub-10,000-yuan PCs. Fosun avoided the diversification trap with 'thorough diversification, thorough specialization' — classic cases of mastering industry patterns.

01
Two Types
Another's Stone Can Polish Your Jade: Two Ways of FuPan of Others
FuPan of others has two types: 1) Pure review — examine who did something well or poorly, extract key patterns. This is 'using another's stone to sharpen your own knife.' 2) Comparative FuPan — you and a competitor did similar things; compare thinking, focus points, outcome differences. Through comparison, discover gaps, key success factors, and acquire 'intuition and instinct.'

02
Benchmark 1
Lenovo Price War: How Comparative FuPan Reshaped China PC Market
In the 1990s, Lenovo used comparative FuPan to discover two key patterns: 1) Foreign firms set high prices then gradually reduce them; 2) Under Moore's Law, whoever clears inventory fastest wins. Lenovo adopted a price-war strategy — slash prices in one move, launching sub-10,000-yuan then sub-5,000-yuan PCs, catching foreign firms off guard and becoming China's #1 PC brand.

03
Benchmark 2
Fosun Diversification: Reviewing a Benchmark to Find Thorough Diversification + Thorough Specialization
China saw a diversification wave: Haier real estate, Lenovo FM365, Vanke supermarkets — most failed. Yet Fosun thrived across real estate, pharmaceuticals, steel, and mining. FuPan reveals the key: 'Thorough diversification' (investment perspective — distribute capital across industries) + 'Thorough specialization' (operations — hire the best professionals, do not mix sand). Wang Shi questioned Guo Guangchang scattered attention, but this model elegantly resolved the contradiction.
⚠️

Common Fallacy

Wang Shi challenge: worrying about real estate and steel simultaneously — attention must scatter. Traditional view: diversification and specialization are incompatible.

💡

Fosun Solution

Industrial investment model: thorough diversification in investment (spread across industries), thorough specialization in operations (hire the best, no mixing sand). Contradiction elegantly resolved.

Four Strategies
From Copycat to Pattern Mastery: Four Competitive Strategies After FuPan of Others
After FuPan of competitors, enterprises adopt progressively sophisticated strategies: 1) Copycat — small players copying JDB; but success is never replicable. 2) Adaptation — branded firms draw inspiration, like Qunhao's 'City Hunter' from Lilang's 'Business Menswear.' 3) Proactive attack — capable players initiate challenges. 4) The most effective — grasp industry patterns and key success factors, like Galanz buying (not importing) production lines, eliminating competitors' manufacturing capacity.

05
Limits & Breakthrough
Incomplete Information & Key-Point Method: Natural Limits and Breakthrough
FuPan of others faces the natural limit of incomplete information — others behavior reveals only the tip of the iceberg. Therefore, situation-reenactment is impossible; the key-point method must be used: identify key points, collect information, reconstruct behavior logic. Avoid rushing to conclusions — approach truth through multiple reviews, reduce risk through cross-validation. Most importantly: focus on the matter, not the person; avoid 5 pitfalls, practice 5 pursuits.
⚠️ 5 FuPan Pitfalls vs 5 Pursuits

❌ Self-deception, no ruthless analysis → Truth (focus on facts)
❌ Going through motions, formality → Substance (content & root causes)
❌ Blame attribution, witch-hunt → Learning (improvement & elevation)
❌ Emphasizing objectivity, shifting blame → Introspection (self-reflection & analysis)
❌ Rushing to conclusions → Essence (finding fundamental patterns)

🔑 Key Takeaway
The highest realm of FuPan of others is not following competitors moves (copycat→adapt→attack), but grasping industry patterns and key success factors through FuPan — Lenovo and Galanz both became industry leaders through this path. Since incomplete information is an inherent limit, the key-point method must replace situation reenactment, and multiple reviews with cross-validation reduce conclusion errors. Most important: build a matter-focused, not person-focused FuPan culture — avoid 5 pitfalls, practice 5 pursuits, and make FuPan the core competency for continuous organizational evolution.
