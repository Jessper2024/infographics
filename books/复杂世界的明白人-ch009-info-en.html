Lesson 1
Slow Is Fast: Be a Value Creator, Not a Traffic Slave
The author once chased trending news — views skyrocketed, and he thought the good times would last forever. Then he realized it's a two-way trap: content selects readers, and readers select content. Keep chasing trends, and readers who value depth will leave, leaving only spectators. Worse: you forget how to write without a trend, and your audience structure becomes locked. He set a rule: every article must teach himself and his readers something. Views dropped, but substance solidified, and he kept progressing.

Mental Fortitude
"No Feelings": Every Master Builder Must Fight Countless Mental Collapses
Reviewing the years, the author found that most derailed endeavors boiled down to mental collapse — emotional swings, paranoia about gains and losses, rushing for results, flagging will, excessive concern with others' opinions. He even abandoned his WeChat public account once and considered abandoning it dozens of times. Those who bought Bitcoin, Moutai, EVs from low prices often ended up with nothing — because these assets rose amid volatility, and most people traded frantically through the swings, achieving nothing after years. Whatever you do, emotional stability is paramount — ideally, "no feelings."

Lesson 2
Build Value Networks: Unstoppable Like the Steam Engine
What kind of business can grow large and endure? The core is a "value network" — you provide value (what helps others), and you need a network (networks amplify value, value expands networks). The steam engine existed in ancient Greece, constantly improved. Watt hit a critical point where his improved version provided positive value across more and more domains — used in trains, ships, catapulting the British Empire into a new era. Anything that succeeds must provide others with value exceeding their time cost.

Lesson 3
Micro-Iteration: Big Things and Small Things Are Essentially the Same
The author once wondered how projects with millions of lines of code were written. He discovered they're like buildings — Phase 1, Phase 2, Phase 3, each adding features. One intern's 50-line "overheat → send SMS" code evolved over five to six years into an independent management system sold standalone. Musk spoke of "micro-iteration" — any grand project divides into many small steps, done slowly. The author writes the same way: build a skeleton, fill it gradually, unreadable at first, shaped after several revisions. Anything becomes doable when broken into sufficiently small actionable steps.

05
Synthesis
Don't Be a Traffic Slave: Value Creates Networks and Shapes You
The author contrasts two paths: working for a company — salary paid, that's it, product sales and longevity are irrelevant to you; creating your own work — randomness and long-tail effects, potential to go viral and change your life, or many articles layering into a massive tail. Choose short-term or long-term, fast or slow — but at minimum, don't be traffic's slave. You can choose what you do, and what you do reshapes you — a mutual acceleration.
🏢

Working for System · Linear Return

Company pays salary → gone · product sales unrelated to you · no long tail · no randomness · locked in system · value can't compound

🚀

Creating Your Own · Exponential Return

Self-sold work → long tail · today's article still draws traffic next year · many articles layer into massive tail · potential viral breakthrough · value compounds

"Provide enduring value, create networks, micro-iterate — and value will shape you."

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
Three ultimate laws for navigating the arena: Slow Is Fast — refuse to be traffic's slave, choose the long-tail path of deep value output, maintain emotional stability "no feelings" to fight countless mental collapses; Build Value Networks — provide lasting value, let it spread through networks, once the positive feedback loop starts, you become unstoppable; Micro-Iteration — break any grand project into sufficiently small actionable steps, and everything becomes achievable. Value creates networks, and value shapes you.
