Core Signal
Divergence — The Short Seller's Strongest Signal
Bearish divergence is the most reliable sell signal in technical analysis. When prices make new highs but MACD histogram, MACD line, or Force Index fails to follow, upward momentum is exhausted. The "Triple Crown" — all three diverging simultaneously — is a near-perfect short signal. MGM case: at ~$100, Triple Crown appeared; the stock collapsed 95% to single digits.

03
System Method
Triple Screen — Cashing In on Value Destruction
The Triple Screen is the core of Elder's methodology: Screen 1 uses weekly charts for strategic direction, Screen 2 uses daily charts for tactical timing. In bear markets, value destruction is the norm — rallies into the EMA value zone are shorting opportunities, declines to the lower channel band are cover signals. MGM -95%, SHLD -80%, BAC -95% — all prove swing trading beats holding through.

04
Tactical Tools
Systematic Shorting in Declining Channels
Once a stock enters a declining channel, two systematic approaches produce consistent profits. MACD Reversal System: short when histogram crosses below zero, cover near the lower band. In-Channel System: short when price rallies into the value zone, stop above the recent high, take profits near the lower band. SHLD's daily chart gave 4 clear short signals during 2007-2008. Capture 30% of channel width for an A-grade.
✅

Correct Approach

Fixed position size, never add. Swing trading — think of yourself as a merchant: rapid turnover is the hallmark of a healthy business. 30% of channel width = A-grade.

❌

Fatal Mistakes

Adding to positions, chasing perfect tops/bottoms, discarding systems. When a system stops working, shelf it — don't discard it. Perfection is the enemy of good.

05
Lessons & Reflection
Bear Market Bottoms & Eternal Lessons
Bear markets have no floor, but bullish divergence is the strongest signal of a bear-to-bull transition. Yet the "Hound of the Baskervilles" warns: divergence can also fail — FRE (Freddie Mac) saw two consecutive bullish divergences collapse, eventually plunging to $0.46. When Buffett bought GE in 2008, Elder's system was still red. Never say a price is "too low" — traders who bought FRE at $5 still lost 85%.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The 2007–2009 bear market taught us not fear, but discipline. Use the Triple Screen system — weekly for direction, daily for timing — short in the value zone, cover at the lower channel band. NH-NL divergences and sentiment indicators ring the earliest alarm bells. Divergence + false breakouts form the strongest short signal. Never buy just because a price "looks cheap." Never abandon your system after a single stop-out. Reserve the right to be smarter tomorrow than you are today.
