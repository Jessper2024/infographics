FuPan: Mental Rehearsal of the Past · Chapter 3 "Three Types of FuPan"

Self-Review · Team Review · Reviewing Others — Three Paths, One Goal: Growth

S Situation: FuPan (after-action review) is a core methodology for continuous growth, yet most people only review their own actions.
C Complication: Reviewing solely from a personal perspective risks self-deception and superficiality, making it hard to uncover deep patterns.
Q Question: How can we systematically leverage different types of FuPan to maximize learning outcomes?
A Answer: Master the three types — Self-Review, Team Review, and Reviewing Others — each with unique strengths, to build a complete review system.

CORE STRENGTH
Self-Review: Free-Flowing, Anytime
Self-Review is the simplest and most actionable form of FuPan, unconstrained by time, space, or progress. A flash of thought, a recollection, a reflection, a comparison, a conclusion — all can be a review. With expert guidance, it can transcend your own level and yield unexpected gains.

⚠️

Common Pitfall: No Records

Fleeting insights vanish without a trace. Without writing it down, the same mistakes repeat. Document conclusions and thought processes immediately.

✅

Key Requirement: Real Application

Without application, patterns remain mere knowledge, not value. Lenovo advocates "live and breathe with FuPan."

⚠️ The Biggest Trap: Self-Deception
Authentic self-review must be "selfless." Kodak knew the digital era had arrived but clung to film profits, delaying transformation until bankruptcy. Emptying yourself is the only way to find the true root cause.
"Through FuPan, summarize lessons — especially failures. Be thorough, spare no feelings, think it through clearly." — Liu Chuanzhi, Lenovo
03
COLLABORATIVE DEEP-DIVE
Team Review: Collective Inquiry, Truth-Seeking
Team Review is a discussion forum, not a lecture. Participants must contribute their wisdom to arrive at conclusions close to truth. It should occur at project milestones, key junctures, or when major questions arise — not at random.
🤐

Extreme 1: Collective Silence

No one meets the facilitator's gaze. Cultural norms like "the nail that sticks out gets hammered" turn discussions into monologues.

🔥

Extreme 2: Blame Game

When assigning responsibility, everyone interrupts to defend themselves. Right vs. wrong becomes a character trial; factions shield allies.

04
LEARNING FROM OTHERS
Reviewing Others: Sharpen Your Blade with Their Stone
Reviewing Others is the most cost-effective growth method — others spend their resources "rehearsing" in front of you, and you gain experience at zero cost. It comes in two forms: pure review and comparative review. Benchmarking is the most powerful variant.
Follow
Default for the weaker
Direct imitation of rivals
e.g., Heqizheng copying Wanglaoji
Borrow
For branded companies
Extract different concepts
e.g., Qunhao's "Urban Hunting Wear"

For resource-rich players
Master patterns, seize control
e.g., Lenovo, Galanz
Through comparative review, Lenovo discovered Moore's Law market dynamics, slashed prices in one step, launched sub-¥10,000 PCs, and became China's #1 PC brand — fundamentally reshaping the market.
⚠️ Natural Limitation: Insufficient Information
Reviewing Others cannot use the "scenario replay" method. Instead, apply the critical point method — identify key moments, gather data, reconstruct logic, and cross-verify to reduce error risk.
05
BEST PRACTICES
Five Pitfalls → Five Pursuits
Regardless of FuPan type, avoiding pitfalls and building culture are essential. Lenovo's 5 pitfalls and "5 Pursuits" are the critical safeguards for successful reviews.
🚫

Five Pitfalls

① Self-deception, lacking ruthless self-analysis
② Going through the motions
③ Turning review into a blame session
④ Emphasizing external factors, deflecting responsibility
⑤ Rushing to conclusions

✨

Five Pursuits

Truth: Seeking facts from reality
Substance: Focus on content and root causes
Learning: Emphasize improvement
Introspection: Self-reflection and analysis
Principles: Grasp essence and patterns

BLUF · KEY TAKEAWAY
FuPan has three types: Self-Review, Team Review, and Reviewing Others, each suited to different scenarios and complementary to one another. Self-Review demands honest recording and application. Team Review requires a healthy culture and clear role division. Reviewing Others means grasping underlying patterns, not surface imitation. Only by combining all three can you build a complete FuPan system that transforms experience into genuine capability.
