Fupan · How Was the Decision Originally Made

SCQA · Core Insight Choosing A over B, one method over another, one target over another — every decision is driven by "intense purposefulness" as its reference point. Yet most people only ask "what went wrong" during fupan, ignoring the deeper question: how was the decision originally made? This involves three dimensions: how the matter was decided, what it was based on, and how well it was executed.

Many equate fupan with summarization — finding causes and fixes. But fupan's greater value lies in deduction: exploring alternative possibilities and boundaries between success and failure to achieve self-growth. Examining "how was the decision originally made" is the button for self-growth. It contains three dimensions: ① how it was decided (form & mindset), ② what it was based on (logic & evidence), ③ how execution went (against the plan, not just results). "Intense purposefulness" — always remembering what you're doing it for — is the ultimate reference for judgment.

Deep Inspection
Examining "How It Was Decided": Seven Questions to Reveal Character
Scrutinizing how a matter was decided reveals the decision form and individual mindset. Seven key questions: imposed or initiated? Were others consulted? Was there full debate? Monologue or engaged discussion? Did you truly listen? Did you absorb value or nitpick inaccuracies? Was the mindset to do it well or to prove yourself right?

03
Evidence Audit
Examining "Based On What": Evidence Survival Determines Course
Examining what a decision was based on targets its logic. Why learn English — to broaden horizons or just because others do it? Why network marketing instead of TV ads? Evidence is the foundation of targets. During fupan ask: does the original evidence still hold? Is it sound? If evidence stands, persist. If evidence shifts, change course.

04
Execution Audit
Reviewing Execution: Go Beyond "What Went Wrong" — Compare Against Plan
Execution is the easiest part to notice in fupan, but most stay superficial: late shipments, poor communication, insufficient promotion. True execution fupan must compare against the original plan: how was execution specified? Was it followed strictly? What surprises emerged? What new conditions? With a plan as baseline, fupan goes beyond "finding problems" to deeply explore "how to fix them."

05
Beyond Summary
Why Fupan Surpasses Reflection: Deduction, Not Induction
The essential difference between fupan and reflection is deduction: reflection is induction — find causes and fixes. Fupan is deduction — through exploring possibilities, find the boundary between success and failure. Review decision → audit evidence → compare execution → discover alternatives → achieve self-growth. Once you've grown, finding causes and fixes becomes effortless. This is how fupan transcends simple reflection.

🔑 Core Takeaway
"How was the decision originally made" is the second core question of fupan and its main content. It contains three progressive dimensions: examine how it was decided (refine character, broaden perspective, unite talent); examine what it was based on (verify evidence, clarify direction, decide to persist or pivot); examine execution (compare against plan, distinguish responsibility, solve deeply). These three dimensions combined with "intense purposefulness" as the ultimate reference form the shortest path to self-growth. Fupan surpasses reflection because it refuses to stop at induction and instead deduces the boundaries of success and failure.
