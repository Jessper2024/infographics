The Key to Review Is Rehearsal

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer Many think review is nothing special—isn't it just summarizing experience and lessons learned? But Liu Chuanzhi made review one of Lenovo's three methodologies, demanding every global employee "share the fate of review." What truly distinguishes review from summary? The answer: rehearsal—virtual exploration of what hasn't happened.

Review is not summary. Summary is a static sorting of event processes, focusing only on key points and milestones. Review mentally walks through the entire journey from beginning to end and also includes virtual exploration of unrealized behaviors—that is, rehearsal. Rehearsal is the most distinctive difference between review and summary. Like playing Go: summary only tells the player where they played well or poorly; review lets the player explore each move, see if different moves were possible and their downstream consequences. Review can also examine the thinking and logic before the event, eliminate wrong understanding and paths, and find more effective approaches. Summary is flat; review is three-dimensional. When one masters review, one no longer needs step-by-step rehearsal—one can directly grasp the key from complex phenomena. This is intuition trained from rehearsal.

Summary (Flat) vs Review (3D): The Dimensional Gap Defines Value
Review can also examine the thinking and logic before the event, scrutinizing the thought process and logic at the time. Through review plus real execution comparison, you can eliminate wrong understanding and paths, find more effective and pattern-aligned approaches, determine which behaviors to continue, which must stop, and what new behaviors to start.

04
Sandbox vs Rehearsal
Sandbox Pre-Action vs Rehearsal Post-Action: The Certainty Divide
The sandbox is pre-action rehearsal, where no practice has occurred and conclusions come from imagined possibilities. Review is post-action rehearsal—because real execution experience and results serve as reference, conclusions are no longer imagined possibilities but deterministic methods that help solve problems. Nobody equates a sandbox with a plan, and naturally, review is not equivalent to summary.

Whether it's review or summary becomes hard to distinguish for the layperson—but the distinction is real, just internalized as intuition.

🔑 Key Takeaway
The essential difference between review and summary lies in rehearsal—virtual exploration of unrealized behaviors. Summary is flat, static, and jumping; review is three-dimensional, dynamic, and continuous. Sandbox rehearsal yields imagined possibilities; review rehearsal yields experience-verified certainty. From deliberate rehearsal to internalized intuition—this is exactly how review elevates from a method to a capability.
