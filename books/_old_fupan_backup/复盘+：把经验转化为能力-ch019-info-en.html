Fupan+ · Ch8 "Scaling Review"

From 1.0 to 3.0 — How to Root Review, Grow It, and Make It Organizational DNA

Review's techniques are simple and its effects are striking, yet Signet Consulting Group found that "many efforts to learn and apply AAR have failed because practitioners repeatedly reduce a vivid practice into a dry, tedious technique." Doing one review is easy; doing it well, scaling it, forming habits, and building a system is hard. This chapter systematically lays out the three-stage evolution of review adoption (1.0 Standardize → 2.0 Achieve Results → 3.0 Institutionalize), three "growth engines" (Employee · Facilitator · Organization), six scaling stages (Loosen Soil → Empower → Pilot → Synthesize → Scale → Optimize), four scaling strategies (Top-Down · Step-by-Step · Embed in Business · Divide and Conquer), plus four levels of evaluation, two dimensions for amplifying impact, and the "Cue → Routine → Reward → Craving" habit loop.

01
3 STAGES
The Three Stages of Review Adoption: From Doing to Achieving
Organizations typically evolve through three stages when scaling review. Review 1.0 moves from ad-hoc to standardized — upgrading work summaries to team reviews, but still plagued by mechanical template use, low engagement, absent facilitation, and no systematic framework. Review 2.0 moves from standardized to results-driven — training certified review facilitators elevates the fit among participants, facilitators, and organization, actually making review effective and avoiding hollow formalism. Review 3.0 moves from results to an institutionalized system — embedding review into business operations, silently forming a complete work-learn-improve cycle that ultimately becomes habit and organizational culture. Wanda, Lenovo, BP, and the US Army all operate at 3.0.

02
GROWTH ENGINES
The Three "Growth Engines" Driving Review Expansion
Sustaining review expansion requires activating three groups' enthusiasm to become driving "engines." Employees: when each review delivers real results — valuable lessons learned in a positive atmosphere — participation rises, forming a virtuous cycle of "feel good → participate more → learn more." Facilitators: effective reviews boost confidence, motivating facilitators to reflect, summarize, and sharpen skills. Organization: done-right reviews improve processes, systems, norms, and even deep-seated beliefs, improving organizational culture so people are more willing to reflect honestly and spark collective intelligence. These three engines interact, jointly driving continuous improvement in review effectiveness.

03
ROADMAP
Six Phases for Scaling Review: Cultivating Review Like Growing Crops
Scaling review cannot be done as a "campaign" or "going through the motions." Like farming, it requires patience through six phases: Loosen the Soil (advocate, clarify value, create atmosphere); Empower (train facilitators, spread knowledge internally); Pilot (start with small teams or specific events, build experience gradually); Synthesize & Refine (use review to review every review, improve design and execution capability); Scale Gradually (expand from easy to hard across four review types); and Evaluate & Optimize (solidify standard operating procedures and templates, influence more people, improve culture). This is a long-term ecological systems engineering project — forcing premature growth only backfires.

04
STRATEGIES
Four Strategies for Scaling Review + Four Levels of Evaluation
Four complementary strategies drive review expansion: Top-Down — leaders walking the talk is the only path to grounding review (Liu Chuanzhi: "Leading by example is not an important way to influence others — it is the only way"); Step-by-Step — start with event/activity-level reviews, easy to organize, fast to see results, builds confidence; Embed in Business — make review an official agenda item, not an "optional extra"; Divide & Conquer — HR/training departments teach, leaders initiate, planning offices coordinate mechanisms, communications teams celebrate successes, business units host sharing sessions. Meanwhile, evaluate review impact across four levels: Reaction → Output → Action Implementation → Business Value.

05
SUSTAIN
Amplify Impact Across Two Dimensions + Make Review a Habit
Review is like dropping a pebble into a pond, creating concentric ripples. Amplify review impact across two dimensions: horizontally expand sharing scope — from the review team's internal learning, to other teams participating live or accessing results, to organization-wide publication via KM systems (US Army's AKO, WeChat communities, email groups like Chevron's "Safety Alert Network"); vertically deepen application depth — from single-team follow-through, to discovering and solving common problems, to optimizing organizational systems, processes, norms, and culture. Making review a habit follows the "habit loop": create "cues" through calendars and agendas, reinforce "rewards" through recognition and visible results, link rewards to the inner "craving" for learning and growth, and leverage the power of community (practice groups, sharing sessions, training).

Team follow-through → Identify common issues → Optimize systems/processes/norms/culture. Move from case-by-case fixes to holistic elevation, maximizing review's leverage value.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
Scaling review is not a campaign — it is ecological cultivation. The journey from 1.0 to 3.0 requires activating the three growth engines of employees, facilitators, and the organization, patiently traversing six phases from loosening soil through optimizing, and flexibly deploying four strategies from top-down leadership through collaborative division of labor. Evaluate impact across four levels from reaction to business value. Amplify impact by expanding horizontally and deepening vertically. The ultimate goal: transform review from "a method" into "a habit," then use habit to shape culture. As the U.S. Army Leadership Handbook states: "Unless AAR is practiced at all levels, it is merely a dispensable hobby."
