Fupan · Examine the Premises of Thinking

SCQA · Core Insight Every action relies on implicit assumptions as its foundation — the starting point of thinking. If the foundation is wrong, no matter how elegant the logic or flawless the execution, the result will be a journey south when you intended north. "Doing the right thing" matters more than "doing things right." Premise review requires examining motivation (both public interest and alignment with essence) and your understanding of the thing's true nature.

If "what's the current situation" reviews execution results, and "how was the decision originally made" reviews execution logic, then "examining premises of thinking" reviews the very foundation of execution. Premise review asks two core questions: is the motivation pure (not just fair to others but aligned with the thing's inherent nature) and is our understanding of the thing's essence correct? TCL's M&A of Thomson was driven by ambition to be world #1 — not to better serve consumers — violating the "justice" of enterprise. In 2012, internet companies making phones "not for user needs but to embed their own services" led to disastrous outcomes. Conversely, McDonald's as real estate, Walmart as logistics, iMac/iPod as fashion — redefining essence opens new frontiers. This chapter also provides a practical fupan checklist framework.

01
Foundation
Why Premise Review Is the Deepest Layer of Fupan
All thinking and action rest on implicit assumptions. If the premise shifts, everything downstream must change. Western medicine sees symptoms as local faults → suppress with drugs. The author of Your Body's User Manual sees symptoms as the body fighting disease → strengthen the resisting organ. Same symptom, different premises, entirely opposite treatments. This is the modern version of "heading south while intending north" — "doing the right thing" matters more than "doing things right."

02
Motivation
Pure Motivation: Not Just "Fair to Others" but "True to Essence"
"Gongyi" (public interest) means fairness to others. "Zhengyi" (alignment with essence) means conforming to the thing's inherent nature. Befriending foreigners at hotels to secure marriage visas may be defensible by public interest — but it violates zhengyi, the true purpose of cross-cultural friendship. TCL's Thomson M&A was driven by "become world #1 in TVs" not "provide better products to consumers" — impure motive distorted every tactic, ending in liquidation. Kai-Fu Lee on 2012's tech-phone wave: "not driven by user needs but embedding their own services" — impure intent led naturally to failure.

03
Essence Cognition
Redefining Essence Opens Entirely New Frontiers
Motivation examines the starting point of the doer; essence understanding tests cognitive ability. McDonald's appears to be food service but makes money from real estate. Walmart is retail but its core is logistics. Books are knowledge carriers, but Guo Fansheng saw them as information carriers → HC360 network platform. MP3 was a commodity, but Jobs made iPod a fashion item → Apple's second high-growth wave. In fupan, re-examining essence opens new spaces.

The Fupan Checklist: A Practical Framework to Avoid Omissions
A checklist is a powerful fupan tool. Building the list itself is a review and reflection process on key points. It creates milestones in discussion and helps manage time. Core framework: current progress → target comparison → unplanned outcomes → consensus check → evidence changes → execution process → right/wrong analysis → new approaches → motive check → essence understanding → key success factor → root cause of failure. Centered on the three core dimensions, adaptable to actual needs.

05
Review Others
How to Fupan Others: Insert Yourself — Learn from Others' Experience
Reviewing others differs from reviewing yourself: you don't know their targets, only observe their actions. Checklist for others: what results did they achieve → what actions did they take → which actions aligned with principles → which were wasted effort → what logic drove them → was their logic premise sound → would I have done differently → could I have done better → where can we break through their predicament. The key is inserting yourself into the analysis — gain their experience while formulating your own solution.
👀

Passive Observation (Ineffective)

Just watch others' wins/losses·don't insert self·don't deduce alternatives·don't learn real skills

🎯

Active Self-Insertion (Effective)

What would I have done·why did they do that·could I have done better·extract principles for future use

🔑 Core Takeaway
The premises of thinking are the foundation of all execution. Fupan must reach the premise level: is motivation pure — both "gongyi" (public-minded fairness) and "zhengyi" (aligned with the thing's inherent nature)? Is our understanding of essence correct — can we redefine it like McDonald's, Walmart, or iPod did? If the premise is wrong, even the most elegant logic leads south when the goal is north. Only after premise review, combined with a full checklist covering motive, evidence, execution, and essence, can fupan be truly complete.
