Fupans+ · Ch.1「Understanding Fupans」

From Go Board to Boardroom — The Essence, Origins, and Value of Fupans

Fupans is learning from your own past experience—the most vital form of adult learning. Originating as a Go term, this chapter defines fupans through three keywords: firsthand experience, past events, and learning. It distinguishes fupans from work summaries, action learning, training, and performance improvement, dismantles five common misconceptions, and reveals four core values via U.S. Army AAR and Lenovo practices. Fupans is the cornerstone discipline for building learning organizations.

01
Definition & Origin
What Is Fupans?—A Millennium Journey from Go Term to Management Method
Fupans originated in the game of Go: after a match, replay every move to identify good plays, bad plays, and better alternatives. Tang dynasty poet Du Mu captured this scene: "After parting, by a bamboo window on a snowy night, a lone lamp illuminates the replay of the Wu diagram." In business, Lenovo founder Liu Chuanzhi defined it as: after completing something, sit down, review what you planned, what went wrong, and why—then next time, you'll naturally absorb the lessons.

02
Three Keywords
The Three Pillars of Fupans: Firsthand · Past · Learning
Fupans is not casual afterthought—it is a rigorous, structured process of learning from your own experience. Three keywords are essential: Firsthand Experience—fupans must be based on your own practice; studying others is "case research," not fupans. Past—the subject is events or actions that have already occurred. Learning—gaining insights, building capability, improving future actions—achieving the unity of knowledge and action. As Xunzi said: "Learning culminates in action."

03
Key Distinctions
Fupans ≠ Work Summary—Differentiating Fupans from Four Learning Methods
Fupans differs from work summaries in three ways: learning-oriented (no praise or blame, faithfully restore facts) vs performance-oriented; structured process (review goals→analyze gaps→extract lessons→apply) vs no fixed template; team deep dialogue vs individual description. Fupans is "informal learning" at the worksite, distinct from action learning and training (formal learning requiring design) and performance improvement (consulting-style interventions).

04
Dispelling Myths
Five Common Misconceptions About Fupans—How Many Do You Hold?
Myth 1: You can do fupans on others—Wrong! Non-participants can't restore facts; that's case research. Myth 2: "Post-project review" equals fupans—Not necessarily! It must include systematic analysis and collective learning. Myth 3: Fupans is just problem-solving—No! Fupans transcends problem-solving; it requires breadth (highlights + gaps) and depth (drawing inferences). Myth 4: Fupans is useless in fast-changing industries—On the contrary! Fupans is about looking ahead and grasping essence. Myth 5: Fupans stifles innovation—Precisely the opposite! Real fupans demands an open mind and sparks innovation.

05
Origins & Value
The Global Journey of Fupans—From Zeng Guofan to U.S. Army AAR, from Lenovo to Alibaba
As a management practice, fupans was pioneered in China by Liu Chuanzhi (inspired by Zeng Guofan) in 2001 at Lenovo, structured in 2011, promoted globally, and praised by Premier Li Keqiang in 2015. In the U.S., the Army introduced AAR in the 1970s post-Vietnam; it became standard after the Gulf War and proved its power in Haiti peacekeeping. Core value for the Army: enhance training, build execution, cultivate leadership, raise collective IQ. For Lenovo: know the "why," pass on experience, avoid repeating mistakes, codify processes. Peter Senge called it "one of the most successful organizational learning methods yet devised."

🔑 Core Takeaway
The essence of fupans is learning from firsthand experience. Its core lies not in "re-viewing" but in "deep analysis and systems thinking." It is not a work summary, not problem-solving, not staring at the rearview mirror—it is the core discipline for holistically upgrading organizational capability. Whether success or failure, understanding the causes and learning the lessons is where the true value resides. As Liu Chuanzhi said: fupans is the single most important thing at Lenovo.
