Fupan · The Liu Chuanzhi Cycle: The PDF Method

SCQA · Core Insight Preview → Do → FuPan — the PDF cycle. It means doing one thing three times: Preview builds a mental simulation, Do provides real experience, FuPan lets you replay it all afterward. Through this method, you can analyze a matter thoroughly — break it open, chew it fully — and extract the maximum possible experience, truth, and patterns from a single event. Named after Liu Chuanzhi, who introduced fupan into the work process.

Preview lets you simulate before acting, building familiarity and reducing uncertainty. Do is the actual execution — people naturally focus on it but often fixate on execution alone. FuPan is the reverse direction of Preview: with simulation and execution already done, the third "pass" achieves final completion. The post-battle 3D simulation of the Battle of Easting 73 is a classic example: soldiers returned to the battlefield, offered diaries and video footage, laser-mapped the terrain — building "a living history book" where future cadets can set hypothetical conditions and replay endlessly. PDF differs fundamentally from PDCA: PDCA is different stages of the same process; PDF means doing one thing three times independently.

Sandbox Simulation
P = Preview: Run It Once in Your Mind First
Sandbox simulation originates as a military term — simulating red-blue confrontation to identify advantages, disadvantages, and paths to victory. Applied to work: before starting, simulate — how might things develop? How will the plan advance? What resistance might arise? Are resources sufficient? What is the critical juncture? Zeng Guofan, facing major events, would retreat alone to a loft, light incense, and quietly think through every possible scenario, weighing and comparing, until he settled on a response — a practice that repeatedly saved him from disaster. Preview gives you a big-picture awareness as you act.

04
FuPan Replay
F = FuPan: The Third Pass Achieves Final Completion
FuPan is the reverse direction of Preview: simulation done, execution done — now look back at the whole thing. Compare execution to simulation, ask questions, find causes, get answers. The Battle of Easting 73 fupan is exemplary: U.S. troops returned to Iraqi soil, soldiers recalled the battle, provided diaries and video, laser-mapped digital terrain, built a 3D simulation. Future cadets can set hypotheticals — "What if Iraqis had infrared night-vision?" — fupan not only replays but discovers entirely new lines of thought.

05
PDF vs PDCA
PDF ≠ PDCA: "Three Passes" vs "Four Stages"
They look similar at a glance, but the gap is vast. PDCA (Deming Cycle): Plan → Do → Check → Act — four different stages of the same process; unresolved issues enter the next cycle. PDF: Preview → Do → FuPan — means doing one thing three times — three independent, complete reviews of the same matter. PDF makes capability spiral upward; Preview quality rises until execution matches simulation perfectly — achieving planned precision strike.

🔑 Core Takeaway
The Liu Chuanzhi Cycle (PDF Method) is the ultimate synthesis of fupan theory: Preview builds contingency plans → Do gains real experience → FuPan conducts the third complete review. One thing done three times — dissected, chewed, from detail to panorama. The fundamental difference from PDCA: PDCA breaks one process into stages; PDF performs three independent full passes of one thing. Apply PDF as a continuous work method and capability spirals upward, eventually achieving "planned precision strike" — execution steps matching the sandbox simulation exactly. This is the right way everyone should work.
