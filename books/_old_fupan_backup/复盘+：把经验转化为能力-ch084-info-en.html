SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer Harvard's Justice course got over 10 million clicks online — yet few made it to episode 5. Learning requires not just information, but "situation." Saying "what I did" is not a full fupan — it's just a work summary. How do we truly recreate past situations? Through the Situation Recreation Method: recreate the Information Field (what was there), Thinking Field (what was thought), and Emotion Field (why people felt that way). Three fields combine into an Energy Field (what happened), reconstructed through direct chronological narration.

Situation recreation is the first fupan method. The concept of "situation" was first proposed by American sociologists W.I. Thomas and F.W. Znaniecki: only by integrating subjective attitudes with objective cultural values can human behavior be fully explained. Situation involves not just background and environment, but also the participants' psychological states — human thought and behavior can only be reasonably explained when combined with situation.

01
Concept Definition
What Is Situation: Three Fields and Three Flows
Situation far exceeds background or environment — it contains three "fields" (spatial state) and three "flows" (temporal change). "Field" emphasizes the spatial dimension; "flow" emphasizes the temporal dimension. Only by recreating all three fields/flows can a situation be relatively completely reconstructed, providing the precondition for understanding behavior. Saying "what I did" is not fupan — that's a work summary.

📋 Information Field (What's There)

What existed in the external environment at the time: Was there sunshine? Who was present? What did they say and do? How were tools arranged? Helps clarify facts about the project or industry.

🧠 Thinking Field (What They Think)

What each person was thinking: How everyone reasoned. What unspoken social consensus (unwritten rules) governed the situation. Helps judge whether methods and thinking were correct.

02
Core Composition
Emotion Field and Energy Field: From "Why" to "What Happens"
The Emotion Field tells us the "why" between people — their relationships and dynamics. Was there friction? Were people elated or dejected? Was psychological conflict brewing? Emotion is intangible and hardest to fupan — by the time it's observable, it has already become action. The combination of Information, Thinking, and Emotion fields forms an "Energy Field" (aura) — energy flows toward the path of least resistance, determining action: "what happens."

03
Recreation Method
Direct Narration: Complete Chronological Recounting
Situation recreation must follow the "direct narration" approach. Start from when the event began, following its natural development flow, recounting part by part, stage by stage — narrating the Information, Thinking, and Emotion fields within. This "chronological" approach is the best way to ensure truth, completeness, and full coverage. Someone truly seeking to improve through fupan cares about information validity, not narration style.
✅ Direct Narration
→ Value →
Ensures truthful, complete, full information. Chronological but effective. Improvement-seekers won't sacrifice valid info for narrative style.
❌ Work Summary
→ Limitation →
Just describes state — raw information, without situation. No "why this was done" or causal connections. Not a complete fupan.
04
Three-Field Mapping
Three Fields Mapped to Narrator's Three Principles
The three fields perfectly correspond to the Narrator's three principles. Information Field → "Be truthful, present events completely" — must follow truth, completeness, and full coverage. Thinking Field → "Be detached, examine from outside yourself" — boldly reveal what you thought. Emotion Field → "Be receptive, empty yourself" — describe only the emotions of that moment, like an observer, without bringing them into the current fupan.

05
Application Value
The Purpose of Situation Recreation: Learning from Past Experience
The purpose of fupan is primarily about action, not emotion. The core value of situation recreation is: clarifying facts (Information Field), judging methods (Thinking Field), and knowing oneself (Emotion Field). Even if the Emotion Field can't be well recreated — especially when fupan-ing others — as long as Information and Thinking fields are recreated, fupan still enables learning and capability improvement. The "disillusionment" with successful people often stems from knowing only fragments, not the complete situation.

🎯 Fupan's Purpose

Learn from past experience, find correct approaches, grasp underlying patterns. The key to capability improvement lies in understanding the complete situation — including information, thinking, and emotion — not isolated behavioral descriptions.

⚠️ Celebrity Disillusionment

Buffett had a congressman father; Gates' mother was an IBM director. Learning this, people "no longer believe in success teachings." But many had such connections — few achieved their level.

🔑 Core Takeaway
Situation recreation is the first fupan method. Recreating only behavior — "what I did" — is a work summary, not fupan. True fupan requires recreating three fields: Information Field (clarify facts), Thinking Field (judge methods), and Emotion Field (know oneself), through direct chronological narration. Three fields combine into an Energy Field that determines "what happens." The value of understanding the complete situation lies not in judging individuals, but in learning from past experience to provide a foundation for future action.
