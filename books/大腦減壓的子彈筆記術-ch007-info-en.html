大腦減壓的子彈筆記術 · Chapter 7 "The Final Choice: Weekly and Daily Bullet Action Lists"

123 Rule + Sunday 6-Step: Convert System-Pushed Actions into Planned Lists

The first six chapters built an "auto-push" system — open Evernote daily and the system's top naturally presents a few focused projects, tasks, and actions. Yet the author still recommends creating a "Weekly Bullet Action List" as a standalone note serving as core guidance. This chapter provides two practical methods: ① Sunday Evening 6-Step Method — sequentially check reminders → Important label → Inbox → Context tasks → Planning label → Tracking label, completing a 7-day action list in 30 minutes; ② Daily 123 Rule — 1 self-fulfillment goal per day, 2 project progress advances, 3 time slots (morning/afternoon/evening) for small tasks and buffer. The goal isn't to fill every minute, but to use structural principles to allocate energy, generate motivation, and find fulfillment.

01
Weekly List Value
Why List Weekly Actions When the System Already Auto-Pushes?
The system from the first six chapters already helps you quickly focus on the best action each day and context. But creating an additional weekly bullet action list brings four extra benefits: ① Further reduces unnecessary brain decisions, achieving deeper mental decompression; ② Preview which slots next week are free vs. packed, allowing advance adjustment; ③ Advance planning provides more time to think, enabling better importance judgments and task linkage analysis; ④ Make the best choices in unhurried time, avoiding forced poor decisions in chaotic moments. This isn't redundant work — it deepens your trust and sense of control over the system through list form.

02
Sunday 6-Step
Sunday Evening 30 min, Check 6 Entry Points in Order
The practical method for creating a weekly action list is extremely simple — just check these 6 entry points in order: ① 'Reminders': which tasks enter preparation phase next week? ② 'Important' label: which projects need progress advancement? ③ 'Inbox': what new tasks were collected this week but not yet scheduled? ④ 'Context tasks': under labels like Solo Free, Couple Time, Thinking, Testing, Writing, Shopping, Chores — which fit next week's various gaps? ⑤ 'Planning' label: can conception-stage projects take a next step? ⑥ 'Tracking' label: any completed projects needing review? Pick corresponding actions from each entry and place into the 7-day grid. Done in 30 minutes.

03
123-1 Goal
Daily 1: Pre-set One Self-Fulfillment Goal Each Day
Some things in life have no deadline and no one pushing you — but you choose to advance them yourself. These are the best source of fulfillment and happiness. For the author: writing a blog post, watching a series with his wife, reading picture books with his child. Actively, concretely pre-select these and place them into each day of the week. Three effects: ① Every day has an action that brings its own fulfillment — the best motivation engine; ② Use gap time to advance these, seizing scattered moments productively; ③ If you have zero motivation for 'real work,' invest time in self-goals — at least better than the regret of avoidance and wasted time — and this fulfillment paradoxically motivates you to tackle difficult work tasks.
✅ 正向

❌ 反向

04
123-2 Projects
Daily 2: Advance Two Project Progresses
Projects often run in parallel — diving in all at once only multiplies anxiety. Instead, spread everything out, spread all available time out, and honestly face: 'What can truly be scheduled for completion?' This pushes you to: ① Identify what truly 'must be completed'; ② Remind yourself some things must start early; ③ Bravely abandon some things, focusing on the most important actions. The practical method: a day typically has two peak energy periods (morning and afternoon). Pre-set one project advancement for each. If you pre-plan the full week, you know 'A project's milestone must be done by Friday,' then work backward to determine Mon-Thu daily steps — avoiding unrealistic expectations, knowing you must start early, fight for more time, and eliminate excess distractions.

05
123-3 Buffer
3 Time Slots: Small Tasks, but More Importantly — Buffer
After your self-goal and project progress are scheduled, you'll feel clearly: the remaining time each day is actually not much — you must drastically reduce trivial tasks and side distractions. But you can indeed arrange some small tasks as mental palate cleansers — one per morning/afternoon/evening slot, three total, preferably fewer. Why? Because every day inevitably brings surprises and ad-hoc situations that add unscheduled trivia. So the better strategy is 'buffer' — give yourself flexible time to handle surprises, rather than filling every minute only to have plans broken. Such an action list isn't idealized theory — it's real, concrete, flexible life design that helps you find achievement, maintain steady progress, and regain motivation.

💡 核心要点
The weekly bullet action list helps you manage not more, but "less, yet better." The 30-minute Sunday planning converts system-pushed focal points into foreseeable actions; the 123 Rule returns daily structure to you — one goal gives fulfillment, two advances give momentum, three buffered slots give flexibility. This list isn't a shackle binding you, but a compass freeing your mind. You no longer face a mountain of tasks each day with dread — you simply open that weekly action list note, and execute one by one.
