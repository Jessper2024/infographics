01
Life Events
Early Marriage and Journey to the Capital
Su Dongpo married Wang Fu at eighteen (she was fifteen), and his brother married at sixteen — early marriage could save young people the time, energy, and emotional turmoil of courtship. From the parents' perspective, they preferred their sons to marry local girls. Lin Yutang commented: all matches were made in heaven but implemented on earth — in any case, all marriages are gambles, like sailing on a vast ocean.
⏳

Going Unmarried: The Risk

After passing the exams in the capital, families with unmarried daughters would swarm in — wealthy merchants all waited on exam results with financial proposals.

🏡

Parents' Arrangement

Simple, convenient, time-saving. The Su brothers happily married local girls from families they knew, everything arranged by parents — saving endless trouble.

02
Capital City
The Great Song Capital: Kaifeng's Splendor
Kaifeng was the main Song capital, thirteen miles in circumference, with four rivers flowing through the city. The imperial palace stood at the city center, with glazed tile roofs gleaming above. A distinctive feature: no one could walk about bareheaded — even the humblest fortune teller dressed like a scholar. The Su brothers strolled the streets, ate at famous restaurants, and stood in the cold wind admiring the sight of famous ministers' carriages.

03
Exam System
The Palace Examination: The Most Important Moment in a Scholar's Life
Candidates had to rise at midnight, arrive at the palace by dawn, bring their own cold provisions, and were confined in separate cubicles guarded by palace soldiers. A rigorous anti-bribery system: all papers were recopied by clerks before being given to examiners, without names. Examiners were confined from late January to early March. Emperor Renzong especially cherished talent, often changing exam questions at the last minute to prevent leaks.

04
Patron Recognition
Ouyang Xiu's Ecstatic Praise: A Destiny-Changing Remark
Ouyang Xiu told a colleague: "Reading Su Dongpo's letter, my whole body sweats with joy. I should retire and let this young man come to the fore." Once these words were spoken, the entire capital heard them. Mistaking the paper for his friend Zeng Gong's work, he moved it from first to second place to avoid suspicion. But he later told his son: "In thirty years no one will talk about me." A contemporary saying went: scholars fear neither punishment, nor promotion lost, nor death — only Ouyang Xiu's opinion.

05
Genius Anecdote
The Fabricated Allusion: A Genius's Boldness and Wit
In his exam essay, Su Dongpo fabricated a dialogue between Emperor Yao and Gao Yao — "Gao Yao said: Execute him, thrice. Yao said: Pardon him, thrice." The examiners dared not question it, afraid to admit they had not read these obscure ancient texts. Afterward, Mei Yaochen asked him: "In which book is this recorded?" Su Dongpo candidly admitted: "I made it up." "Really?" "Well, a sage ruler would certainly have done this, wouldn't he?" This was not cheating — it was genius intuition, grasping the behavioral logic a sage ruler should have.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Becoming a Presented Scholar at twenty, Ouyang Xiu's ecstatic praise, the wit of fabricating an allusion — Su Dongpo's exam journey is legendary. But more importantly: in this most important exam of his life, he established his political philosophy of "simplicity and leniency," which became the intellectual foundation for his later opposition to Wang Anshi's reforms. And Ouyang Xiu's prediction came true: ten years after Su Dongpo's death, everyone was talking about him and secretly reading his banned works.
