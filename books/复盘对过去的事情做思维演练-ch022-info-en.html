Excuses for being late look reasonable, but one fupan layer tears them apart. 01
Surface vs. Essence
Meeting lateness: every reason is an excuse — one fupan layer peels it all away
\"Traffic jam\" \"Printer jam\" \"My boss was talking to me\" — every reason sounds reasonable, but all crumble under fupan. The meeting time and agenda were set long ago; everyone knew the deadline. Those needing materials shouldn't print them minutes before. Those in conversations should estimate time or give advance notice. Those going out should build in a buffer. Lateness has no excuse — it's always your own time-management problem. Fupan reveals: how much was external uncontrollable, how much was controllable, how much was luck, how much was capability.
🗣️

Surface excuses (self-deception)

\"Traffic after client visit\" \"Printer jammed\" \"Boss was talking to me\" — all blamed on external forces beyond control.

🔍

Fupan truth (facing yourself)

Didn't prep materials early · didn't estimate conversation time · didn't leave travel buffer. Every root cause is within yourself.

02
Classic Case
TCL-Thomson: from \"18-month profitability\" to ST delisting — the biggest lesson in Chinese corporate globalization
On Nov 4, 2003, TCL formed TTE with Thomson, becoming the world's largest TV maker. Li Dongsheng declared \"18 months to profitability.\" Instead, the acquisition brought massive losses; TCL's stock was marked *ST in 2005–2006. Stark contrast with Lenovo's IBM PC acquisition that led to becoming world #1. Post-fupan, five reasons were identified — but upon deeper questioning, were these truly the root causes?

03
Peeling Layers
Five surface reasons all fail under questioning — deeper fupan reveals they are results, not causes
Fupan identified five reasons, but ask one more \"why\" and they all collapse. ① Misjudged TV technology trends — why was Thomson selling? Because it refused to invest in flat-panel TVs. ② Ignorant of French policy — the law was written down, ask one French speaker and you'd know. Why didn't they? ③ Cultural integration trouble — any idiot could foresee this. Why didn't they prepare? ④ Didn't adapt operating habits — environment changed, tactics should change. Why no contingency plan? ⑤ Morgan Stanley was neutral, Boston Consulting opposed — yet TCL ignored these obvious risk warnings. Upon questioning, none of these were the real problem.
📺
Tech misjudgment
Bet on CRT longevity, DLP rear-projection, plasma. But Thomson sold precisely because it wouldn't invest in flat panels.

EU law: must fire young/capable first. But this was written law — ask one French speaker and you'd know immediately.
🌍
Culture & approach
French refused English, \"looked down on nouveau riche Chinese boss\" — any common sense could foresee this cultural clash.
04
The True Root
Wrong motivation: Li wanted \"#1 in the world\" (pointing at rivals) vs. Jobs loved music (pointing at users)
Fupan to the deepest layer: TCL's acquisition failed because Li Dongsheng's motivation was wrong. TCL's biggest drive was \"becoming the world's #1 TV maker\" — this goal pointed at competitors, not consumers. Contrast with Jobs on Microsoft's Zune failure: \"We won because we truly love music.\" As a business, every thought and action should face products and consumers. When the motivation is wrong, everything that follows is wrong — that's why even elite advisors' warnings were ignored.
🏆

Li's motivation (pointing at rivals)

\"Become world's #1 TV maker\" — pointing at competitors. Blinded by the goal, afraid of missing the opportunity, rushed in 4 months, ignored all risk warnings.

🎵

Jobs' motivation (pointing at users)

\"Truly love music from the heart\" — pointing at users. iPod succeeded not from wanting to beat anyone, but from wanting to make a great music product.

05
Fupan's Value
From \"Rebirth of the Eagle\" to \"losing an opportunity is better than doing something wrong\" — Li's fupan redemption
Li Dongsheng is after all an excellent entrepreneur; he used fupan to extract lessons from catastrophic failure. In 2006, facing TCL's severe internationalization crisis, Li wrote the reflective essay \"Rebirth of the Eagle,\" deeply analyzing his decision-making errors. In April 2007, TTE's European unit filed for bankruptcy, finally freeing TCL from losses. Later he told CCTV: \"Don't be swayed by opportunity — losing an opportunity is better than doing something wrong.\" This is a painful summary from fupan, and the best proof that an entrepreneur can rise from failure.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Fupan's core power lies in asking \"why\" until you can ask no further. Meeting lateness isn't a traffic problem — it's a time-management problem. TCL's Thomson acquisition failure isn't a tech misjudgment problem — it's a motivation problem. Every surface-level \"cause\" is peeled away under sufficiently deep fupan questioning. You'll ultimately find that the true root is rarely external, rarely technical, rarely about insufficient information — it's about motivation. Right motivation makes everything right; wrong motivation makes everything wrong. Fupan is what lets you see the problem behind the problem.
