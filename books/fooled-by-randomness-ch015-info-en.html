Postscript

Fooled by Randomness Beware the Black Swan

📖

The Fable of Taleb

S
Situation: Taleb defeated cancer against 28% odds. His wealth multiplied; his life and career flourished. He indulged himself — earning a helicopter pilot license, enjoying weekends at his Cotswolds villa.
C
Complication: On a windy day, Taleb's helicopter crashed while approaching Battersea Park. He was alone on board. The Black Swan prevailed after all. Even the most cautious cannot escape the devious hand of randomness.
Q
Question: Why are even probability-savvy traders fooled by randomness? When faced with unpredictable, unbearable extreme events — how should we respond?
🦢

Four Characteristics of Black Swans

①

Extremely Rare

Too few data points to detect patterns. Infrequency breeds complacency — people stop planning for what never seems to happen.

②

Massive Impact

One Black Swan outweighs countless minor events. Removing the 50 worst trading days from 30 years of S&P 500 data nearly doubles compound returns.

③

Unpredictable Ex Ante

Endless post-hoc explanations, yet impossible to foresee. Humans fabricate causal narratives to feel in control — stories that comfort but deceive.

④

Preventable

You cannot predict when it will rain, but you can carry an umbrella. You cannot foresee an earthquake, but you can reinforce your house.

📌 The Turkey Parable: A turkey fed for 1,000 days concludes "my master treats me well." On day 1,001 — Thanksgiving — the same hand that brought food wrings its neck. We are that turkey — focused on historical experience while ignoring risk exposure.

💡

Buffett's Response to Black Swans

Ⅰ

Noah's Ark Principle

Predicting rain is useless; building an ark is prudent. After 9/11, Buffett admitted: "I did think about it, but sadly, I failed to convert thought into action."

"I violated the Noah principle: predicting rain doesn't count; building arks does."
Ⅱ

The Twin Insurance Principles

Principle One: Accept only risks you can properly evaluate — including remote loss scenarios — then price for expected profit.
Principle Two: Strictly limit exposure so no single event or correlated cluster can threaten solvency. Relentlessly hunt for hidden correlations among seemingly unrelated risks.

Ⅲ

Right Process Over Good Track Record

When selecting his successor, Buffett looked at how they swing — not just their scorecard. Even stellar investment records may be pure luck. One Black Swan can devour every bit of historical good fortune.

"A长长 string of impressive numbers multiplied by a single zero always equals zero."
⚡

Core Insights

🛡️

Negative Black Swans: Avoid at All Costs

If the cost of failure is too heavy to bear, the probability of success is irrelevant. Better to forgo profit than risk ruin. The skilled warrior stands on unbeatable ground first.

🚀

Positive Black Swans: Seize Rare Asymmetric Bets

When risk is manageable but potential return is enormous — these positive Black Swan opportunities are the true source of outsized gains. This is how Buffett made his fortune.

"Better to die from uncertainty than from ignorance."
