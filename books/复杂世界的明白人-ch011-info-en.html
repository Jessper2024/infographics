Understanding a Complex World · Ch.11 "A Million-Yuan Salary vs. Earning a Million from Your Own Business — Which Is 'Better'?"

Two Completely Different Evaluation Systems · Debating Who Is \"Better\" Is Itself a Sign of Confined Thinking A million-yuan salary comes almost entirely from blood and sweat — elite-school graduates working themselves to death. The entry barrier is sky-high. Earning a million from your own business covers every walk of life — small workshop owners, contractors, barbecue-stall operators, and vegetable-market aunties. These are two different logics. Office work undergoes two rounds of evaluation. Business operators face the market directly. Debating which path is \"better\" is itself a limitation bred by employee-style thinking.

This chapter dissects the nature of two income paths. The author points out: The million-yuan-salary path is \"high-barrier, high-certainty\" — elite-school graduates, nearly tech geniuses, working themselves to exhaustion, with practically no exception. Earning a million from your own business is \"low-barrier, low-certainty\" — highly random. If you catch the right wave, you may strike it rich. If you don't, you may close shop within six months. The core difference between the two logics lies in the \"evaluation system\": employees go through two assessments (market + hierarchy) and hold no leverage. Business owners face the market directly — whatever sells, you earn, but you bear the risk yourself. The final conclusion is sharp: debating which is \"better\" is itself a sign that employee-style thinking has confined you.

Employee Path
Million-Yuan Salaried Workers Are All Elite-School Graduates Working Themselves to Death — Entry Barrier Is Sky-High
Wanting a high salary through employment demands an extremely high entry barrier. Over the years, every person the author has met earning a million through employment graduated from an elite school and worked themselves to death. Most approached the level of tech genius — the kind where a whole group is helpless and one master cracks it instantly. Every yuan of a million-yuan salary is blood and sweat money. Compared to running a business, \"earning a million through technical skills demands exceptionally high personal caliber.\" Yet people earning a million from their own business span every trade and walk of life.

02
Business Path
The Defining Feature of Business Is Randomness — Catch the Wave and Strike It Rich; Miss It and Close Shop
The defining feature of doing business is randomness. Catch the right moment and you may strike it rich. The author's elementary-school classmate dropped out of high school, opened an auto-repair shop in Baotou, also ran car washes and a barbecue stand, and planned to add beauty and hair salon services — pulling in a million a year. But a former colleague opened a barbecue restaurant. Last year, their marinating technique earned money. This year, they lost hundreds of thousands and shut down. With a job, at least you know roughly what tomorrow looks like. With a business, six months out is anyone's guess. But the entry barrier is indeed far lower — how many people ever land a million-yuan-salary position? With business, you can sell anything. High-end goods make money. Low-end goods make money too.

03
System Gap
Employees Go Through Two Assessments: The Market Assesses Whether It Sells, Then the System Decides Your Cut
Employment and business operate on two different logics. An employee's value goes through two assessments — first, the market evaluates whether what you produce can sell; second, the system (your boss) decides how much of the market revenue to give you. Since you hold no leverage, you contribute the lion's share of output and receive a sliver of the returns. Business owners face an entirely different reality — as long as the product meets market demand, whatever sells, you earn. No one cares about your degree. You don't need sky-high intelligence. The key is figuring out what kind of business you can run.

04
Marginal Returns
Your Code Deploys Across Tens of Thousands of Servers Making the Company Money — But You Don't Get Paid Per Server
An employee's marginal returns are zero. A business owner's marginal returns are unlimited. A programmer writes code deployed on tens of thousands of servers generating revenue for the company. Does the company pay them a cut for each server sold? Of course not. Business is different — as long as the product meets market demand, whatever sells, you earn. Keep selling, keep earning. The author gives an Africa example: a colleague noticed locals wanted phones and colorful clothes, so they bought second-hand goods in China and shipped them by sea. Despite low unit prices, total revenue soared — that person is now likely the wealthiest among the author's acquaintances.

05
Breaking the Frame
Debating Who Is \"Better\" Is Itself a Sign Your Thinking Is Confined by Employee-Style Framing
Business success doesn't hinge on being \"better.\" A high corporate salary and earning a million from your own business belong to completely different evaluation systems. Someone earning a million-yuan salary almost certainly cannot reach that number without top-tier technical skills. Someone earning a million from business doesn't need an elite degree or supreme technical ability at all. Employment is more like trading the certainty of security for capped upside. Business trades uncertainty for uncapped upside. Debating which is \"better\" is a classic symptom of \"workplace thinking\" — ranking the world along a single dimension of \"better,\" when the real world runs multiple parallel evaluation systems simultaneously.
🏢

Million-Yuan-Salary Evaluation

Credentials, tech skills, experience, management ability… high-barrier filtering on a single dimension. Very few people ever reach this level.

🛒

Million-from-Business Evaluation

Market demand, random luck, execution, information asymmetry… multiple dimensions, no credential check. Anyone can take a shot.

"Debating which one is 'better' is itself a sign that employee-style thinking has the questioner confined in a box."
🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
A million-yuan salary and earning a million from your own business belong to two completely different evaluation systems — like using a thermometer to measure weight. They are simply not the same dimension. A million-yuan salary relies on technical moats and institutional recognition — sky-high entry barriers but high certainty. Earning a million from business relies on market intuition and random luck — practically zero entry barrier but enormous uncertainty. Don't compare two completely different paths through a single-dimension lens of \"better.\" The person who asks this question is essentially still viewing the world through the confined frame of employee-style thinking.
