Reviewing the Past: Mental Drills · Chapter 4 "Three Roles in a Review"

Facilitator manages the process, Inquirer digs for essence, Narrator builds the foundation — triangle synergy drives deep review

A review requires three functions to be performed by people: facilitating ensures the process stays on track; inquiring triggers thinking to reach conclusions; narrating restores the situation and dissolves confusion. In team reviews, these three roles must be clearly distinguished — whether roles are properly positioned directly determines if the review yields valuable conclusions. This chapter dissects each role's scope, techniques, and common pitfalls, building a synergistic review triangle.

01
Process Authority
Facilitator: Inside the Review, Outside the Review
The facilitator's core function is to maintain a normal discussion climate and ensure the review proceeds smoothly through its established process. The role demands being "inside the review" — grasping the logical direction at every moment — while also being "outside the review" — uninfluenced by any opinion, focused solely on whether discussion stays within the proper framework.
🧭

Facilitate

Guide along the direction of logical thinking; go with the flow.
Like the moderator at a Premier's press conference — manages procedure, calls on reporters, but expresses no view of their own.

🎯

Dominate

Decide the direction of logical thinking; cause it to happen.
Like the Premier at the conference — the protagonist of content, answering questions and shaping the substantive direction.

①
Do Not Judge Opinions
Stay neutral, "ignore" all viewpoints. Only intervene to stop deviation — never to evaluate correctness.
②
Who Can Facilitate
Senior people have a natural advantage but can delegate. Once designated, no one may usurp procedural authority — even senior leaders.
③
Prevent Role Confusion
Senior facilitators most easily conflate "process authority" with "opinion authority," becoming both referee and player.
"A good facilitator's words land precisely on the joints of the thinking process — invisible yet fitting, effortless yet just right."
02
Deep Questioning
Inquirer: A Good Question Reveals Half the Answer
The inquirer uses questions to guide thinking, explore essence, and discover patterns — the key to a successful review. Core requirements: ask questions rather than give explanations; use genuine questions rather than rhetorical ones; use questions to guide logic, letting conclusions emerge naturally through question-answer exchanges.
🔍 Facts Level
Any missing info?
Use open-ended questions
→
🧠 Logic Level
Is logic sound?
Verify info–logic relationships
→
💡 Assumptions Level
Are axioms correct?
Use closed-ended questions
Toyota "5 Why" Analysis Case: Machine stopped → overload blew fuse → bearing lubrication insufficient → pump couldn't draw oil → shaft worn → no filter installed, impurities entered. Installing a filter solved the root cause.
❓

Genuine Questions (Inquiry posture)

Open, tentative, consultative tone.
"Beyond your current explanation, is there a better one?"
Hands answering initiative to the respondent.

❗

Rhetorical Questions (Confrontational posture)

Clear stance, interrogative tone.
"Do you really think it will rain?"
Prescribes how to answer; easily triggers conflict.

⚠️ Two Tendencies to Avoid
① Fearing your own embarrassment — thinking your question is too naive → losing a new perspective
② Fearing others' embarrassment — thinking your question is too sharp → asking irrelevant questions or even explaining for them → Stopping methods: ¥50 fine or collective laughter
03
Factual Foundation
Narrator: Foundation of the Review, Primary Beneficiary of Growth
The review's process and content are built upon the narrator's narration and responses. The narrator is the event participant; besides the principal leader, the narrator gains the most from a review. Their attitude directly shapes the review climate — detachment, objectivity, truthfulness, and openness are the four core requirements.

Role Boundaries
Role Assignment and Dynamic Transformation Across Review Types
Role distribution patterns differ markedly across review types — self-review is "three-in-one," team review demands "clear separation," and reviewing others allows "flexible switching." Inquirer and narrator roles can dynamically swap during the review, but the facilitator must be held stably by one person throughout.

⚠️ The Convener / Sponsor Role
Team reviews also have a convener (review sponsor). The sponsor convenes before the review and assumes the facilitator role during it — unless they designate someone else. Even as sponsor, when acting as facilitator, they must comply with facilitation principles.
05
Triangle Synergy
Facilitate × Inquire × Narrate: The Triangle Engine Driving Reviews
The facilitator keeps direction from drifting, the inquirer digs for deep patterns, the narrator provides the factual soil — together they form an inseparable review triangle. If any corner is missing or misplaced, the review degrades into free-wheeling chatter or a blame session, losing the possibility of reaching true insights.
🪑 → ❓
Facilitator activates Inquirer
Opens topics, invites deep questioning, keeps discussion on track
❓ → 📖
Inquirer drives Narrator
Questions force the narrator to confront blind spots, moving from facts through logic to assumptions
📖 → 🪑
Narrator supports Facilitator
Truthful and complete information is the basis for judging process juncture and conclusion quality
Liu Chuanzhi's practice: "In Lenovo's IBM PC acquisition, though I didn't join the negotiations directly, my key role was continuously asking the negotiation team every kind of question — using questions to gather information, provoke thinking, and formulate contingency plans. This is what produced the most successful overseas acquisition by a Chinese company to date."

Core message of the book: In reviews, be frank, don't pretend. Where you don't understand, get clarity — pretending to understand when you don't harms yourself, the team, and growth itself.
💡 Key Takeaways
A successful review depends on clear division and effective synergy among three roles: the Facilitator acts as process authority, not opinion judge, controlling the rhythm of "open, deepen, close"; the Inquirer uses genuine questions (not rhetorical) across three levels (Facts → Logic → Assumptions), digging to root causes like Toyota's 5 Whys; the Narrator provides truthful, complete, and detailed information with detachment and objectivity, serving as the review's factual foundation. When each role performs its function and transformations flow naturally, "free-form chat" is elevated to "deep review with real conclusions" — the day the review triangle stands firm is the day team learning truly takes root.
