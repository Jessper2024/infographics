01
Moral Awakening
The Resolve of Fan Pang: An Immortal Dialogue Between Mother and Son
His mother taught Su Dongpo the "Biography of Fan Pang" from the History of the Later Han — Fan Pang calmly faced death at 33 for opposing the eunuchs. The young Dongpo looked up and asked: "Mother, if I grow up to be like Fan Pang, would you allow it?" His mother replied: "If you can be Fan Pang, can I not be Fan Pang's mother?" This dialogue was the cornerstone of Su Dongpo's lifelong character.
⚔️

Fan Pang's Choice

When arrested, the magistrate wanted to flee with him, but he refused. At his farewell he said: "If Pang dies, the trouble ends. How dare I implicate you and cause my mother to wander homeless!"

🕯️

His Mother's Reply

"Now you will share fame with Li Ying and Du Mi — what regret is there in death? Having won a noble name, can you also seek long life — can both be attained?"

02
Study Method
Copying Character by Character: Effort Rewarded
The most diligent students copied all ancient texts and dynastic histories character by character — Su Dongpo did this in his school days. This seemingly clumsy method ensured he never misused a historical reference when drafting imperial edicts. Scholars then used historical references as lawyers use precedents today — getting one wrong was a grave offense. Su Dongpo also read unpunctuated historical texts as a self-test to confirm understanding.

03
Father's Return
A Hunter Whose Last Arrow Missed: Su Xun's Hopes
His father returned home after failing the exams and lay on a couch listening to his sons read aloud — his mood like a hunter whose last arrow had missed, as if forging new arrows to send his sons after that elusive deer. Customarily, children read aloud while the father lay on a couch listening — said to be one of the most pleasing sounds in the world. The children's eyes and voices convinced him they would succeed.

04
Brotherly Bond
Brother and Teacher: Su Dongpo and Ziyou
The love between the two brothers and their loyalty through hardship was the theme Su Dongpo most often celebrated — they comforted each other in sorrow, helped each other in disaster, often dreamed of each other, and constantly exchanged poems. Ziyou was calm, cool, and practical, and somehow his official rank was always higher than his brother's. Su Dongpo wrote: "Not only is he my younger brother, he is also my worthy companion." Ziyou wrote in the epitaph: "He nurtured me as an elder brother, taught me as a teacher."

05
Naming Tradition
Given Name, Courtesy Name, Literary Name: The Multiple Identities of a Chinese Scholar
According to ancient custom, Chinese scholars always had several names — given name, courtesy name, literary name, alias, posthumous title — each layer representing different social relationships and life stages. Su Dongpo's naming system is a microcosm of this cultural tradition. Since his grandfather was named Su Xu, all "prefaces" (序) in his works became "introductions" (引).

🔑 Key Takeaway
Three layers of education shaped young Su Dongpo's lifelong character: his mother's moral lessons taught him integrity (the resolve of Fan Pang), his father's stylistic views taught him simplicity (opposing ornate emptiness), and his brother's companionship gave him the most precious friendship of his life. These three forces intertwined to shape the great soul who could later say: "Above, I can accompany the Jade Emperor; below, I can keep company with beggars."
