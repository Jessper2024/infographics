1 Dating Is a Science

The author draws an analogy with quantitative trading: just as quant trading is based on the belief that "markets are predictable," dating should also be grounded in empirical scientific methods. The author criticizes most Chinese people for never truly mastering scientific thinking despite formal education. He claims to have studied game theory under two Turing Award laureates — Prof. Andrew Yao and Prof. Silvio Micali — using this as the theoretical foundation for the book.

"The first step to learning dating is to recognize that it is a science — women's psychology and behavior are predictable, even very easily predictable."

2 Li Xinye's Four Axioms of Dating

This section constructs four axioms within a game theory framework:

🧠

Axiom 1: Feeling Person

Women's decisions are based on feelings → feelings based on instinct → instinct based on primal Nash equilibrium

👑

Axiom 2: Polygyny

Polygyny is the human norm; female hypergamy is a Nash equilibrium strategy

💔

Axiom 3: No-Love

Women only love children; for men they have only infatuation (genes) and dependence (provision)

🔍

Axiom 4: Indirect Detection

Women assess male value through indirect cues, not direct evaluation

Key Corollaries

Theorem 1: Female hypergamy is innate.

Corollary 1: Female hypergamy is morally equivalent to male polygyny.

Theorem 2: Any nation that simultaneously emancipates women and enforces monogamy will inevitably perish.

Corollary 2: Women have no romantic love (if love is defined as unconditional devotion between man and woman).

3 The "AI-Era Primate" Theory

The author characterizes modern women as "female primates living in the AI era," arguing their behavior can be decoded through "feelings" rather than logic. He analyzes feminism's underlying emotions: contempt for ordinary men and security anxiety. Examples include Yang Li's "ordinary yet confident" remark and Zhang Guimei's vocabulary. The chapter also discusses the plight of older single women — simultaneously tormented by inability to find superior men and fear of harassment by ordinary men.

4 Five Attraction Laws

Law 1: Maintain Multiple Partners

Preselection mechanism: women feel attraction when a man has multiple partners. A/B testing showed direct disclosure of polygyny intent led to faster infatuation, but also faster breakup due to insecurity. No side effects with married women.

Law 2: Always Be Ready to Leave

Abandoned cat effect: never fear losing someone. Guo Juyang was dumped in high school, yet came to Tsinghua to find the author during sophomore year.

Law 3: Domestic Violence Effect

Women are innately attracted to aggressive men. Research data shows pregnancy rates increase significantly after domestic violence. Verbal aggression serves as a legal alternative.

Law 4: Revolutionary & Artist

Revolutionaries (aggression + justice + courage) and artists (primal emotional expression) never lack women. Examples: Che Guevara, Wang Feng, Cui Jian.

5 Married vs. Single Women

The author argues married women are easier to attract than single women: single women need both infatuation and security, while married women's security needs are met by their husbands — the "other man" only needs to provide infatuation. A married woman's sense of deficiency toward her husband creates opportunities — even if overall qualities are inferior to the husband, filling a specific psychological gap can trigger infatuation. The author uses mathematical formulas to model the expected returns of a "wide-net" strategy.

Key Takeaways
