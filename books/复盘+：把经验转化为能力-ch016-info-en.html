From Google OKRs to Lenovo Retreats — How Top Companies Embed Review into Operations to Drive Strategic Evolution

Great entrepreneurs are forged through action, not taught in classrooms. Running a business is a complex, subtle system demanding not just knowledge but also experience, intuition, and wisdom. Embedding review into business operations through regular business and strategy review is a best practice at Google, Lenovo, and other world-class organizations. This chapter distills three core review approaches (QBR, annual strategy workshops, and organizational learning history) alongside four key success factors (top-leader ownership, clear division of responsibilities, inside-and-outside awareness, and skilled facilitation), while dismantling the myth that startups do not need strategy.

BENCHMARK
Google: Business Review + OKR Are a Match Made in Heaven
Google seeks learning animals with outsized ambition. Everyone from the CEO down sets stretch objectives and measurable key results (OKRs). Every quarter, the whole company convenes: leaders present their OKRs, self-score against last quarter targets, and candidly dissect their own mistakes and root causes. After the meeting, next-quarter OKRs are set and published company-wide. Regular review is the indispensable element making Google management system a transparent, closed-loop feedback mechanism — simple yet powerfully effective.

BENCHMARK
Lenovo: Embedding Review into Operations as Both Art and Discipline
Review is a fundamental component of the Lenovo methodology. Following Liu Chuanzhi three management elements (build the team, define the strategy, lead the troops), the executive team regularly holds retreats to shape strategy through review. Before major decisions, the leader engages the team and external advisors in repeated discussions to clarify principles, overarching logic, and fundamental questions — only reaching consensus before resolute execution. Lenovo divides reviews into strategic (direction and roadmap) and tactical (project methods and daily operations), forming a Plan → Execute → Review → Optimize closed-loop management system.

HOW-TO
How to Conduct Business and Strategy Review: Three Core Approaches
Drawing on global best practices, business and strategy review typically takes three forms: transform monthly/quarterly meetings into Quarterly Business Reviews (QBR) that lift managers above day-to-day firefighting; hold annual strategy review and planning workshops to confront original targets and respond to environmental shifts; create Organizational Learning Histories — dual-column documents (events + commentary) — followed by collective reflective dialogue sessions of 2-3 hours. This is not about studying external cases; it is about reflecting on the organization own journey.

04
SUCCESS FACTORS
Four Critical Success Factors for Business and Strategy Review
Strategy review may be the hardest type — no fixed cycle, broad scope, many participants, thorny issues, and heavy facilitation demands. Four factors make or break it: the top leader must personally own and drive it (strategy is the leader core duty, not a delegation task); clearly define each department role and build a lasting mechanism; never neglect external insight into industry, customers, and competitors — dedicate time for market insight and competitive analysis; and deploy skilled facilitators who combine review techniques with strategic planning tools (Balanced Scorecard, SWOT, Porter Five Forces, etc.).
👤
Top Leader Ownership
Defining strategy is the leader core responsibility. Strategy review cannot be delegated to a planning department. It is the essence of leadership.
📋
Clear Division and Mechanism
Planning office coordinates; every business function participates. Departments complete self-reviews beforehand. Institutionalize it for the long term.
🌐
Inside and Outside Awareness
Dedicate time for market insight and competitive analysis. Build decisions on a comprehensive assessment of internal and external factors.
🧭
Skilled Facilitation Is Critical
Facilitators need strategy experience + skilled review guidance. Apply professional analysis tools to avoid chaotic, fragmented discussions.
05
MYTH-BUSTING
Small Companies and Startups Need Business Review Just as Much
Many believe strategy is only for big companies — a thoroughly wrong idea. Strategy is fundamentally about holistic, long-term, foundational design: Which markets to enter? Who is the target customer? What unique value does the company offer? Every enterprise must answer these questions. Liu Chuanzhi advises entrepreneurs to evolve from groping blindly to thinking deliberately to aiming precisely. In an era where speed dominates, the secret to being fast without being chaotic is review: pick an entry point, move quickly, then review immediately — right or wrong — to learn from trial and error and adjust rapidly.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
Business and strategy review is not an extra burden — it is the core mechanism of organizational cultivation. Whether a multinational or a startup, embedding review into the rhythm of operations — QBR for quarterly performance, annual workshops for strategic calibration, and learning histories for institutional wisdom — combined with top-leader ownership, clear division of labor, inside-and-outside awareness, and professional facilitation — transforms experience into competitive capability, enabling organizations to be fast without being chaotic in a complex world.
