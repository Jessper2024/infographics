01
A New Idea Is Born
The U.S. Army reviewed why plans kept failing → created 「Commander's Intent」: no details, only mission
The U.S. military once had an extremely detailed planning system — from the President's orders cascading down to every infantryman's specific actions. The planning process took years to refine, involving repeated communication. But it had one fatal flaw: plans at the end kept failing. In the 1980s, the Army reviewed this reality and overhauled the planning process, creating the 「Commander's Intent」 (CI) concept — appearing at the top of every order, clearly stating the plan's goal and desired end state. No specific details prescribed, because details become invalid due to unexpected events. But it ensures: "You never lose the responsibility to execute the ultimate intent."
📋

Old model: exhaustive plans (often failed)

President → Joint Chiefs → General → Colonel → Captain → infantryman. Every step: what to do, what equipment, how to get supplies. More detail = more ways to fail.

🎯

New model: Commander's Intent (CI)

States only the goal: "Destroy enemy morale in SE region" or "Take Hill 4305 and provide cover for 3rd Brigade's flank." One soldier on the hill must still accomplish the mission.

02
Knowledge Framework
The three-layer structure of human knowledge: genetic, cultural, and atomic knowledge
Every person's knowledge system consists of three layers: genetic knowledge is instinct, cultural knowledge is environmental imprint, atomic knowledge is actively acquired. Genetic knowledge: evolution-internalized instincts over millennia — dodging punches, avoiding fire, seeking food when hungry, drinking when thirsty. Cultural knowledge: absorbed from one's cultural environment — Chinese use chopsticks, value filial piety, shake hands when greeting. Both are acquired unconsciously. Only atomic knowledge is actively acquired through personal effort — and this is where fupan exerts its greatest power.

03
Acquisition Paths
Of the three paths to atomic knowledge, the first two are fundamentally fupan
Atomic knowledge is acquired through three paths — and the first two are essentially fupan. Path 1: through personal practice, experiencing different things, trial and error, summarizing — this is fupan of one's own experience. Path 2: observing others, summarizing their successes and failures as your own lessons — this is fupan of others' experience. Path 3: learning from others' summarized experience, from books, from training — this is traditional learning. Of the three paths, two are fupan. This means fupan is the primary way to acquire personal knowledge.

04
Two Ways to Learn
Reading and fupan are the only two ways to learn — fupan matters more, because what you awaken to yourself cuts deepest
Liu Chuanzhi said: learning from books and others accounts for only 30%; the remaining 70% comes from learning from yourself. The key to learning from yourself is fupan. Reading absorbs knowledge and patterns summarized by others — it builds a knowledge framework, but it's one layer removed: it's someone else's "awakening" and "realization." Fupan, by contrast, produces lessons you have awakened to and realized yourself — remembered deeply, understood thoroughly. In future application, only then can you integrate and apply them seamlessly. Shen Nong tasting herbs was fupan. Scientific experiments are fupan. Children observing parents is fupan. The knowledge in books is nothing more than conclusions others reached through fupan.

05
Ultimate Conclusion
Fupan is awakening: without mastering fupan, you walk on one leg — you can't go fast or far
If you want your life to amount to something, you must first love reading, and second master fupan. All human knowledge is the result of fupan — Shen Nong tasting herbs, scientific experiments, children observing parents — all discover patterns through post-event review. In an era where people read less than ever, mastering fupan is even more critical. Without it, you have no means of learning and improving. It's like having neither leg to walk on — unable to walk at all, let alone walk fast or far. Of course, if you just want to drift through life, then fupan has nothing to do with you.

🔑 Key Takeaway
All human knowledge is the result of fupan — from Shen Nong tasting herbs to the U.S. Army's 「Commander's Intent」, without exception. Reading and fupan are the only two ways to learn, and Liu Chuanzhi's "30% vs 70%" tells us fupan is far more important than reading. Because books are other people's fupan results — one layer removed. What you arrive at through your own fupan, you have awakened to and realized yourself — remembered most deeply, understood most thoroughly. Without mastering fupan, you walk on one leg — you can't go fast or far. Fupan is your core ability to understand the world, elevate yourself, and create new knowledge.
