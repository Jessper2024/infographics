Fupan · Chapter 5「Two Methods of Fupan」

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer Fupan has two methods: Scenario Reconstruction restores events like a movie replay for objectivity; the Key Point Method cuts through the ocean of information to seize the main contradiction. But when photographic memory is unavailable, when reviewing others, or when time spans are too long, scenario reconstruction becomes impossible. How to choose the right method for each context? The answer lies in flexibly combining the Three Fields with the three Key Point sub-methods.

The two methods of fupan—Scenario Reconstruction and Key Point Method—are not opposing options but complementary tools in the reviewer's toolbox. Beginners should start with Scenario Reconstruction to build full awareness of the Information Field, Thinking Field, and Emotion Field. Once proficient, the Key Point Method becomes primary and Scenario Reconstruction becomes supplementary. The switch depends on whether you can obtain complete information, whether participants share the context, and the time span vs. information density—always choose the one with the highest information validity.

Core Method
Scenario Reconstruction: Restore the Three Fields — Complete Fupan Goes Beyond "What I Did"
Context is not merely "background" or "environment" — it is the interweaving of the Information Field (what exists), Thinking Field (what it means), and Emotion Field (why it happened) across both spatial and temporal dimensions. Simply recounting "what I did" is only a work summary — without reconstructing the Three Fields, fupan loses its foundation for understanding behavior and learning from it.

02
Key Framework
Key Point Method: Three Sub-methods to Escape the Information Deluge
When you cannot or need not reconstruct the full scenario, the Key Point Method lets you seize the main contradiction from the start. It rests on the mathematical concept of "complete coverage" — each process node is a subset; by reviewing each subset, you achieve full coverage of the event. Three sub-methods each have a distinct focus:

Identify milestone nodes

⚠ Important Note
Beginners must use the Key Point Method only as a supplement to Scenario Reconstruction. Relying on KPM too early, without the foundational training of full scenario review, risks missing key information hidden in details. The ability to perceive the Three Fields must be built through Scenario Reconstruction practice first.
03
Forward Practice
Forward KPM: Milestone-Driven Review Along Time and Content Nodes
Forward KPM traces the flow of time or event evolution to identify key nodes (milestone moments or milestone events), then reconstructs and reflects around each node. The "Resolution on Certain Questions in the History of the Party" is the definitive exemplar — facing 60 years of CPC history, Deng Xiaoping proposed "keep it rough, keep it general," compressing six decades into clearly delimited time nodes while avoiding narrative writing in favor of judgment-based language.
"Avoid narrative style; write more concisely. Important issues should be asserted with judgmental language — precise judgmental language."
— Deng Xiaoping on drafting the Resolution

04
Applied Practice
Reverse KPM & Questioning: Reverse-Engineer from Success Factors, Pursue from Doubt
Reverse KPM first defines critical success factors, then reviews what was done well, what wasn't, and which factors ultimately determined the outcome — leading to remediation and reinforcement. The Questioning Method uses "why" as the starting point, distributing questions evenly and excavating logic layer by layer — the shared methodology behind Lenovo's fupan of Dell and the book editor's comparison of two competing titles.
🔍

Lenovo's Fupan of Dell's Rise

Key Insight: Direct vs. distribution sales was merely superficial — the real competition was process efficiency and customer-base mastery.
Actions: ① Strategic contraction to specialization ② Inventory compression (15→7 days) ③ Flattened organization (7→18 sales regions) ④ Created telesales + large-account teams: dual-mode combat.

Book Editor's Fupan: Sales Gap

3 Critical Success Factors: Book quality (opening/contents/focus), promotional intensity (online ads + serialization), and distribution display (stacked vs. spine-out). After comparative fupan, average sales more than tripled.

05
Method Selection
Scenario Reconstruction vs. Key Point Method: When to Use Which?
These two methods are not either-or options but complementary tools. The selection criteria depend on whether you can obtain complete information, whether participants share context, and the event's time span and information density — always choose the method with the highest information validity. Beginners must build a foundation with Scenario Reconstruction; only once proficient should the Key Point Method become primary.
🎬

Use Scenario Reconstruction

✅ Beginners new to fupan
✅ Photographic memory or audio/video records available
✅ Reviewing events you personally experienced
✅ Short events with moderate information
✅ Need to understand oneself and others emotionally
Technique: Direct narrative, restore the Three Fields chronologically

🎯

Use Key Point Method

✅ Advanced practitioners, experienced with fupan
✅ Reviewing others — incomplete information access
✅ Long event spans, excessive information
✅ Only need to find problems and propose fixes
✅ Info Field and Thinking Field already clear
Technique: Flexible combination of Forward/Reverse/Questioning

🔑 Key Takeaway
The essence of fupan is not to summarize behaviors but to reconstruct the Three Fields (Information, Thinking, Emotion) and seize the key contradiction. Beginners must build complete perception of the Three Fields through Scenario Reconstruction, using direct narrative to ensure information is real, complete, and whole. Once proficient, use the Key Point Method (Forward, Reverse, Questioning) for efficient fupan, with Scenario Reconstruction as a supplement. The selection criterion is simple — always choose the method with the highest information validity. Whichever method you use, the purpose of fupan is always about action, not emotion: learn from past experience, find the right approach, and uncover the underlying laws.
