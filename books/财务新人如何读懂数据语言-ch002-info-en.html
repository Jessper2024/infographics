How Finance Newcomers Can Understand the Language of Data · Chapter 2
"What Will Future Accounting Look Like?"

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → AnswerThe assignment for Zhejiang University's Supply Chain International class was an open-ended question — what changes and disruptions will rapid technological advancement bring to financial accounting? Technology is evolving at dizzying speed while traditional accounting systems seem untouched. What will future accounting look like? Diverse thinking from ZJU students reveals six major trends.

A question with no standard answer. ZJU students delivered far more diverse responses than expected: from virtual currencies to multi-organizations, from historical cost to real-time reporting — six major future trends. Each trend challenges fundamental accounting assumptions — entity concept, historical cost, asset definition, reporting cycles. The article reminds us: "Be alert to big things, be clever about small things" — when Google's machines can write poetry, every professional must stay vigilant about the future.

01
Currency Revolution
Virtual Currency: From Real Currency to Dual Virtual-Real Valuation
Financial bookkeeping uses real currency as the unit of measurement. As virtual currencies enter usage, will organizations need "virtual" and "real" sets of books? When Bitcoin, central bank digital currencies, and various tokens become everyday transaction media, the traditional monetary measurement basis faces a fundamental challenge. Valuation systems may shift from the certainty of a single currency measure to a dual-dimension virtual-real parallel.
💡 Core question: When the monetary measurement benchmark is upended, how can ledger systems accommodate dual "virtual-real" valuation?
02
Organizational Change
Multi-Organizations: The Collapse of the Accounting Entity Assumption
Traditional finance treats the legal entity as the accounting subject. But future organizations will feature multi-headed aggregation — for Project A, micro-orgs A and B combine; after the project ends, A combines with another organization. Japan's Amoeba model and Haier's micro-organization entrepreneurship are early signals of this trend. The fluidity and fusion of assessment units will shatter the "accounting entity" — a foundational assumption of financial accounting.

03
Cost Revolution
From Historical Cost to Current Cost: IoT's Information Revolution
Currently most assets are valued at historical cost — a ¥50,000 machine is booked at its purchase-time value. As market information becomes increasingly real-time, when information costs approach zero, current cost will replace historical cost. The proliferation of IoT allows every object to have real-time networked comparative value — the traditional historical cost principle will be completely rewritten.

04
Asset Revolution
Redefining Assets: Data More Valuable Than Oil
The business model of giving away products to obtain user data is overturning the sole reference standard of physical objects as assets. A phone's value may be zero, while the user registration data obtained through free phone distribution is appraised as a valuable asset. In an era where "data is more valuable than oil," the asset expression of customer data is a matter of time — the definition of assets extends from tangible physical objects to intangible data.
  • 📱Phone value may be zero → free acquisition of user data
  • 📋User registration data → appraised as a valuable asset
  • 🛢️Data more valuable than oil → customer data will appear on balance sheets
  • 💎Asset definition extends from tangible objects to intangible data assets
05
Cycle Revolution
From Annual Reports to Real-Time Reporting: The Sensor + MES Revolution
Traditional financial reporting — from annual to quarterly to monthly — the granularity is never fine enough. When sensor costs become negligible and every machine, every operator is connected through MES, the finished products per minute and the cost settlement per process step can be reflected in real time. A new financial report can be generated daily, even every minute. Technology is ready; what's missing is industry consensus on new standards — when everyone gets used to weekly and daily metrics, new accounting standards will emerge organically.

🔑 Key Takeaway
"Be alert to big things, be clever about small things" — every finance professional must stay vigilant about the future. Virtual currencies, multi-organizations, current cost, data assets, real-time reporting, non-monetary transactions — six major trends are rewriting accounting from six fundamental dimensions. When Google's machines can write poetry, can we still be just white-collar professionals compiling reports in front of a computer? What future accounting will look like cannot be asserted, but it certainly won't be the old image of a report-compiler.
