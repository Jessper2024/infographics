Those Ming Matters · Zhu Yuanzhang
Chapter 13 "Next Target, Zhang Shicheng!"

After eliminating Chen Youliang, Zhu Yuanzhang turns his full force against Eastern Wu. From multi-directional encirclement to an eight-month siege of Pingjiang, the last obstacle to southern unification is removed.

Situation: With Chen Youliang destroyed, only two powers remain in the Yangtze basin—Zhu Yuanzhang and Zhang Shicheng. Complication: Zhang holds wealthy Jiangnan and the allegedly impregnable city of Pingjiang. Meanwhile, Zhu's own nephew Zhu Wenzheng secretly defects to Zhang's side. Problem: How to crush Pingjiang's legendary walls while neutralizing internal betrayal? Answer: First isolate Pingjiang by taking all surrounding cities, then surround it with eleven army columns and siege towers, forcing surrender after eight grueling months.

Opponent Analysis
Zhang Shicheng's Fatal Flaw: Ambitious but Small-Minded
Zhang was a salt smuggler who once held out against a million-strong Yuan army for three months at Gaoyou with just 3,000 men—courageous, but no strategist. After conquering wealthy Jiangnan, he settled into comfortable opulence, delegating military affairs to his playboy brother Zhang Shixin. Zhu Yuanzhang's verdict: "Small vessel, no grand vision." In an elimination tournament, comfort-seekers lose.

02
Strategy
Cut Off Support Before the Main Assault
Generals wanted a direct strike on Pingjiang. Zhu Yuanzhang disagreed. Attacking Pingjiang head-on would trigger relief forces from Hangzhou, making the city impossible to take. Instead, he first captured Hangzhou and Huzhou, eliminating Zhang's outer defenses. With Pingjiang isolated, only the city itself remained. This decision proved his military genius once again.

03
The Siege
Eight Months at Pingjiang: Will vs. Firepower
Pingjiang's walls, built with glutinous rice mortar, were legendary. Zhu deployed eleven army columns attacking all eight gates plus the northeast, southwest, and northwest corners. Three-story wooden siege towers overlooked the city, raining arrows, bullets, and cannon fire. Defenders had to dodge projectiles from above while bearing the stench of tower soldiers' waste raining down. Zhang Shicheng held out eight brutal months.

Trust Collapses
Zhu Wenzheng's Defection: The Nephew Who Betrayed Everything
Zhu Yuanzhang's most trusted nephew defected to Zhang Shicheng—over a promotion snub. Zhu Wenzheng had earned the greatest merit in defeating Chen Youliang, but when Zhu Yuanzhang offered him a reward, he modestly declined, expecting to be pressed. Zhu Yuanzhang simply awarded the post to someone else. Feeling betrayed, Zhu Wenzheng secretly allied with Zhang Shicheng. Zhu Yuanzhang personally went to Hongdu and whipped him. The incident destroyed his trust in everyone—forever.

05
Unification of Jiangnan
Zhang Shicheng's Death: The End of Eastern Wu
After Pingjiang fell, Zhang Shicheng fought street by street before being captured. He killed his own family, tried to hang himself, was rescued—then imprisoned. When Li Shanchang interrogated him, Zhang only said: "You're just a dog—tell your master to come." When Zhu Yuanzhang came personally, Zhang refused to surrender, saying: "I lost only because heaven favored you, not me." Enraged, Zhu killed him and burned his body to ashes. 🔑 Core Takeaway
Eliminating Zhang Shicheng was the decisive step toward unification. Zhu Yuanzhang's strategic brilliance lay not just in battlefield tactics but in grand strategy—"cut off the wings before striking the nest" saved countless lives. Zhu Wenzheng's defection changed Zhu forever: from this moment, deep paranoia took root, leading to the eventual purge of founding generals. Zhang Shicheng, a smuggler who died unbroken, earned grudging respect. With Zhang gone, the final enemy—the Yuan dynasty—loomed on the horizon.
