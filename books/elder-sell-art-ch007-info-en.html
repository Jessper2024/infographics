The New Sell & Sell Short
Chapter 7 "Shorting Stocks"

SCQA · Situation → Conflict → Question → Answer Stock markets rise over the long term, but declines are twice as fast. Short sellers trade against the prevailing wind, seeking profit in faster moves while performing a public service. The question: how do you short safely and profitably in a market that rises over the long term?

01
Mindset
Who Are Short Sellers and What Does the Market Need from Them?
Most traders are naturally biased toward going long. But trading is simply about betting on the direction of price movement. Short sellers perform three public services: increasing supply at highs, buying first during crashes, and dampening excessive volatility. Declines are twice as fast — that's the short seller's natural edge.

02
Market Structure
Why Are Tops Harder to Identify Than Bottoms?
Market bottoms are driven by fear — longs who can no longer bear the pain sell at any price, making bottoms narrow and brief. Market tops are driven by greed — the pleasure of profit persists, and bulls keep pouring in money, making tops wide, prolonged, and riddled with false breakouts.
📈

Market Top · Greed-Driven

Wide and prolonged. Frequent false upside breakouts. MACD divergences need repeated confirmation. FI spike up ≠ top signal.

📉

Market Bottom · Fear-Driven

Narrow and brief. Panic selling washes out weak hands; reversal follows quickly. FI spike down = clearer exhaustion signal.

03
Strategy
Shorting at Tops vs. Shorting in Downtrends
Elder divides short-selling into two modes. Shorting at tops is like "running with the bulls" — fighting the crowd at market peaks, requiring smaller positions, wider stops, and readiness to re-enter. Shorting in established downtrends is safer: enter near value and cover in oversold territory. ≥30% of channel width earns an A-grade.

Dual Confirmation
How to Find Short Candidates Using Fundamentals + Technicals?
Fundamentals act as the "signal generator"; technicals are the "aiming scope" — both must confirm; conflicting signals mean no trade. Scan industries → subsectors → constituents, sorted by price high-to-low, looking for "high and wobbly" stocks. No matter how compelling the fundamental story, do not trade unless the technical picture confirms.

Risk Control
What Risk Metrics Must Short Sellers Monitor?
Your first short must be small — position size amplifies emotions. Two essential filters: Short Interest Ratio (<10% tolerable, >20% dangerous) and Days-to-Cover (<10 days safe, >20 days signals stampede risk). Use these as auxiliary tools for entry/exit, not as standalone trading signals.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Successful short-selling hinges on acknowledging and exploiting market asymmetry — declines are twice as fast (that's the edge), while the long-term uptrend is the headwind. Choose the method that fits you (top-shorting or trend-shorting), insist on small initial positions, confirm every entry with dual signals, rigorously monitor short interest metrics, and keep a visual trading journal. Shorting is not fighting the market — it is finding the profit window that opens when the crowd heads in the opposite direction.
