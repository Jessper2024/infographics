We Have Always Been Reviewing

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer Review seems like a professional term, but traders review after market close, teachers review after class, and military forces review after exercises—all routine. In truth, we all review unconsciously—daily recalling, reflecting, and summarizing are the core steps of review. How does review permeate every aspect of daily life? It doesn't require physically redoing things—only mental reconstruction.

Review is not an esoteric management term—it's a method everyone uses every day. Traders review daily market movements to build market sense, teachers review classroom activities to improve lessons, military forces review after exercises to find problems—these are all review. But more common are everyday reviews: after a gathering, wondering why a friend seemed unhappy; after talking with a boss, reflecting on whether your attitude was too casual; after meeting a client, summarizing their excitement points; a child fell ill—was it last night's blanket or bad food? Review doesn't require physically redoing things—it is mental reconstruction of events, which can be real-scenario replay or a paper brainstorm.

Trader Review
Trader Daily Review: From Trend Analysis to Market Sense
Every day after market close, traders review the day's transactions and the full-day market trend. They review each stock's movement, think about why it moved that way, and propose a plausible explanation. Through review, each trader distills their trading techniques and forms their own trading logic. Additionally, they hope to develop "market sense" through review—this sense allows one to perceive a stock's future direction, seize the advantage, conquer the market, and achieve profitability.

03
Everyday Review
Everyday Review Is Everywhere
Nobody is unfamiliar with review—many people conduct review consciously or unconsciously in daily life. Recalling, reflecting, and summarizing are important steps in the review method, and also a simplified form of review. After a gathering, recalling why a friend seemed unhappy; after talking with your boss, reflecting on whether your attitude was appropriate; after meeting a client, summarizing their excitement points—all these are using the review method. Review is everywhere in our work and life, and we all use it.
04
No Physical Redo
Review Doesn't Require Physically Redoing
Review does not require us to actually redo things every time. Go review involves replaying stones in order, but military drills don't need another round of combat to find problems, and teachers don't need to re-teach a class to improve. This is unnecessary and infeasible. Review's true form is mental reconstruction, not physical reenactment.

05
Advisor as Review
Seeking Advice IS Review: The Essence of Asking a Friend to Help
When we face problems, we often ask friends: "Help me think it through." This "advising" is less about giving ideas and more about review. By recounting past experiences, letting another person synthesize and sort through them, new angles and conclusions can emerge, leading to clearer understanding and guidance for subsequent action.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Review is not an elevated management concept—it is a meta-capacity that humanity uses daily without even realizing it. From traders' daily review to teachers' classroom reflection, from military exercise review to "help me think it through" among friends—review is everywhere. It doesn't require physical reenactment; through mental reconstruction, capability gain is achieved. Recognizing review's universality allows us to apply this method more consciously, moving from unconscious review to high-quality deliberate review.
