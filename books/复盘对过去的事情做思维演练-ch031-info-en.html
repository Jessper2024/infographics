Fupan · Ch.31 The Questioner (設問人)

SCQA · Situation → Complication → Question → Answer A good question reveals half the answer — yet people hesitate to ask, fearing their question is naive or too sharp. How do we use questions to guide thinking, uncover essence, and discover patterns? Through the Questioner role: wielding questions as weapons, relentlessly following up, using interrogative rather than rhetorical tone, and guiding logic through questioning — penetrating the three layers of Information → Thinking → Assumption to approach the truth.

The Questioner holds the key to successful fupan. It's through their questions that the group explores the essence of events and discovers hidden patterns. The question is the answer — a good question already reveals half the answer. Questions are also carriers of logical thinking, revealing hidden thought processes and different perspectives. Every fupan participant can be a Questioner, as long as they are not the current Narrator.

01
Core Proposition
The Question Is the Answer: A Good Question Reveals Half the Truth
Asking the right question foreshadows finding the answer. Liu Chuanzhi, during Lenovo's acquisition of IBM's PC division, didn't join the negotiations but relentlessly questioned the negotiating team — gathering crucial intelligence and pushing the team to consider every possible scenario, forming contingency plans. This created the most successful Chinese overseas acquisition to date.

❌ The Cost of Not Asking

Fear of looking foolish blocks further thought. Fear of offending leads to superficial questions or even helping to explain away others' answers. Both rob the team of new perspectives and reduce chances of discovering the essence.

✅ To Ask Is to Think

Every question triggers new thought. Questioning is the role's core duty — treat it as routine. The cycle of new questions and new thinking propels both Questioner and Narrator forward together.

"One person's trash is another's treasure. A casual remark may become someone else's key to enlightenment."
02
Question Layers
Three Progressive Layers: Information → Thinking → Assumption
Questions divide into three progressive layers, each deeper than the last. The Information Layer confirms whether facts are objective and complete. The Thinking Layer verifies whether logic is sound and information is correctly applied. The Assumption Layer examines a person's internal standards and axioms. For major event fupan, go one step further and verify whether the assumptions themselves are correct.

03
Relentless Follow-Up
The 5-Why Method: Toyota's Classic Approach to Root Cause
Relentless questioning is the best way to reach conclusions. Continuous follow-up exposes insufficient thinking, reveals contradictions, and identifies knowledge gaps on the spot. Toyota's 5-Why method is the classic case: Machine stopped → Overloaded → Poor lubrication → Pump couldn't draw oil → Worn shaft → No filter installed. Final answer: install a filter.
🔄 Correct Question Path
→
Use questions as offensive weapons, Narrator's answers as defensive shields. Questions and logic emerge clearly in this mental sparring. Ask 3 times, 10 times — whatever solves the problem.
⚠️ Wrong Question Path
→
Wrong direction leads to wrong conclusions: "Why did the fuse blow under overload?" → Replace fuse. "Why overload?" → "Too many orders" → Buy new equipment. Wrong questions never solve real problems.
04
Tone Strategy
Interrogative vs. Rhetorical Questions: Tone Determines Effect
Use more interrogative questions; use fewer or no rhetorical questions. Interrogative questions adopt a posture of seeking to learn — humble, exploratory, implicitly respectful. Rhetorical questions adopt a challenging posture — confrontational, implying the questioner knows better. As Liu Chuanzhi said: "Speak directly, but speak well." Interrogative questions are one way of "speaking well."

05
Advanced Skill
Guiding Logic Through Questions: Let Conclusions Emerge Naturally
The most challenging skill: don't state conclusions directly — let them emerge through the question-answer process. This requires interlocking questions that align with the Narrator's responses. Open questions at the Information layer to maximize facts; flexible choice at the Thinking layer; closed questions at the Assumption layer for precision. The example: Ms. Huang guides Ms. Sun to realize "the boss is the market's representative" — asking only questions, letting Sun reach the conclusion herself.

🔑 Core Takeaway
A good Questioner with good questions determines whether fupan succeeds. Use interrogative questions — not rhetorical ones — to maintain a learning stance. Probe through three layers (Information → Thinking → Assumption) to peel away the surface. With relentless follow-up, exhaust cause and effect. Ultimately, let conclusions emerge naturally from the question-answer interplay rather than imposing them — because only conclusions reached through one's own thinking can truly be grasped and applied.
