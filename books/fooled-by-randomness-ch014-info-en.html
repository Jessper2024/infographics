Life is filled with randomness; fortune can turn in an instant — Stoic philosophy provides an answer two millennia old: how to face adversity with nobility

PHILOSOPHICAL ROOTS
Stoics & Epicureans: Two Paths to the Same Mastery
The two great opposing schools of ancient Greece — the ascetic Stoics and the pleasure-seeking Epicureans — agreed on one point: one must master one's own fate. Stoicism, founded by Zeno of Citium, developed in Roman times into a way of life centered on virtue, wisdom, integrity, and courage. Epicureanism, though advocating pleasure, equally holds that one should master fate. The "final choice" in the face of uncontrollable randomness is the shared core belief of both schools.

POETIC REVELATION
Cavafy's "The God Abandons Antony": Stoic Spirit in a Poem
At Jacqueline Kennedy's funeral, her last companion, Maurice Tempelsman, read Cavafy's poem "The God Abandons Antony." The poem describes Antony after his defeat by Octavian — abandoned by all, even his horse gone over to the enemy, and his patron god Bacchus departing. The poet's counsel: do not mourn your misfortune, do not deny everything, do not be deceived by empty hopes. When emotion trembles, simply listen; do not wail and plead like a coward.

HUMAN TRUTH
Emotions Are Not Sinful; Behavior Is What Counts
Stoicism is not the cold rigidity we imagine. Seneca's Letters from a Stoic has a deeply humane side and is highly readable — Taleb often gives copies to his trader friends. Having emotional reactions is not wrong; it does not forfeit dignity — humans are born with emotions. What is wrong is failing to stand tall like a hero, or at least like a person of dignity. Yet even Cavafy himself, dying of throat cancer, failed to embody his own poem's spirit — he clung to visitors, refusing to let them leave. Between knowing the principle and living it lies the unbridgeable chasm of human nature.

ULTIMATE CONVICTION
The One Thing Fortune Cannot Control: Your Behavior
No matter how complex our choices or how skilled we are at managing luck, randomness is always the final judge. All we have left is dignity — behavior that does not need to look to the surrounding environment for validation. This may not be the most appropriate course of action, but it gives us the best feeling. The epic hero is judged by conduct, not by success or failure.

🔑 KEY TAKEAWAY
The next time you face adversity, begin by emphasizing elegance in your personal conduct. Show that no matter the circumstances, you "know how to survive." When losing money, be nicer to your staff. When diagnosed with cancer, do not wail. Even if your spouse runs off with someone else, never indulge in self-pity. The one thing Lady Fortune cannot control is your behavior. Good luck!
