1 Anti-Slut Defense (ASD)

Women universally exhibit an "Anti-Slut Defense" before engaging sexually with a new partner — verbally refusing while simultaneously seducing through behavior and expressions. The author analyzes its dual function:

Surface Function

Maintain virtuous image
Avoid being labeled a "slut"
Protect social reputation

Deep Function

Deny responsibility for "initiating"
Let the man "force" for masculine appeal
Preserve option for future accusation

The author notes extreme cases may escalate to false assault allegations, citing the Liu Qiangdong Minnesota case. Strategy: stay清醒, claim impotence, trigger the abandoned-cat effect and leave.

2 How to Court: Attract First, Pursue Second

The author emphasizes that "courting" must follow "attraction" — never precede it. Core timeline:

①

Attraction Phase

Display high value, create infatuation
No gifts or transfers

②

Courtship Phase

Provide a graceful exit, romantic ritual
Poems/piano/candlelit dinner

③

Intimacy Phase

Let it happen naturally
Passionate but not desperate

The author quotes Tong Jincheng: "Money is for women to see, not to spend on women." Experimental finding: the no-money group outperformed the money-transfer group, as transfers triggered materialistic calculation.

4 STD Prevention System

The author proposes a systematic STD prevention methodology, analogizing sexual activity to surgery:

HIV/AIDS

Incurable, highly fatal. Pre-exposure (PrEP) and post-exposure (PEP) prophylactics available but extend testing window periods.

Syphilis

Caused by Treponema pallidum bacteria. Early-stage treatable with penicillin or doxycycline; late-stage causes serious harm.

Hepatitis B

Chronic HBV not fully curable. After Merck's 1989 vaccine technology transfer to China, post-90s generation largely controlled. Booster recommended.

HPV

Can cause cervical cancer and genital warts. 9-valent vaccine recommended for all ages and genders. China opened male HPV vaccination in 2025.

5 Surgical-Safety Protection Framework

The author proposes a protection framework analogizing sexual activity to surgery:

🩺

Pre-Op Preparation

Rapid saliva/blood testing
Ask about recent risk exposure
Joint shower, skin inspection

🧤

Intra-Op Procedure

Condom = surgical glove
Ensure no skin exposure
Prevent direct fluid contact

💊

Post-Op Care

Immediate wash & disinfect if exposed
Take prophylactic medication
Regular follow-up testing

The chapter also covers social responsibility in STD prevention and lists common STIs: HIV, syphilis, gonorrhea, chlamydia, mycoplasma, hepatitis B, HPV, and HSV.
