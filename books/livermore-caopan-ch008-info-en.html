01
Core Tool

The Six-Column Table: A Trading Map for Anticipating Future Movements

Livermore designed specially printed sheets with six columns per stock — this table is what he called his "map for anticipating future market movements."

The six columns correspond to six types of price movement: Secondary Rally, Natural Rally, Upward Trend, Downward Trend, Natural Reaction, and Secondary Reaction. Prices are classified and recorded in each column according to specific rules, forming a three-dimensional panorama of the market.

The four pencil columns (two on each side) represent "natural oscillations" — they help the trader understand the initial stages of reactions and rallies. The two central columns recorded in ink are the ones that truly issue action signals. Once an ink color persists, the record "speaks to you," telling its story clearly and unmistakably.

02
Operational Rule

Three-Color Ink Coding: Making the Record Speak

Ink color is far from arbitrary — it is the language of the entire recording system. "Whether red ink or black ink, once persistently used, it will tell a story unmistakably."

🖤 Black Ink → Upward Trend Column

Price data recorded in the Upward Trend column uses black ink; the left two columns (Secondary Rally, Natural Rally) use pencil. Persistent black ink means the trend is up — the market is saying: "Hold on."

❤️ Red Ink → Downward Trend Column

Price data recorded in the Downward Trend column uses red ink; the right two columns (Natural Reaction, Secondary Reaction) use pencil. Persistent red ink means the trend is down — the market is saying: "Stay away or go short."

If you are always recording in pencil, you realize you are merely noting natural oscillations. When it switches from black ink to red (or vice versa), the trend has changed direction — this is the clear signal demanding immediate action. The ink color itself is a direct dialogue between the market and you.

03
Judgment Criterion

The 6-Point Movement Criterion & Composite Price Verification

By measuring movement amplitudes, Livermore established two ironclad rules from which he never deviated and for which he never made excuses: 6 points per stock confirms a natural movement; 12 points combined from two stocks verifies a trend change.

📏 6-Point Rule
Only when a stock priced at $30+ rallies or reacts approximately 6 points from an extreme can a Natural Rally or Natural Reaction be confirmed — this does NOT mean the trend has changed, only that a natural movement is underway.
🔗 Composite Price
Combine the price movements of two stocks from the same group to form the "Composite Price." When each moves about 6 points in the opposite direction, totaling ≥12 points combined, this is the definitive verification signal for a trend change.
🛡️ False Signal Guard
A single stock can sometimes form a large enough move to enter the trend column, but relying on just one stock risks false signals. Composite verification provides essential protection.

Once the movement reaches a recording point, in an uptrend, record every new high; in a downtrend, record every new low — continuing until a reversal movement begins.

04
Classic Case Study

The Silent Signal of Steel Stocks: Market Action Is the Only Reason You Need

The deepest lesson from the 1939–1940 steel stock case: you never need to know the reason before acting — market action itself is always the only reason you need to know.

In 1939, after all stocks experienced a rapid advance, the outbreak of war in Europe triggered a natural reaction. Subsequently, all stocks in the four prominent groups recovered their reactions and made new highs — except the Steel group, which conspicuously failed to follow. The market record had already made it unmistakably clear: the upward movement in Steel stocks was over.

"If you insist on figuring out the reason before you act, you will miss the opportunity and fail to take appropriate action at the right moment! The only reason an investor or speculator ever needs to know lies solely in the market's performance itself."

The core lesson: whenever the market does not act right, or does not act the way it should — that is sufficient reason to change your opinion, and to change it immediately. By the time you learn the full story at some future date, it will be too late to profit from it.

05
Trading Discipline

Eliminate Minor Fluctuations, Forge Action Courage Through Discipline

Livermore's system does not chase minor intraday swings — it captures only the beginning and end of major movements. Whether you succeed depends on the courage and decisiveness with which you act when the record issues its signal.

Livermore frankly admitted this method was not perfect, but after years of testing and refinement, he "felt he had approached a state" where he could rely on it. The key lies in sustained discipline — from then on he never deviated from those point values, never made exceptions, never made excuses to bend the rules.

✅ What This System IS For

Capturing major market movements, revealing the beginning and end of moves
Applicable to active stocks priced above $30
Adhering strictly to 6-point/12-point rules, no exceptions
Maintaining the record personally and continuously
Listening only to signals from the market itself

❌ What This System Is NOT For

Chasing minor intraday fluctuations
Making extra trades on small swings within major moves
Waiting to find a "good reason" before acting
Relying on isolated signals from a single stock
Bending the rules when results don't match expectations

"Opportunity knocks but once. There is no room for hesitation — you must train your will along these lines. If you wait for someone to explain it, or tell you the reason, or give you courage, the moment for action will have already slipped away."

🗺️
Core Takeaway

The essence of the Livermore Market Analysis Method: through a six-column table, three-color ink coding, the 6-point movement criterion, and two-stock composite price verification, construct an objective, quantified "trading map." It filters out all minor fluctuations, capturing only the beginning and end of major trends; it relies on no external reasons, obeying only the signals the market itself emits. Keep recording, strictly adhere, never make exceptions — market action itself is always the only reason you ever need to know.
