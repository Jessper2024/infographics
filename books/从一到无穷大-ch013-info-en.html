01
Core Framework
Rotating Spacetime Coordinates: Two Faces of One Reality
Space distances and time intervals are merely different projections of a fixed four-dimensional interval onto coordinate axes. Just as rotating a 2D coordinate system changes projections on x and y axes, rotating spacetime axes converts parts of space distance into time interval and vice versa. The secret of "rotating spacetime axes" is mundane—board a moving bus and the time axis tilts. From a moving observer, both the space distance and time interval between two events differ from a stationary observer. This is not an illusion; it is a property of spacetime itself.

02
Experimental Revolution
The Vanishing Ether Wind: Michelson Shatters Classical Spacetime
The greatest error of 19th-century physics: assuming the ether possessed mechanical properties like ordinary matter. Michelson built an ingenious interferometer, using the null method to compare light speeds in two perpendicular directions. By expectations, the ether wind accompanying Earth's motion should produce a ~0.01% speed difference. But the result: interference fringes did not budge. No matter the direction, the ether wind had zero effect on light speed. This "stubborn fact" shattered Newton's absolute spacetime. The only reasonable explanation: moving bodies undergo a universal spatial contraction in the direction of motion—dependent on speed alone, not material composition.

03
Time Dilation
Moving Objects Shrink, Time Slows Down
Length contraction and time dilation are two faces of one coin: the same factor governs both—division for space, multiplication for time. A car at 50 mph shortens by one atomic nucleus diameter; a 100m rocket at 25,000 mph contracts by only 0.01mm—daily speeds produce imperceptible effects. But near light speed: 50% c → 86% length; 90% → 45%; 99% → 14%. If a spaceship travels at 99.99999999% c round-trip to Sirius (9 light-years away), 18 years pass on Earth while the astronaut feels just a few hours—leaving after breakfast, returning before dinner, while friends have eaten 6,570 dinners in between. Yet no matter how hard we try, the speed of light remains forever unreachable; the last decimal can never be conquered.

04
General Relativity
Gravity Is Not a Force—It Is Curved Spacetime
Massive bodies curve spacetime around them; matter moves along geodesics (shortest paths) through curved space. Two-dimensional shadow scientists could determine whether their surface is flat or curved by measuring triangle angle sums: flat=180°, sphere>180°, saddle<180°. Our 3D world works the same way—the Sun's mass curves the space around it, deflecting starlight passing nearby. The 1919 solar eclipse observation confirmed: starlight deflection of 1.61″±0.30, matching Einstein's prediction of 1.75″. Thus, gravity as an "independent force" disappears, replaced by pure geometry: all matter moves along geodesics in the curved space created by massive bodies.

05
Shape of the Universe
Closed vs. Open Space: How Big Is the Universe?
Beyond local "space pimples"—curvatures caused by massive bodies—is the universe's overall face flat or curved? Positively curved space corresponds to a sphere or any closed geometry, bending uniformly in all directions; negatively curved space resembles a saddle, bending upward in one direction and downward in another. On a sphere, area within a given radius grows slower than on a plane; on a saddle, faster. Likewise, 3D observers can deduce whether the universe is finite and closed or infinite and open by measuring giant triangle angle sums or counting matter within different radii. This question will be answered in the cosmological discussion of Chapter 10.

🔑 Key Takeaway
Space and time are not separate absolute stages but different projections of a four-dimensional spacetime continuum. In moving systems, both time and space axes rotate together—automatically yielding length contraction, time dilation, and relativity of simultaneity. Michelson's experiment shattered the ether illusion; Einstein replaced gravity-the-force with curved-spacetime-the-geometry. Matter tells spacetime how to curve; curved spacetime tells matter how to move. This is the essence of the 20th century's greatest scientific revolution.
