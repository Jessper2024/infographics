Shorting Non-Equity Products: Three Doors, Three Survival Rules

Shorting is completely unrestricted in futures, options, and forex markets — there are no uptick rules or insider trading bans. Yet freedom does not equal safety: futures follow a brutal "slow rise, fast crash" rhythm, option buyers are destined to lose like gamblers, and the retail forex market is a platform-vs-client conflict-of-interest trap.

Key insight: Successful short-sellers must master each market's unique dynamics — short futures by exploiting periodic collapses, always stand on the seller's side in options, and choose transparent platforms while riding forex trends. Three markets, three shorting logics — but discipline and risk control are the shared foundation.

01
Core Pattern

Futures: Slow Rise, Fast Crash — The Carrying-Cost Cycle

Futures prices are steadily pushed upward by carrying costs — storage, financing, and insurance — creating a slow, grinding uptrend. But underlying value hasn't risen proportionally, so the price structure must be interrupted by periodic sharp collapses. Cocoa futures can rise for weeks or months, only to crash back to origin in a single week. Gold climbed $85 over three months, then surrendered 64% of those gains in just three days.

Price Anchors

Ceiling and Floor: The Pricing Anchors for Shorting Futures

Unlike stocks, futures have natural pricing boundaries. Before shorting, always determine whether price is closer to the ceiling or the floor. Short near the ceiling, cover near the floor.

📈 Ceiling · Substitute Cost

Commodity price ceilings are set by the cost of substitutes. When grain prices soar, farmers switch to cheaper alternative feeds, collapsing grain demand. Prices may briefly spike above the ceiling during market euphoria, but they won't stay — this is precisely when savvy traders short.

📉 Floor · Production Cost

The price floor is the cost of production. When prices fall below production cost, producers cut output, supply drops, and prices rise. Exceptions exist — a desperate poor nation selling commodities for hard currency while paying workers in devalued local money — but these don't last.

Options: The Dividing Line Between Winners and Losers

In the options market, the line separating winners from losers is not about skill — it's between buyers and sellers. Winners are always sellers; losers are always buyers. Options are wasting assets — you can be right about direction, but if time runs out, your option expires worthless.

Tactics

Covered vs. Naked: Two Paths to Selling Options

Two paths exist for selling options: covered writing uses owned shares as collateral to earn premium income, while naked writing creates options out of thin air against account capital. The former is conservative income enhancement; the latter maximizes profit but demands iron discipline.

🛡️ Covered Writing

Sell calls against owned shares (or puts against short positions). Stock flat → keep premium. Stock down → premium cushions loss. Stock up & exercised → profit on shares + premium. Best for large portfolios; single-stock covered writing is labor-intensive for modest gain.

⚡ Naked Writing

Create options from nothing and sell them into the market. The most stressful, most promising, and most dangerous form. Requires substantial capital ($100K–$250K) and ruthless discipline. Worst case: buying shares at the strike price — entirely acceptable to a professional.

Risk Alert

Forex: Bucket Shop Traps and Trend Trading Rules

Forex is the most trend-driven market in the world — currency trends can persist for years. But retail forex dealers harbor severe conflicts of interest: they act as your counterparty, meaning your profit is their loss. They offer insane 400:1 leverage that renders money management meaningless. Currency futures are the more transparent alternative.

⚠️
Beware Bucket Shops: Most forex dealers do not route your orders to the market — they take the other side. Spreads, commissions, and interest on your capital steadily drain your account. Elder's advice: until a transparent system emerges, only one word — caution!
💡
Choose Currency Futures: Electronically traded, well-regulated, and far more transparent. You must understand futures trading before shorting forex futures. Every forex trade is a spread trade: buying one currency automatically shorts the other.

Key Takeaway

The essence of shorting non-equity products lies not in technique, but in understanding each market's game structure: short futures by exploiting the slow-rise/fast-crash pricing rhythm; short options by always standing on the side of time (the seller); short forex by avoiding bucket shops that bet against their own clients. The shared foundation across all three markets: sufficient capital, ruthless discipline, and the rule of cutting losses short while letting profits run.
