Wenhua Kulv · Ch.11 "The Hangzhou Declaration"

The secret of the world's most beautiful city: not nature's gift, but generations of cultural giants fighting barefoot in the mud to save an ecosystem

Core Insight: All of Hangzhou's splendor hangs on a fragile ecological thread — ecology precedes culture, and this city is the result of Bai Juyi, Qian Liu, and Su Dongpo setting aside their literary fame to wage desperate battles in the silt and sludge.

QuestionA shallow bay destined by natural law to become saline wasteland — how did Hangzhou defy nature to become the world's most beautiful city?

AnswerHangzhou's beauty is entirely man-made: Emperor Yang of Sui built the Grand Canal → Bai Juyi dredged West Lake → King Qian Liu tamed the waters and built the city → Su Dongpo launched emergency rescue. Four generations of giants completed this ecological relay; even the greatest poets came here and set aside verse to become ecological rescuers.

1
A World Question

Why did Marco Polo call Hangzhou the world's finest?

Marco Polo's judgment carries weight — he came from Venice, itself Europe's most beautiful city, with the eye to judge. Moreover, he arrived after Hangzhou had suffered devastation from the Mongol conquest; even in its damaged state, it was still "noble and beautiful to the world's highest degree."

Even more remarkable: Hangzhou's origin was utterly ordinary. It began as a shallow bay, silted into a saltwater lake by tides and Yangtze sediment. By natural law, aquatic plants would proliferate, water would evaporate, wetlands would become marshes, then saline wasteland — this is not speculation but "repeatedly confirmed natural law."

"The tragedy of great beauty: people always believe it can be described, so they let it drown in a sea of words."

Yu Qiuyu abandons describing Hangzhou's beauty, asking only how it was created and protected. This is the same Hangzhou that Columbus sought when he placed Marco Polo's Travels on his helm and set sail — Hangzhou once quietly propelled the world.

2
Bai Juyi's Hydraulics

Why did a literary titan become a water engineer?

Past fifty, Bai Juyi — already the author of "Song of Everlasting Sorrow" and "The Song of the Pipa," indisputably one of the greatest figures in Chinese literary history — displayed no cultural arrogance. Instead, he busied himself directing lake works: deepening the lakebed, building raised embankments, regulating water release, dredging the "Six Wells" — the bearing of a hydrologist and urban planner.

In the 820s as Hangzhou governor, Bai Juyi found West Lake choked with reeds, its water capacity greatly diminished while farmland thirsted for irrigation. He studied the relationship between storage and release, writing poetry with mud and stone while hiding his own verses among the water grasses to chuckle in silence.

3
Qian Liu's City-Building

How did the Wuyue king forge Hangzhou through peace?

Qian Liu, one of China's rare urban design masters, deployed "lake-dredging troops" with military discipline to clear West Lake, dispatched over seven thousand men to Lake Tai, built sea-defending dykes with bamboo-caged boulders, and earned the title "Sea Dragon King."

Even more profound was his political wisdom. As ruler of the Wuyue kingdom, he adopted a strategy of "serving the greater power" — deliberately making himself small and others large, with "protecting the realm and pacifying the people" as his guiding principle, which he wrote into his will. His grandson Qian Chu peacefully incorporated Wuyue into the Song Empire — "a small kingdom's wisdom that also preserved a great nation's integrity."

🏗️ Water Mastery

Built sea dykes to pacify tides; deployed troops to clear West Lake, Lake Tai, South Lake, Jian Lake; constructed inner and outer city walls with comprehensive urban planning.

🕊️ Political Wisdom

Ignored historical glory and moral exemplar status; focused solely on public welfare, regional peace, tides, and water clarity. Hangzhou grew peaceful and prosperous — eventually chosen as the Southern Song capital.

4
Su Dongpo's Rescue

Why would Su Dongpo rather not be a poet than let West Lake die?

Su Dongpo served Hangzhou twice and refused to organize poetry societies or literary prizes — he saw West Lake's mortal crisis: one-third choked on his first posting, half gone by his second; in twenty years, the lake would vanish entirely, and with it, Hangzhou itself.

The chain of consequences was clear and terrifying: lake death → canal imbalance → salt tide backflow → silt-choked canals → useless wells → fleeing citizens → ruined city → dead farmland → collapsed industries → vanished tax base. Su Dongpo petitioned the court, raised funds, and simultaneously launched six major works: returning farmland to lake, deepening the bed, building the Su Causeway with six bridges, constructing sluice gates, dredging the canal, and replacing bamboo water pipes with stone-tile conduits.

"A poet's duty is to describe a beauty's dying pallor. Su Dongpo wanted to save her. So he chose to be a real man rather than a poet."
5
Millennial Declaration

What is Hangzhou's thousand-year humanist manifesto?

Hangzhou presents a millennial humanist manifesto — drafted by Qian Liu, co-authored by Bai Juyi and Su Dongpo, polished by Lady White Snake, and continued by today's builders. Its content is simple: about nature, about ecology, about beauty, about humanity.

The manifesto's essence: ecology precedes culture. Whatever culture enters Hangzhou immediately becomes landscaped, ecological existence — the White Snake legend receives a lake, a bridge, and a pagoda as its stage. This story itself is an interrogation of Chinese history: too many demons, too many immortals, too few humans. Lady White Snake stands at the boundary asking what it means to be human.

Walking alone on the Su Causeway at dusk — no inscribed tablets, no couplets, no plaques, no textual pollution, no aggression of text-culture against natural ecology. This was Su Dongpo's design: a long causeway where people could escape everything and walk a pilgrimage to nature.

💡
Key Takeaway

Hangzhou's beauty is entirely the product of human creation and stewardship — ecology precedes culture. Even the greatest poets set aside verse upon arriving to become ecological rescuers. This thousand-year manifesto tells us: before natural ecology, all cultured people are as humble and insignificant as any villager.
