Cultural Bitter Journey · Ch.17 "Fish Tail Lodge"

Examining human civilization at the foot of the Himalayas—why, of all ancient civilizations, has only the Chinese survived uninterrupted?

CORE PROPOSITION

Of all ancient civilizations, only Chinese civilization has survived uninterrupted—this is not a boast but a puzzle demanding an answer. After examining every major ancient civilization site—Egypt, Babylon, Crete, Hebrew, Persia, India, Arabia—the author identifies five factors behind Chinese continuity: geographic barriers, civilizational scale, unified writing, pragmatic rationality and the Golden Mean, and the examination system. But the new century's direction is shedding burdens and returning to nature.

01
Discovery

Collective Aging: All Ancient Civilizations Have Declined

Without exception, every cradle of ancient civilization has declined—the older, the deeper the scars.

Crete's Minoan civilization possessed 'modern' amenities—advanced drainage and bathroom systems—yet perished millennia ago. Egypt's pyramids hide profound mysteries behind simple forms, but their civilizational foundation vanished into the Sahara. Babylon, Persia, India—one after another, interrupted or extinguished.

“
If we can step right into them across time, then destruction can step right into us. No civilization can fantasize about immortality, but it can choose its bearing in its final days.
—— Yu Qiuyu

The Middle East, crowded with humanity's most brilliant ancient civilizations, is now exhausted by extremist conflicts. The zone of greatest civilization became the zone of greatest hatred.

02
Geography

Natural Barriers: The Himalayan Gift

Chinese civilization's first protective factor: naturally enclosed geography that avoided inter-civilizational mutual destruction.

The Himalayas, Kunlun, Tianshan, and Altai mountains connect to impassable deserts; vast seas lie east and south. During China's Shang dynasty, Egypt was campaigning in West Asia; during Confucius's time, Persia conquered Babylon and Egypt—only China was absent from this chaos.

🔑
Key Difference: Wars between civilizations were both 'homicide' and 'suicide.' China's internal wars merely contested civilizational dominance—no one abolished Chinese characters or destroyed the classics. Invading ethnic groups lacked sufficient civilizational weight and were eventually absorbed.

Chinese civilization's geographic scale—the Yellow and Yangtze basins combined—exceeds the sum of all other ancient civilizations' territory, preventing stagnation within its enclosure.

03
Wisdom

Pragmatic Reason and Exams: Internal Immunity

Pragmatic rationality prevented religious extremism; the imperial examination system ensured social management and cultural transmission advanced together.

Chinese civilization lacks grand, exclusive transcendental spirit—a loss for philosophy and art, but not necessarily bad. Chinese sages' theories are never mystical; even major religions were humanized. The examination system continuously selected talent for over a millennium—'Do not do to others what you would not want done to yourself' has been transmitted for over two millennia.

✅ Pragmatic Rationality

Balanced, moderate, universal. Confucianism and Daoism complement rather than exclude. Never captured by mysticism.

⚠️ Religious Extremism

In the Middle East, collective extremism blended with religion and history turned conflict into unshakeable faith. All sides reject reason—this is the deep root of civilizational mutual destruction.

China had periods of extremist mob violence, but they were brief and never formed complete religious extremism.

04
Direction

Returning to Nature: The New Century's Path

Chinese civilization's uninterrupted millennia have also brought crushing burdens—shedding them and returning to nature is the new century's direction.

Nepal has no independent civilization, yet brought travelers the most joy—its nature is far older than any human civilization. After millennia of struggle, what humans love most is not their own creations. Crowded cities can be more desolate; dense crowds more barbaric.

“
Even when pearls and treasures weigh you down until you can barely breathe, you should be willing to cast them aside—when human strength can no longer bear them, they have become inhuman.
—— Yu Qiuyu

Confucius advocated 'temperate joy, in harmony with heaven'; Mozi opposed wasteful consumption; Laozi and Zhuangzi smiled silently—'If you want to speak of the Way, the only Way is nature.' At its highest level, Chinese culture is a culture of subtraction.

💡
KEY TAKEAWAY

Chinese civilization is the only ancient civilization that never broke—behind this fact lies geographic fortune (mountains and deserts as natural barriers) and conscious wisdom (pragmatic rationality, the Golden Mean, the examination system). But continuity itself has also brought crushing historical burdens. At the foot of the Himalayas, the author found another dimension of the answer: civilization's meaning lies not in how much it accumulates, but in whether it can return to nature. Chinese culture's deepest code is subtraction.
